## Key Reference Artifacts

### $100M Launch Phases

#### Phase 1: Pre-Launch (4-6 weeks before)
- Build anticipation (teasers, waitlist)
- Content seeding (value, authority)
- Social proof collection (testimonials)
- Partner outreach (affiliates, collaborators)

#### Phase 2: Warm-Up (1-2 weeks before)
- Increase content frequency
- Behind-the-scenes access
- Q&A sessions
- Objection handling content
- Early bird announcements

#### Phase 3: Cart Open (Launch Week)
- Day 1: Cart opens, early bird
- Day 2-3: Value + social proof
- Day 4-5: Objections + case studies
- Day 6-7: Scarcity + urgency
- Final hours: Last chance

#### Phase 4: Cart Close
- Clear deadline (no extensions!)
- Final urgency push
- FOMO amplification
- "Last call" messaging

#### Phase 5: Post-Launch
- Deliver exceptional results
- Collect testimonials
- Case study development
- Segment non-buyers for future

### Launch Success Metrics
- **Pre-launch**: Waitlist signups, engagement rate
- **During launch**: Registrations, attendees, conversion rate
- **Revenue**: Total sales, AOV, refund rate
- **Post-launch**: Customer success rate, NPS, LTV

### Hormozi's Launch Principles
1. **Build Anticipation** - Don't just drop, tease first
2. **Create Scarcity** - Limited spots or time
3. **Social Proof** - Show others buying/succeeding
4. **Value First** - Give before you ask
5. **Clear Deadline** - Cart closes, no extensions
6. **Maximize Revenue** - Premium pricing, upsells, payment plans
7. **Focus on Buyers** - Serve customers, not prospects

### Common Launch Mistakes
- No pre-launch warm-up
- Weak or fake scarcity
- Cart extensions (kills urgency)
- Ignoring non-buyers
- Poor post-launch follow-through
- Not collecting enough social proof
- Weak offer presentation
- No clear deadline
