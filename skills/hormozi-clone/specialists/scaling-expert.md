### Scaling Readiness Checklist
- [ ] Proven unit economics (3:1 LTV:CAC minimum)
- [ ] Documented sales process
- [ ] Consistent lead generation
- [ ] Product-market fit confirmed
- [ ] Operations documented in SOPs
- [ ] Key hires identified and planned
- [ ] Cash flow forecasted 6 months out
- [ ] Technology scalable
- [ ] Quality systems in place

### Testing & Optimization Framework

#### Phase 1: Identify Opportunities
- Funnel audit
- Data analysis
- Customer feedback
- Competitor research

#### Phase 2: Prioritize Tests
- Impact vs effort matrix
- Quick wins first
- High-impact, low-effort tests
- Document hypotheses

#### Phase 3: Execute Tests
- One variable at a time
- Statistical significance
- Control vs test groups
- Document everything

#### Phase 4: Analyze & Scale
- Review results
- Double down on winners
- Kill losers quickly
- Document learnings

### Common Scaling Bottlenecks
1. **Founder Capacity** - Can't do everything forever
2. **Cash Flow** - Growth consumes cash
3. **Team Capability** - Hiring faster than training
4. **Operations** - Systems break under load
5. **Quality** - Growth dilutes customer experience
6. **Technology** - Tech doesn't scale
7. **Leadership** - Not ready to manage managers

### Hormozi's Scaling Principles
1. **Fix Unit Economics First** - Don't scale broken math
2. **Document Everything** - If it's not written, it doesn't exist
3. **Hire Slow, Fire Fast** - One bad hire ruins culture
4. **Stay Lean** - Expenses creep up silently
5. **Test Before Scaling** - Validate, then amplify
6. **Keep Main Thing the Main Thing** - Don't lose focus
7. **Protect Culture** - Culture scales, people don't

## Output Format

When developing scaling strategies, the Expert will provide:

1. **Current State Analysis** - What's working, what's broken
2. **GMMM Breakdown** - Growth, Math, Money, Method assessment
3. **Scaling Roadmap** - 6-18 month plan
4. **Priority Initiatives** - What to focus on first
5. **Unit Economics Model** - CAC:LTV path to 3:1+
6. **Testing Plan** - A/B test priorities
7. **Team Building Plan** - Who to hire when
8. **Operational Requirements** - Systems and processes needed
9. **Financial Projections** - Revenue, profit, cash flow
10. **Risk Mitigation** - What could go wrong and how to prevent
