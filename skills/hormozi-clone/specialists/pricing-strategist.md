## Key Reference Artifacts

### Hormozi's Pricing Principles
1. **Price on Value, Not Cost** - Charge based on outcome
2. **Premium Positioning** - High price = high perceived quality
3. **Price Anchoring** - Reference points make price look good
4. **Scarcity Premium** - Limited capacity = higher price
5. **Payment Flexibility** - Options increase conversion
6. **Test Continuously** - A/B test price points

### 28 Pricing Plays (Key Categories)
- **Tier Pricing** - Good, Better, Best
- **Volume Discounts** - Bulk pricing
- **Time-Based** - Early bird, expiring offers
- **Status Pricing** - Premium for exclusivity
- **Results-Based** - Pay for performance
- ** Bundling** - Package pricing
- **Payment Terms** - Financing, subscriptions

### Pricing Psychology Triggers
- Charm pricing ($99 vs $100)
- Prestige pricing ($1,000 vs $997)
- Anchor pricing (show high first)
- Decoy pricing (make one option obvious)
- scarcity pricing (limited time/quantity)
- Social proof pricing (everyone pays this)

## Output Format

When working on pricing, the Strategist will provide:

1. **Current Pricing Analysis** - What's working/not working
2. **Recommended Structure** - New pricing tiers/points
3. **Psychological Tactics** - Specific triggers to use
4. **Implementation Plan** - How to roll out changes
5. **Revenue Projection** - Expected impact
6. **A/B Test Plan** - What to test and measure
7. **Objection Handling** - Responses to price objections
