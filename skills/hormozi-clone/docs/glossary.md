## A-C

### A/B Testing
**Definition:** Splitting traffic between two versions to see which performs better.

**Hormozi's Approach:** Test 10× more variations than competitors. Let data decide winners and losers. Never assume - always test.

**Example:** Test Price A ($100) vs Price B ($200) with equal traffic. See which generates more total revenue.

---

### Activation Rate
**Definition:** Percentage of users who experience first value moment.

**Why It Matters:** Activated users churn 5× less than non-activated users.

**Example:** For fitness app - completing first workout. For SaaS - using first key feature.

**Benchmark:**
- Poor: <20%
- Great: >60%

---

### Ad Assembly
**Definition:** Creating ads by combining three components: Hook + Meat + CTA.

**Formula:**
- Hook (5 seconds) - Stops the scroll
- Meat (15-60 seconds) - Delivers value/persuasion
- CTA (5 seconds) - Drives action

**Key Insight:** Ad creation is assembly, not artistry. Use templates, test systematically.

---

### Ad Spend ROI
**Definition:** Return on investment from advertising over lifetime.

**Hormozi's Benchmark:** 36:1 lifetime ROI on ad spend across all businesses.

**Calculation:** Total lifetime customer value ÷ Total ad spend

---

### Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)
**Definition:** Predictable revenue expected annually from subscriptions.

**Formula:** MRR × 12

**Why It Matters:** Recurring revenue increases business valuation and stability.

---

### Ascension Offer
**Definition:** Premium version of core offer for VIP customers.

**Purpose:** Maximize customer value and serve high-end clients better.

**Example:** Core program ($997) → Premium version ($9,997) with personal coaching.

---

### Attractions Offer
**Definition:** Free or low-cost offer to generate leads.

**Purpose:** Get prospects into funnel, demonstrate value, build trust.

**Examples:** Free trial, free consultation, free PDF, free challenge.

---

### Average Order Value (AOV)
**Definition:** Average amount customer spends per transaction.

**Formula:** Total Revenue ÷ Number of Orders

**How to Increase:**
- Order bumps (+10-30%)
- Upsells (+20-50%)
- Cross-sells (+15-40%)
- Bundling (+25-50%)

---

### BAMFAM
**Definition:** Book A Meeting From A Meeting.

**Concept:** Every meeting should end with next meeting booked.

**Why It Matters:** Maintains momentum, builds relationship, accelerates sales cycle.

---

### Benchmark
**Definition:** Standard or reference point for comparison.

**Usage:** Compare your metrics to industry benchmarks to identify areas for improvement.

**Example:** If conversion rate is 2% (below benchmark of 5%), optimize conversion.

---

### Bonuses
**Definition:** Additional value items added to offer to increase perceived value.

**Strategy:** Stack 5-10 bonuses to create 10× value-to-price ratio.

**Best Types:** High value/low cost items (digital downloads, templates, access).

---

### Breakeven Analysis
**Definition:** Calculating how many sales you can lose and still make more money at higher price.

**Example:**
- Current: 100 × $100 = $10,000
- New: 50 × $250 = $12,500
- Result: Lose 50% of customers, make 25% more money

---

### Burn Rate
**Definition:** Rate at which company spends cash reserves.

**Formula:** Monthly expenses - Monthly revenue

**Benchmark:**
- <3 months runway: Critical
- 12-24 months runway: Healthy

---

### Call Out
**Definition:** First part of hook that identifies target audience.

**Examples:**
- "You're struggling to get clients"
- "If you're a business owner..."
- "Attention: [specific profession]"

**Formula:** Call Out + Condition for Value = Hook

---

### Cancellation Calls
**Definition:** Calling customers immediately when they request cancellation.

**Success Rate:** 30-50% save rate.

**Purpose:** Learn why they leave, fix systemic issues, sometimes win them back.

---

### Categorization (The Trap)
**Definition:** Making your offer comparable to competitors.

**Problem:** If they can compare you, you commoditized yourself.

**Solution:** Create category of one with unique mechanism, delivery, and guarantee.

---

### Churn Rate
**Definition:** Percentage of customers who stop paying in given period.

**Formula:** Customers Lost ÷ Total Customers

**Monthly Benchmark:**
- Poor: >15%
- Good: 5-10%
- Great: <5%

**The Mathematics:** 10% churn = 10-month lifespan. 3% churn = 33-month lifespan (3.3× longer).

---

### Click-Through Rate (CTR)
**Definition:** Percentage of people who click after seeing ad/content.

**Formula:** Clicks ÷ Impressions × 100

**Ad Benchmark:**
- Poor: <1%
- Good: 2-5%
- Great: >5%

---

### Cold Outreach
**Definition:** Contacting prospects who have no prior relationship.

**Methods:** Cold email, cold calling, cold DMs.

**Pros:** Scalable, targeted, immediate results.
**Cons:** Low conversion (1-5%), legal restrictions.

---

### Condition for Value
**Definition:** Second part of hook that promises value.

**Examples:**
- "...this will fix it"
- "...watch this video"
- "...here's the framework"

**Formula:** Call Out + Condition for Value = Hook

---

### Content Marketing
**Definition:** Creating free content to attract leads organically.

**Platforms:** YouTube, SEO, social media, podcasts, blogs.

**Pros:** Builds brand, compounds over time, high trust.
**Cons:** Slow to start, inconsistent results initially.

---

### Conversion Rate
**Definition:** Percentage of prospects who take desired action.

**Formula:** Conversions ÷ Visitors × 100

**Benchmark:**
- Poor: <2%
- Good: 5-10%
- Great: >10%

---

### Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)
**Definition:** Systematic process of increasing conversion percentage.

**Priority:** Fix conversion BEFORE scaling traffic. Don't scale broken funnel.

---

### Core Four Lead Generation
**Definition:** The ONLY four ways to generate leads (everything else is variation):

1. **Warm Outreach** - DMs, referrals, network
2. **Free Content** - YouTube, social, SEO
3. **Cold Outreach** - Email, calls, DMs
4. **Paid Advertising** - Facebook, Google, TikTok

---

### Cost Per Click (CPC)
**Definition:** Amount paid for each click on advertisement.

**Benchmark:** $0.50-8.00 depending on platform and industry.

---

### Cost Per Lead (CPL)
**Definition:** Amount spent to acquire one lead.

**Formula:** Total Ad Spend ÷ Number of Leads

**Benchmark:** $10-500 depending on offer type.

---

### Cost Plus Pricing
**Definition:** Pricing based on costs plus markup.

**Problem:** Doesn't consider value to customer. Leaves money on table.

**Better:** Value-based pricing.

---

### Crazy 8 Framework
**Definition:** Eight ways to increase customer lifetime value:

1. Raise prices
2. Add upsells
3. Add downsells
4. Add cross-sells
5. Add continuity
6. Increase frequency
7. Add referrals
8. Reduce churn

---

### Cross-Sell
**Definition:** Selling related products to existing customers.

**Example:** Selling supplements to fitness coaching clients.

**Impact:** +15-40% increase in average order value.

---

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
**Definition:** Total cost to acquire one customer.

**Formula:** (Marketing + Sales Costs) ÷ New Customers

**Benchmark:** Varies by offer type ($50-5,000).

**Target:** LTV:CAC ratio of 3:1 minimum.

---

### Customer Journey Mapping
**Definition:** Documenting every touchpoint customer has with business.

**Purpose:** Identify friction points, improve experience, reduce churn.

---

### Customer Lifetime Value (LTV or CLV)
**Definition:** Total revenue expected from single customer account.

**Formula:** Average Revenue Per Customer ÷ Churn Rate

**Why It Matters:** Most important metric. Determines how much you can spend on acquisition.

---

## D-F

### DAU/MAU Ratio
**Definition:** Daily Active Users ÷ Monthly Active Users.

**Benchmark:**
- Poor: <10%
- Great: >40%

**What It Measures:** Engagement and product stickiness.

---

### Done-For-You (DFY)
**Definition:** Service where you do everything for customer.

**Pricing:** Highest ($10K-100K+).

**Example:** "We run your ads for you."

---

### Done-With-You (DWY)
**Definition:** Service where you do it together with customer.

**Pricing:** Medium ($1K-10K).

**Example:** Group coaching, workshops.

---

### Do-It-Yourself (DIY)
**Definition:** Customer does work themselves using your materials.

**Pricing:** Lowest ($97-997).

**Example:** Online course, book, templates.

---

### Downsell
**Definition:** Offer presented when customer declines main offer.

**Purpose:** Capture lost sales, maintain relationship.

**Example:** If they decline $2,997 program, offer $997 lite version.

---

### Dream Outcome
**Definition:** Specific result customer wants to achieve.

**Key:** Be specific, not vague.

**Example:**
- ❌ "Get fit"
- ✅ "Lose 30lbs in 30 days"

**Part of Value Equation:** More specific = more valuable = higher price.

---

### Drop-Off Point
**Definition:** Stage in funnel where prospects leave.

**Strategy:** Fix biggest drop-off first, then next biggest. Test one variable at a time.

---

### Email List
**Definition:** Database of email addresses you can market to.

**Value:** Owned asset you control (unlike social media followers).

**Benchmark:**
- Cold list: 10-20% open rate
- Warm lead list: 20-35% open rate
- Customer list: 30-50% open rate

---

### Engagement Rate
**Definition:** Percentage of audience that interacts with content.

**Formula:** (Likes + Comments + Shares) ÷ Impressions × 100

**Social Media Benchmark:**
- Poor: <1%
- Good: 3-6%
- Great: >6%

---

### Escalation Ladder (Guarantees)
**Definition:** Six levels of guarantees from weak to extreme:

1. No guarantee (weak)
2. Standard money-back (expected)
3. Extended guarantee (better)
4. Conditional guarantee
5. Reverse risk ("We pay YOU")
6. Extreme ("We don't get paid until you succeed")

---

### Exit Interview
**Definition:** Conversation with customer when they cancel.

**Purpose:** Learn why they leave, fix systemic issues, sometimes win them back (30-50% save rate).

---

### Fire Bad Customers
**Definition:** Proactively ending relationship with problematic customers.

**Who to Fire:**
- Complainers who never implement
- Always want refunds/discounts
- Toxic to community
- Drain team energy
- Don't do the work

**Why:** One bad customer destroys team morale and costs more than they're worth.

---

### Five Horsemen of Retention
**Definition:** Hormozi's five retention strategies that reduce churn 50%+:

1. Track Attendance Religiously
2. Reach Out 2× Per Week Minimum
3. Send Handwritten Cards
4. Host Member Events
5. Exit Interviews When They Cancel

---

### Funnel
**Definition:** Journey prospect takes from first touch to purchase.

**Typical Stages:** Awareness → Interest → Consideration → Conversion → Retention → Advocacy

---

## G-I

### Grand Slam Offer
**Definition:** Offer so good people feel stupid saying no.

**Three Characteristics:**
1. Solve ALL problems (not just one)
2. Be incomparable (category of one)
3. Create massive value discrepancy (worth 10× price)

---

### Gross Margin
**Definition:** Revenue minus cost of goods sold, expressed as percentage.

**Formula:** (Revenue - COGS) ÷ Revenue × 100

**Benchmark:**
- Digital products: 70-95%
- Services: 30-60%

---

### Guarantee
**Definition:** Promise that removes risk from customer.

**Types:** Money-back, satisfaction guaranteed, conditional, reverse risk, extreme.

**Principle:** Make guarantee so good they'd feel stupid not to try.

---

### Hook
**Definition:** First 5 seconds of content/ad that stops the scroll.

**Formula:** Call Out + Condition for Value

**Archetypes:**
- Problem-Focused
- Benefit-Focused
- Story-Focused
- Contrarian
- Numbers-Based

**80/20 Rule:** Spend 80% of time on hook. If they don't stop, nothing else matters.

---

### Hook Rate
**Definition:** Percentage of people who watch past first 3 seconds of video.

**Benchmark:**
- Poor: <20%
- Great: >60%

---

### Hyper-Specificity
**Definition:** Being extremely specific about outcomes and claims.

**Principle:** Specificity = credibility = value = higher price.

**Example:**
- ❌ "Make more money"
- ✅ "Make $10,347 in your first 60 days"

---

### Lead
**Definition:** Potential customer who has expressed interest.

**Types:**
- Cold lead: No prior relationship
- Warm lead: Some interaction/interest
- Hot lead: Ready to buy now

---

### Lead Magnet
**Definition:** Free offer to capture contact information.

**Three Types:**
1. Info products (PDFs, guides)
2. Tools (calculators, trials)
3. Samples (consultations, products)

---

### Lead-to-Customer Rate
**Definition:** Percentage of leads who become customers.

**Formula:** Customers ÷ Leads × 100

**Benchmark:**
- Poor: <5%
- Good: 10-20%
- Great: >20%

---

### Lifetime Value (LTV)
**Definition:** See Customer Lifetime Value.

---

### LTV:CAC Ratio
**Definition:** Lifetime value divided by acquisition cost.

**Formula:** LTV ÷ CAC

**Benchmark:**
- Break even: 1:1
- Healthy: 3:1
- Excellent: 5:1+

**Target:** Minimum 3:1 to sustain growth.

---

## J-L

### Journey Mapping
**Definition:** See Customer Journey Mapping.

---

### Keyword Research
**Definition:** Identifying search terms target audience uses.

**Purpose:** Optimize content for SEO, understand customer language.

---

### Landing Page
**Definition:** Single web page designed for specific marketing campaign.

**Key Elements:** Headline, subheadline, value stack, social proof, offer, CTA, guarantee.

**Benchmark:** 20-40% conversion from warm traffic.

---

### Launch
**Definition:** Coordinated effort to introduce new product/promotion.

**Hormozi's 8-Phase Launch:**
1. Pre-Launch (7-14 days): Tease, build waitlist
2. Problem (Day 1): Agitate pain
3. Solution (Day 2): Introduce offer
4. Proof (Day 3): Case studies
5. Framework (Day 4): Teach method
6. Offer (Day 5): Stack value, show price
7. Urgency (Day 6): Scarcity, deadline
8. Close (Day 7): Final chance, doors close

---

### Lead Nurturing
**Definition:** Building relationship with leads over time before asking for sale.

**7-Day Sequence Example:**
- Day 1: Welcome + Quick Win
- Day 2: Story + Vulnerability
- Day 3: Framework + Value
- Day 4: Case Study + Proof
- Day 5: Objection Handling
- Day 6: Testimonial Stack
- Day 7: Offer + Urgency

---

### Lookalike Audience
**Definition:** Target audience similar to your existing customers.

**Platform:** Facebook, Google, etc. create audiences based on your customer data.

**Purpose:** Scale winning ads to similar prospects.

## M-O

### M-A-G-I-C Framework
**Definition:** Framework for naming offers:

- **M** = Magnet (Unique Mechanism)
- **A** = Avatar (Target Audience)
- **G** = Goal (Dream Outcome)
- **I** = Interval (Timeframe)
- **C** = Container (Delivery Format)

**Example:** "The 30-Day Shred For Busy Professionals"

---

### Marketing Math
**Definition:** Using numbers and formulas to make marketing decisions.

**Examples:**
- Breakeven analysis
- LTV calculation
- ROAS tracking
- Conversion optimization

**Principle:** Let data drive decisions, not opinions.

---

### Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)
**Definition:** Predictable revenue expected monthly from subscriptions.

**Why It Matters:** Increases business valuation and stability.

**Growth Target:** 5-15% month-over-month for growth-stage businesses.

---

### Net Promoter Score (NPS)
**Definition:** Measure of customer loyalty and satisfaction.

**Question:** "How likely are you to recommend us to a friend? (0-10)"

**Scoring:**
- 9-10: Promoters (your advocates)
- 7-8: Passive (satisfied but not excited)
- 0-6: Detractors (at risk of leaving)

**Formula:** % Promoters - % Detractors

**Benchmark:** >40 is excellent.

---

### Nurture Sequence
**Definition:** Series of emails/content to build relationship before sale.

**See:** Lead Nurturing

---

### Offer
**Definition:** What you sell, how you deliver it, and what it costs.

**Components:** Product, service, bonuses, guarantee, price, scarcity.

**Types:** Attraction, Core, Ascension.

---

### Open Rate (Email)
**Definition:** Percentage of recipients who open email.

**Formula:** Emails Opened ÷ Emails Delivered × 100

**Benchmark:**
- Poor: <15%
- Good: 25-35%
- Great: >35%

---

### Order Bump
**Definition:** One-click add-on offered at checkout.

**Impact:** +10-30% increase in average order value.

**Example:** "Add this to your order for just $27 more."

---

### Organic Traffic
**Definition:** Visitors from unpaid sources (SEO, social, referrals).

**Pros:** Free, compounds over time, high trust.
**Cons:** Slow to build, requires consistency.

---

### Outbound Marketing
**Definition:** Proactively reaching out to prospects.

**Examples:** Cold email, cold calls, warm DMs, networking.

**Opposite of:** Inbound (attracting prospects to you).

---

### Over-Delivering
**Definition:** Exceeding customer expectations consistently.

**Strategy:** Under-promise, over-deliver.

**Result:** Raving fans, referrals, retention.

---

## P-R

### Payment Plan Premium
**Definition:** Charging 20-30% more for payment plans.

**Example:**
- Full price: $1,997 (save $500)
- Payments: $249 × 10 months = $2,490

---

### Perceived Likelihood
**Definition:** How certain customer is they'll achieve desired outcome.

**How to Increase:**
- Testimonials
- Case studies
- Proof
- Credentials
- Guarantee

**Part of Value Equation:** Higher perceived likelihood = higher value = higher price.

---

### Pre-Frustration Strategy
**Definition:** Solving problems before customer experiences them.

**Process:**
1. List every problem/frustration they'll have
2. Create solution for each
3. Include in offer
4. Raise price accordingly

---

### Prestige Pricing
**Definition:** Using round numbers to signal luxury quality.

**Example:** $1,000 instead of $997 for premium offer.

**Opposite of:** Charm pricing ($97 vs $100).

---

### Price Anchoring
**Definition:** Showing high price first to make other prices seem reasonable.

**Example:** Show $10,000 package, then $997 looks cheap.

---

### Price Elasticity
**Definition:** How demand changes with price.

**Testing:** Always test price. Market will tell you if you're overpriced, but never if you're underpriced.

---

### Premium Pricing
**Definition:** Charging 2-10× more than competitors.

**Why Works:** Higher prices = better clients = better results = more testimonials = more sales.

---

### Product-Led Growth
**Definition:** Using product itself to drive acquisition and retention.

**Example:** Free trials, freemium models, viral sharing features.

---

### Proof Elements
**Definition:** Evidence that your offer works.

**Types:**
- Testimonials
- Case studies
- Before/after photos
- Income reports
- Customer count
- Social media engagement
- Expert endorsements

---

### Qualified Lead
**Definition:** Lead who meets criteria for being ideal customer.

**Criteria:** Budget, authority, need, timeline (BANT).

**Better:** Leads who have demonstrated clear interest and fit.

---

### RAISE Framework
**Definition:** Framework for communicating price increases:

- **R**emind them of value delivered
- **A**ddress price directly
- **I**nvest in their future
- **S**often with loyalty reward
- **E**xplain away concerns

---

### Referral
**Definition:** New customer recommended by existing customer.

**Benefits:** Highest conversion (20-30%), lowest acquisition cost, highest lifetime value.

**Target:** 30%+ of new customers from referrals.

---

### Retention Rate
**Definition:** Percentage of customers who continue paying.

**Formula:** ((Customers at End - New) ÷ Customers at Start) × 100

**Annual Benchmark:**
- Poor: <50%
- Good: 70-80%
- Great: >90%

---

### Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)
**Definition:** Revenue generated for every dollar spent on ads.

**Formula:** Revenue ÷ Ad Spend

**Benchmark:**
- Break even: 1×
- Good: 3-5×
- Great: >10×

**Hormozi's Benchmark:** 36:1 lifetime ROAS.

---

### Return on Investment (ROI)
**Definition:** Profit relative to investment cost.

**Formula:** (Return - Investment) ÷ Investment × 100

**Example:** Invest $1,000, make $3,000 = 200% ROI.

---

### Revenue Churn
**Definition:** Revenue lost from customer cancellations/downgrades.

**Formula:** MRR Lost ÷ MRR at Start × 100

**Why It Matters:** More important than customer count churn for revenue forecasting.

---

### Risk Reversal
**Definition:** Removing risk from customer through guarantees.

**Principle:** The more risk you remove, the more sales you make.

**See:** Guarantee

---

## S-U

### Scalability
**Definition:** Ability to grow without proportional increase in costs.

**Characteristics:**
- Digital products (highly scalable)
- Services (less scalable without systems)
- Software (highly scalable)

---

### Scarcity
**Definition:** Limited amount of something.

**Types:**
- Limited spots
- Limited bonuses
- Limited time offers

**Rule:** Only use if REAL. Don't fake it.

---

### Segmentation
**Definition:** Dividing audience into subgroups based on characteristics.

**Examples:** Demographics, behavior, purchase history, engagement level.

**Purpose:** Deliver more relevant messaging and offers.

---

### Social Proof
**Definition:** Evidence that others have used and benefited from your offer.

**Types:** Testimonials, reviews, case studies, social media engagement, expert endorsements.

---

### Split Testing
**Definition:** See A/B Testing.

---

### Subscription
**Definition:** Recurring payment model for ongoing access.

**Benefits:** Predictable revenue, higher LTV, easier upsells.

**Challenges:** Churn management, continuous value delivery.

---

### Testimonial
**Definition:** Customer statement about their experience and results.

**Best Practices:**
- Specific results (numbers)
- Before/after
- Video > audio > text
- Include customer details (name, photo, location)

---

### Tiered Pricing
**Definition:** Offering multiple price points with different features.

**Example:**
- Basic: $997
- Premium: $2,997
- VIP: $9,997

**Strategy:** Make premium option obvious value at highest price, then make middle option seem reasonable.

---

### Time Delay
**Definition:** How long it takes to achieve result.

**Part of Value Equation:** Shorter time = higher value = higher price.

**Example:**
- ❌ "Get in shape in 6 months"
- ✅ "Lose 30lbs in 30 days"

---

### Traffic
**Definition:** Visitors to your website or content.

**Quality Hierarchy:**
1. Warm Referrals (best quality, lowest quantity)
2. Warm Outreach
3. Cold Outreach
4. Content Marketing
5. Paid Ads (variable quality, unlimited quantity)

---

### Traffic Hierarchy
**Definition:** See Traffic.

---

### Trim & Stack Matrix
**Definition:** Framework for deciding what to include in offer.

```
               | HIGH VALUE    | LOW VALUE
               | to Customer   | to Customer
─────────────────────────────────────────────
HIGH COST      | KEEP          | ELIMINATE
to You         | (Premium)     |
─────────────────────────────────────────────
LOW COST       | KEEP          | ELIMINATE
to You         | (GOLD)        |
```

**Key Insight:** High value/low cost items are GOLD. Include as bonuses.

---

### Unique Mechanism
**Definition:** Proprietary name for your process/method.

**Purpose:** Create category of one, can't be compared to competitors.

**Examples:**
- "The Hypertrophy Matrix"
- "The 6-Figure Email System"
- "The 30-Day Fat Loss Protocol"

---

### Upsell
**Definition:** Offer to purchase more expensive or upgraded version.

**Impact:** +20-50% increase in average order value.

**Example:** "Would you like to upgrade to premium version for just $500 more?"

---

### Urgency
**Definition:** Limited time to take action.

**Types:**
- Price increase (ends Friday)
- Bonus disappearing (gone at midnight)
- Doors closing (program starts Monday)

**Rule:** Only use if REAL. Don't fake it.

---

### User Experience (UX)
**Definition:** Overall experience customer has with your business/product.

**Impact on Metrics:** Good UX increases conversion, retention, referrals.

---

## V-Z

### Value Equation
**Definition:** The mathematical formula for value:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood)
        ──────────────────────────────────
                (Time Delay × Effort)
```

**How to Use:**
- Make numerator BIG: Specific outcome, high belief
- Make denominator SMALL: Fast results, easy implementation

**Most Important Concept:** Master this, master pricing.

---

### Value Ladder
**Definition:** Series of increasingly expensive offers.

**Example:**
- Free lead magnet
- $97 tripwire
- $997 core offer
- $2,997 premium
- $9,997 VIP

---

### Value Stacking
**Definition:** Listing all components of offer with individual values.

**Purpose:** Create massive value discrepancy (10× price).

**Example:**
- Core: $2,000
- Bonus 1: $500
- Bonus 2: $300
- Total Value: $2,800
- Your Price: $997

---

### Value-to-Price Ratio
**Definition:** Perceived value divided by actual price.

**Target:** 10× minimum.

**Example:** $10,000 value ÷ $997 price = 10× value-to-price ratio.

---

### Viral Coefficient
**Definition:** Number of new customers each customer brings.

**Formula:** If each customer brings 0.5 friends, viral coefficient is 0.5.

**Target:** >1.0 means business grows virally (each customer brings >1 new customer).

---

### Warm Outreach
**Definition:** Contacting people with some prior connection or relationship.

**Methods:** DMs, referrals, network connections.

**Pros:** Zero cost, high conversion (10-20%), perfect for starting.
**Cons:** Hard to scale, time-intensive.

---

### Webinar
**Definition:** Live or automated online presentation.

**Structure:**
1. Intro (5 min)
2. Problem (10 min)
3. Solution (20 min)
4. Proof (10 min)
5. Offer (10 min)
6. Q&A (15 min)
7. Close (5 min)

**Conversion Benchmark:** 5-15% from registrants to buyers.

---

### Welcome Sequence
**Definition:** Series of emails new subscribers receive immediately.

**Purpose:** Deliver quick wins, build relationship, establish trust.

**See:** 7-Day Nurture Sequence

---

### Zero-Click Content
**Definition:** Content that delivers value without requiring click.

**Platforms:** Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram (carousel), TikTok.

**Benefit:** Builds trust and authority, doesn't rely on algorithm for clicks.
