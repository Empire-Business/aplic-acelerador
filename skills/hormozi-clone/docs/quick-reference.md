## CORE FORMULAS

### The Value Equation (Most Important)
```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood)
        ──────────────────────────────────
                (Time Delay × Effort)
```

**How to Use:**
- Make numerator BIG: Specific outcome, high belief
- Make denominator SMALL: Fast results, easy implementation

---

### The Grand Slam Offer Formula
```
Grand Slam Offer = Solve ALL Problems + Be Incomparable + Create Massive Value Discrepancy
```

---

### The LTV Formula
```
LTV = Average Revenue Per Customer ÷ Churn Rate

Example: $100/month ÷ 5% churn = $2,000 LTV
```

---

### The 70-20-10 Rule
```
70% Budget → Proven winners (do more of what works)
20% Budget → Winner adjacent (model others' successes)
10% Budget → Radical experiments (moonshots)
```

## ALL 50+ FRAMEWORKS

### OFFER CREATION FRAMEWORKS

#### 1. Value Equation Components
- **Dream Outcome:** Be specific ("Lose 30lbs" not "get fit")
- **Perceived Likelihood:** Testimonials, proof, guarantees
- **Time Delay:** Deliver faster ("30 days" not "6 months")
- **Effort & Sacrifice:** Done-for-you > Done-with-you > DIY

#### 2. Trim & Stack Matrix
```
               | HIGH VALUE    | LOW VALUE
               | to Customer   | to Customer
─────────────────────────────────────────────
HIGH COST      | KEEP          | ELIMINATE
to You         | (Premium)     | (Profit killer)
─────────────────────────────────────────────
LOW COST       | KEEP          | ELIMINATE
to You         | (GOLD)        | (Distraction)
```

**Key Insight:** High value/low cost items are GOLD. Include as bonuses.

#### 3. M-A-G-I-C Naming Framework
- **M** = Magnet (Unique Mechanism)
- **A** = Avatar (Target Audience)
- **G** = Goal (Dream Outcome)
- **I** = Interval (Timeframe)
- **C** = Container (Delivery Format)

Example: "The 30-Day Shred For Busy Professionals"

#### 4. The Three Offer Types
- **Attraction Offer:** Free or low-cost to get leads
- **Core Offer:** Main product/service
- **Ascension Offer:** Premium version for VIPs

#### 5. Bonus Stacking Strategy
Stack 5-10 bonuses to create 10x value perception:
- Core program: $2,000
- Bonus 1: $500
- Bonus 2: $300
- Bonus 3: $500
- Total Value: $3,300
- Your Price: $997
- **Value-to-Price: 3.3x**

### MARKETING FRAMEWORKS

#### 11. Hooks Formula
```
Call Out + Condition for Value = Hook

Example: "You're struggling to get clients + this will fix it"
```

#### 12. Hook Archetypes
- **Problem-Focused:** "Stop struggling with [problem]"
- **Benefit-Focused:** "How to [result] in [timeframe]"
- **Story-Focused:** "I was [struggle]. Here's what I learned."
- **Contrarian:** "Everything you know about [topic] is wrong"
- **Numbers-Based:** "7 lies about [topic]"

#### 13. Ad Assembly Formula
Every effective ad = Hook (5s) + Meat (15-60s) + CTA (5s)

#### 14. The Five Ad Formats
1. **Demonstration:** Show it working
2. **Testimonial:** Customer results
3. **Education:** Teach something valuable
4. **Story:** Personal journey
5. **Faceless:** Pattern interrupt

#### 15. 80/20 Content Rule
Spend 80% of time on the hook (first 5 seconds).
20% on the rest.

If they don't stop, they can't buy.

#### 16. Goated Ads Framework
- Create 150-750 ads per week
- Test everything
- Document results
- Scale winners
- Kill losers

### LEAD GENERATION FRAMEWORKS

#### 17. Core Four Lead Generation
**ONLY 4 ways to generate leads:**

1. **Warm Outreach** (DMs, referrals, network)
   - Pros: Free, high conversion
   - Cons: Hard to scale
   - Best for: Starting out, high-ticket

2. **Free Content** (YouTube, social, SEO)
   - Pros: Builds brand, compounds
   - Cons: Slow to start
   - Best for: Long-term asset

3. **Cold Outreach** (Email, calls, DMs)
   - Pros: Scalable, targeted
   - Cons: Low conversion, legal issues
   - Best for: B2B, specific niches

4. **Paid Advertising** (Facebook, Google, TikTok)
   - Pros: Unlimited scale, fast
   - Cons: Requires capital
   - Best for: Proven offers, scaling

#### 18. Lead Magnets: The Three Types
1. **Info Products:** PDFs, guides, templates
2. **Tools:** Calculators, assessments, trials
3. **Samples:** Free consultation, sample product

#### 19. Lead Getters Framework
- Free + shipping (physical products)
- Free consultation (services)
- Free trial (software)
- Free challenge (courses)
- Free webinar (education)

### RETENTION FRAMEWORKS

#### 20. The Five Horsemen of Retention
1. **Track Attendance Religiously** - Zero attendance = churn imminent
2. **Reach Out 2× Per Week Minimum** - Presence prevents leaving
3. **Send Handwritten Cards** - Physical mail stands out
4. **Host Member Events** - People don't leave friends
5. **Exit Interviews** - Learn why they leave

#### 21. The Churn Mathematics
```
10% Monthly Churn: $100 × 10 months = $1,000 LTV
3% Monthly Churn: $100 × 33 months = $3,300 LTV
Result: 3.3× increase from same customer
```

3. Incentive Alignment (success together)
4. Community Linking (social glue)
5. Fire Bad Customers (protect energy)
6. Annual Pricing (2-3× less churn)
7. Cancellation Calls (30-50% save rate)
8. NPS Surveys (measure satisfaction)
9. Customer Journey Mapping (fix friction)

#### 23. The Crazy 8 Framework (LTV Optimization)
Eight ways to increase customer value:
1. Raise prices

3. Add downsells
4. Add cross-sells
5. Add continuity
6. Increase frequency

2. **Problem** (Day 1): Agitate the pain

7. **Urgency** (Day 6): Scarcity, deadline
8. **Close** (Day 7): Final chance, doors close

#### 25. The 7-Day Nurture Sequence
- **Day 1:** Welcome + Quick Win
- **Day 2:** Story + Vulnerability
- **Day 3:** Framework + Value
- **Day 4:** Case Study + Proof
- **Day 5:** Objection Handling
- **Day 6:** Testimonial Stack
- **Day 7:** The Offer + Urgency

**Key Principle:** The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make.

### GUARANTEE FRAMEWORKS

#### 26. The Guarantee Escalation Ladder
1. No guarantee (weak)
2. Standard money-back (expected)
3. Extended guarantee (better)
4. Conditional guarantee ("If you do X, we guarantee Y")
5. Reverse risk ("We pay YOU if it doesn't work")
6. Extreme ("We don't get paid until you succeed")

#### 27. Risk Reversal Formula
```
Risk Reversal = Guarantee + Conditions + Speed
```

**Example:** "30 days or money back + we pay you $100 + immediate refund"

### PSYCHOLOGY FRAMEWORKS

#### 33. Scarcity Types
- Limited spots (capacity constraint)
- Limited bonuses (first X buyers)
- Limited time (deadline)

#### 34. Urgency Types
- Price increase (ends Friday)
- Bonus disappearing (gone at midnight)
- Doors closing (program starts Monday)

**Rule:** Only use if REAL. Don't fake it.

#### 35. Anchoring Effect
First number seen sets expectation.
Show $10K first, then $997 feels cheap.

#### 36. The Rule of 100
**Under $100:** Percentage discounts seem bigger
- "20% off $50" = sounds good
- "$10 off $50" = sounds small

**Over $100:** Absolute discounts seem bigger
- "20% off $500" = hard to calculate
- "$100 off $500" = sounds huge

#### 37. Charm Pricing
- $97 vs $100
- $997 vs $1,000
- $9,997 vs $10,000

Left-digit effect creates perception of much lower price.

#### 38. Prestige Pricing
Round numbers signal luxury:
- $1,000 vs $997 for premium
- $10,000 vs $9,997 for VIP

## COMMON QUESTIONS QUICK ANSWERS

### Q: How do I price my product?
**A:** Start 2-3× higher than seems reasonable. Test with new customers. Use value-based pricing, not competitor-based.

### Q: How many hooks should I test?
**A:** 50-100 minimum. Most businesses test 1-2. Test 10× more than competitors.

### Q: What's the best lead generation method?
**A:** Start with warm outreach (DMs, referrals). Then add content. Then cold outreach. Finally, paid ads (after offer is proven).

### Q: How do I reduce churn?
**A:** Implement the Five Horsemen: Track attendance, reach out 2×/week, send handwritten cards, host events, do exit interviews.

### Q: Should I offer a discount?
**A:** Never discount. Always add bonuses. Discounts devalue. Bonuses increase perceived value.

### Q: How do I create a Grand Slam Offer?
**A:** Solve ALL problems, be incomparable (unique mechanism), create massive value discrepancy (10x value-to-price).

### Q: What's the best guarantee?
**A:** So good they'd feel stupid not to try. But with clear requirements that only committed people qualify.

### Q: How much should I spend on ads?
**A:** Start with $50-100/day to test. Never spend on ads until your offer converts with warm traffic.

### Q: How long should my content be?
**A:** As long as it needs to be, no longer. Focus 80% of effort on the first 5 seconds (hook).

### Q: Should I focus on SEO or paid ads?
**A:** SEO for long-term asset. Paid ads for immediate scale. Do both if possible. Start with what you can sustain consistently.

### Q: How do I increase LTV?
**A:** Use the Crazy 8: Raise prices, add upsells/downsells/cross-sells, add continuity, increase frequency, generate referrals, reduce churn.

### Q: What's the #1 mistake entrepreneurs make?
**A:** Underpricing. Most charge 10× less than they could. Test higher prices.

### Q: How do I write a hook?
**A:** Call Out + Condition for Value. "You're [situation] + here's [solution]."

### Q: Should I offer lifetime access?
**A:** NEVER. Lifetime deals become liabilities. Always offer time-limited or subscription.

### Q: How do I scale?
**A:** Fix conversion first. Then increase AOV. Then scale traffic. Never scale a broken funnel.

### Q: What's more important: traffic or conversion?
**A:** Conversion. Fix the leaky bucket before pouring more water in.

### Q: How do I get testimonials?
**A:** Deliver exceptional results. Ask immediately after wins. Make it easy (provide template).

### Q: Should I niche down?
**A:** Yes. Riches are in the niches. Specificity = credibility = higher prices.

### Q: How do I handle price objections?
**A:** Don't lower price. Improve value communication. Add more bonuses. Show ROI.

### Q: What's the best business model?
**A:** Recurring revenue > one-time. High-ticket > low-ticket. Done-for-you > DIY.
