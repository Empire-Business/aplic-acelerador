## GETTING STARTED QUESTIONS

### Q: I'm just starting out. What should I do first?

**A:** First, create a Grand Slam Offer. Everything else is noise if your offer sucks.

If people don't want what you're selling, nothing else matters. Spend 80% of your time here. Make it so good people feel stupid saying no. THEN worry about traffic, marketing, and everything else.

**See:** `implementation-guides.md` - "How to Create a Grand Slam Offer"

---

### Q: I have no money. Can I still use these frameworks?

**A:** Absolutely. In fact, most of these work better when you're broke.

Start with warm outreach (DMs, referrals). It's free. Then add content creation (also free). These two alone can build a six-figure business. I started with $1,036 in my bank account and $3,300/day in debt. You have more than I did.

**See:** `implementation-guides.md` - "How to Generate Leads"

---

### Q: I'm not sure what business to start. Any advice?

**A:** Here's the truth: Ideas are worthless. Execution is everything.

Pick a niche you know something about. Find a problem people will pay to solve. Create a solution. Sell it. You'll figure out the rest along the way. The money isn't in the idea - it's in the execution.

**Better yet:** Buy an existing business. You can find profitable businesses for 2-3× annual profit. Skip the startup phase entirely.

---

### Q: Do I need to be an expert to teach something?

**A:** No. You just need to be one step ahead of the people you're teaching.

Here's what matters: Can you get them results? If yes, you're qualified. If no, you're not (even if you have 100 certifications).

Be a practitioner, not a theorist. Results are the only credential that matters.

30 days if you go ALL IN. 90 days if you're consistent. Never if you keep looking for shortcuts.

Here's what I did: 6 gym launches simultaneously. $3,300/day in debt. Worked 16-hour days. Made $100,117 in month one (just enough to cover debt). 24 months later: $120M in sales.

You want the result? Put in the work.

Here's the truth: You're going to fail. A lot. The question isn't if you'll fail. It's whether you'll quit when you do.

## OFFER CREATION QUESTIONS

### Q: What makes an offer "Grand Slam"?

**A:** Three things:

1. **Solve ALL the problems** - Not just one. Every frustration, every objection, solve it BEFORE they experience it.

2. **Be incomparable** - Create a category of one. Unique mechanism, unique delivery, unique guarantee. If they can compare you to competitors, you fucked up.

3. **Massive value discrepancy** - Make it worth 10× what you charge. Stack bonuses until the value is obvious.

Do those three things and price doesn't matter.

---

### Q: How do I know if my offer is good enough?

**A:** Test it.

Put it in front of real people. See if they buy. If they don't, your offer sucks. Improve it. Test again. Repeat until they do.

Here's a clue: If nobody's telling you "you're crazy" for offering so much at this price, your offer isn't good enough yet.

**See:** `templates-forms.md` - Offer Templates

---

### Q: How many bonuses should I include?

**A:** 5-10 minimum. But not random crap.

Each bonus must solve a REAL problem your customer has. Use the Trim & Stack Matrix:
- High value/low cost = GOLD (include these)
- High value/high cost = Keep (price premium)
- Low value/anything = Eliminate

Stack the gold. That's how you create 10× value.

---

### Q: What if I can't think of any unique mechanism?

**A:** Then you're not trying.

Take your process. Give it a name. Make it sound proprietary.

"The 5-Step Fat Loss System"
"The 30-Day Business Accelerator"
"The Million Dollar Launch Framework"

It doesn't have to be actually unique. It just has to SOUND unique. People can't compare what they can't categorize.

---

### Q: Should I offer a discount?

**A:** Never. Ever. Fucking discounts.

Discounts devalue your offer. Always add bonuses instead.

Discount: "Was $997, now $497" (signals it wasn't worth $997)
Bonus: "Still $997, but I'll add $500 in bonuses" (signals it's worth even more)

Same math. Totally different psychology.

---

### Q: What guarantee should I offer?

**A:** So good they'd feel stupid not to try.

But here's the key: Make the requirements so clear that only committed people qualify.

"Lose 30lbs in 30 days or I'll refund every penny + pay you $100.

Requirements: Attend 20 sessions, follow meal plan 90%, check in daily."

The guarantee is extreme (hard to say no). The requirements are strict (only committed qualify). That's how you win.

## PRICING QUESTIONS

### Q: How much should I charge?

**A:** More than you're comfortable with.

Start 2-3× higher than seems "reasonable." Then test higher. Let the market tell you if you're overpriced. You'll never know if you're underpriced.

Most entrepreneurs charge 10× less than they could. Don't be most entrepreneurs.

---

### Q: What if nobody buys at my price?

**A:** Then two things are true:

1. You found your ceiling (good data)
2. Your value communication sucks (fixable)

Lower price slightly. Test again. Repeat until people buy.

But here's what usually happens: You charge more, sell fewer, but make MORE total profit.

---

### Q: How do I raise prices on existing customers?

**A:** Use the RAISE letter framework:

1. **R**emind them of value you've delivered
2. **A**ddress the price directly (don't bury it)
3. **I**nvest in their future (show how higher price helps THEM)
4. **S**often with loyalty reward (grace period)
5. **E**xplain away concerns (safety valve)

If the increase is >50%, pick up the phone. Call them personally.

**See:** `templates-forms.md` - RAISE Letter Template

---

### Q: Should I charge monthly or annually?

**A:** Annual. Always annual.

Annual customers churn 2-3× less than monthly. Plus, you get cash upfront. Win-win.

Make monthly the expensive option. Offer 1-2 months free for annual. Default to annual billing.

---

### Q: What about payment plans?

**A:** Charge 20-30% more for them.

Full price: $1,997
Payments: $249 × 10 months = $2,490

People pay for convenience. Don't give it away for free.

---

### Q: How do I know if I'm overpriced?

**A:** The market will tell you.

If conversion drops below 1% and stays there even after improving your offer, you might be overpriced.

But test first. Most people are underpriced, not overpriced.

---

### Q: Can I change my price later?

**A:** Absolutely. In fact, you SHOULD.

Raise prices at least once per year. Inflation happens. Your value increases. Costs rise. Regular price increases normalize it.

Just communicate clearly and give existing customers grace period.

## MARKETING & ADVERTISING QUESTIONS

### Q: How many hooks should I test?

**A:** 50-100 minimum. Then 10 more variations of the winners.

Most businesses test 1-2 hooks. Test 100× more than that. Let data do the teaching. Top 10% → replicate. Bottom 10% → eliminate forever.

---

### Q: What's the best hook?

**A:** The one that performs best.

Don't guess. Test. Document results. Scale winners. Kill losers.

That said, start with these frameworks:
- Problem-focused: "Stop struggling with..."
- Benefit-focused: "How to [result] in [timeframe]"
- Story-focused: "I was [struggle]. Here's what I learned."

**See:** `quick-reference.md` - Hooks Formula

---

### Q: How much should I spend on ads?

**A:** Not a fucking penny until your offer converts.

Test with warm audience first (email list, website visitors). Verify people actually want what you're selling. THEN spend money on ads.

Start with $50-100/day. Scale what works. Kill what doesn't.

---

### Q: Which platform should I advertise on?

**A:** Where your customers are.

But if you don't know, start with Facebook. It works for almost everything. Once you have a winner there, expand to YouTube, Google, TikTok.

Don't try everything at once. Master one platform first.

---

### Q: How long should my ads be?

**A:** As long as it needs to be. No longer.

But spend 80% of your time on the first 5 seconds (the hook). If nobody stops, nothing else matters.

Most of my best ads are 30-60 seconds. Some are 2 minutes. It depends on the offer.

---

### Q: What's a good CTR?

**A:** Context matters, but here are benchmarks:

- Poor: <1%
- Okay: 1-2%
- Good: 2-5%
- Great: >5%

If you're below 1% after 50 tests, your offer might be the problem, not your hooks.

---

### Q: Should I use an agency or run ads myself?

**A:** Learn to do it yourself first.

Agencies are great, but how will you know if they're doing a good job if you don't understand it yourself?

Once you're spending $10K+/month and have proven creatives, then consider an agency.

---

### Q: How do I scale winning ads?

**A:** Two ways:

1. **Vertical scaling:** Increase budget on winning ads
2. **Horizontal scaling:** Create similar ads to the winners

Use the 70-20-10 rule:
- 70% budget to proven winners
- 20% to adjacent variations
- 10% to radical experiments

Scale gradually. Watch profitability closely.

## LEAD GENERATION QUESTIONS

### Q: What's the best way to generate leads?

**A:** The one that works for you.

But if you're starting out, here's the progression:
1. Warm outreach (DMs, referrals) - FREE, high conversion
2. Content marketing - FREE, slow but compounds
3. Cold outreach - Low cost, scalable
4. Paid ads - Requires capital, unlimited scale

Don't skip steps. Master each before moving to the next.

---

### Q: How many DMs should I send per day?

**A:** 50 minimum. 100 is better.

But not spammy bullshit. Actual value. Personalized. Relevant.

If you're not getting at least 5-10 responses per 50 DMs, your messaging sucks. Fix it.

---

### Q: What should my lead magnet be?

**A:** Something your ideal customer actually wants.

Three types that work:
1. **Info product:** PDF guide, template, checklist
2. **Tool:** Calculator, assessment, trial
3. **Sample:** Free consultation, free challenge

Make it so valuable they'd pay for it. Then give it away.

---

### Q: How do I build an email list from scratch?

**A:** One email at a time.

1. Create valuable lead magnet
2. Create landing page
3. Drive traffic (warm outreach, content, ads)
4. Capture emails
5. Deliver value immediately
6. Nurture with email sequence
7. Make offer after trust is built

Rinse and repeat. Every. Single. Day.

---

### Q: Should I buy email lists?

**A:** No. Fucking never.

Purchased lists have terrible engagement. Are often illegal (GDPR, CAN-SPAM). Destroy your sender reputation.

Build your list organically. It takes longer but the quality is 10× better.

---

### Q: How often should I email my list?

**A:** More than you're comfortable with.

Daily when you have something valuable to say. 2-3× per week minimum.

Nobody unsubscribes from "too many good emails." They unsubscribe from "too much spam."

---

### Q: What's a good open rate?

**A:** Depends on your list:

- Cold list: 10-20% is good
- Warm lead list: 20-35% is good
- Customer list: 30-50% is good

If you're below these, improve your subject lines and deliver more value.

## SALES QUESTIONS

### Q: I hate selling. How do I sell without feeling sleazy?

**A:** Make a Grand Slam Offer.

When your offer is so good people feel stupid saying no, you don't have to "sell." You just help them buy.

Sleazy sales happens when you're convincing someone to do something they shouldn't. Great sales is helping someone do what they already want to do.

---

### Q: Should I do sales calls or sell directly from a page?

**A:** Both. Start with what you can afford.

Under $1,000: Sell directly from page (need better funnel)
$1,000-5,000: Sales call optional
$5,000+: Sales call required

Higher price = more friction = more trust needed = sales call.

---

### Q: How do I handle price objections?

**A:** Don't lower price. Improve value communication.

"Let me ask you this: What would it be worth to you to [solve problem/achieve result]?"

Show ROI. Break down cost to daily. Show what it costs if they DON'T solve the problem.

Price is almost never the real objection. It's usually value, trust, or timing.

---

### Q: What's the best closing technique?

**A:** The one that feels natural.

But here's what works:

"Based on everything we discussed, does this make sense to move forward with?"

If yes: "Great, let's get you started..."
If no: "What concerns do you have?"

Then address the concern and revisit the close.

---

### Q: How many follow-ups should I do?

**A:** 5-7 minimum. Most sales happen after the 5th contact.

But provide value with each follow-up. Don't just "checking in." Share something useful. Teach something. Help them.

---

### Q: Should I use urgency and scarcity?

**A:** Only if it's REAL.

"Limited spots" - better actually be limited
"Ends Friday" - better actually end Friday

People can smell fake urgency from a mile away. Once they catch you lying, you lose them forever.

---

### Q: How do I increase my conversion rate?

**A:** Fix the leaky bucket.

1. Improve your offer (biggest lever)
2. Add more proof (testimonials, case studies)
3. Improve your copy (clearer, more compelling)
4. Add stronger guarantee
5. Remove friction (simpler checkout)

Test one variable at a time. Document results. Keep winners.

## RETENTION QUESTIONS

### Q: Why is retention so important?

**A:** Because retention = 3.3× more money from same customers.

Here's the math:
10% monthly churn = 10-month customer lifespan = $1,000 LTV
3% monthly churn = 33-month customer lifespan = $3,300 LTV

Same customer. Same price. 3.3× more money.

---

### Q: What's the #1 way to reduce churn?

**A:** Track attendance religiousiously.

Zero attendance = churn imminent. Contact absentees within 24 hours. Document why they stopped showing up. Fix the issue.

This one thing will reduce churn 30%+.

---

### Q: How often should I contact customers?

**A:** 2× per week minimum.

Personal touchpoints. Value delivery. Check-ins. Progress updates.

Presence prevents leaving. Out of sight = out of mind = churn.

---

### Q: Should I fire bad customers?

**A:** Immediately. Absolutely.

Toxic customers destroy team morale. Drain your energy. Cost more than they're worth.

Fire them fast and politely. "This isn't a good fit." Refund if needed. Protect your business and your team.

---

### Q: What's a good churn rate?

**A:** Depends on your business, but:

- Terrible: >15% monthly
- Okay: 10-15% monthly
- Good: 5-10% monthly
- Great: <5% monthly
- Elite: <3% monthly

Aim for <5%. Everything below that is bonus.

---

### Q: Should I offer annual or monthly billing?

**A:** Annual. Always.

Annual customers churn 2-3× less. Plus, cash upfront. Better for them (commitment), better for you (cash flow).

Make monthly the expensive option.

---

### Q: How do I win back cancelled customers?

**A:** Call them immediately when they cancel.

30-50% save rate if you handle it right. Learn why they're leaving. Address the real issue. Sometimes win them back. Always gain insight.

**See:** `templates-forms.md` - Exit Interview Script

---

### Q: What's the best retention strategy?

**A:** Deliver exceptional results.

Everything else is tactics. If people get what they paid for, most will stay. If they don't, nothing else matters.

Then layer on: Community, accountability, events, personal touches.

## SCALING QUESTIONS

### Q: When should I start scaling?

**A:** After you have something worth scaling.

Proven offer? Check.
Converting funnel? Check.
Positive unit economics? Check.
Cash to invest? Check.

Now scale. Not before.

---

### Q: Should I scale traffic or conversion first?

**A:** Conversion. Always conversion.

Fix the leaky bucket before pouring more water in. Scaling a broken funnel just loses money faster.

Once conversion is solid, THEN scale traffic.

---

### Q: How fast should I scale?

**A:** As fast as you can without breaking things.

But watch these metrics like a hawk:
- Conversion rate (should stay stable)
- Churn rate (should not increase)
- Profit margins (should not compress)
- Cash flow (don't run out)

Scale gradually. Monitor daily. Adjust as needed.

---

### Q: When should I hire my first employee?

**A:** When the cost of NOT hiring exceeds the cost of hiring.

You're working 16-hour days. You're turning down opportunities. You're the bottleneck.

That's when you hire. Not before.

Start with low-cost, high-impact hires:
- VA for admin work
- Content writer for marketing
- Customer service for support

---

### Q: Should I raise money or bootstrap?

**A:** Bootstrap until you can't anymore.

Raising money sells equity. Bootstrapping keeps ownership. Plus, constraints make you creative and efficient.

Only raise money when:
1. You need it for growth (not survival)
2. You have proven unit economics
3. You can deploy capital effectively

---

### Q: How do I know if I'm ready for the next level?

**A:** You're not.

Nobody ever feels "ready." The people who succeed just start anyway.

Here's what matters: Can you deliver? If yes, you're ready. Everything else is noise.

---

### Q: What's the biggest mistake people make when scaling?

**A:** Scaling before product-market fit.

They have mediocre offer. Converting at 2%. Churn at 15%.

So they spend $50K/month on ads. Now they have more leads, same problems. Just bigger.

Fix the fundamentals first. Then scale.
