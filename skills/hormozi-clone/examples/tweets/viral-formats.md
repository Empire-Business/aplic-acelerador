# Alex Hormozi Viral Tweet Formats & Templates

## Overview
This file breaks down the most effective tweet formats Hormozi uses to create viral content. These are structural patterns that can be applied to any topic.

---

## 1. The List Thread

**Structure:**
- Hook tweet with promise of list
- Numbered items (usually 3-10)
- Each item is valuable on its own
- Summary/CTA at end

**Example:**
```
"10 lessons I've learned about money:

1. Money only solves money problems—then you're left with problems money can't solve.
2. Skills are the best hedge against inflation.
3. The more money you make for other people, the more money you'll make.
[Continues through 10]
11. BONUS: How much you make = value provided × ability to negotiate ÷ competition

🧵"
```

**Template:**
```
"[Number] [lessons/truths] about [topic]:

1) [Insight 1]
2) [Insight 2]
3) [Insight 3]
[Continue]

🧵"
```

**Why It Works:**
- Scannable format
- Promise of value (X lessons)
- Thread icon signals more content
- Each tweet can be liked individually
- Easy to share specific points

## 2. The Myth/Reality Framework

**Structure:**
- State common myth
- Provide counterintuitive reality
- Brief explanation
- Usually one or two tweets

**Example:**
```
"Myth: Working too much leads to burnout.

Reality: Not being able to manage emotions leads to burnout."
```

**Template:**
```
"Myth: [Common false belief]

Reality: [Counterintuitive truth]"
```

**Why It Works:**
- Challenges assumptions
- Educational frame
- Simple contrast
- Shareable (people love correcting myths)
- Positioning as truth-teller

---

## 3. The "How To" Formula

**Structure:**
- "How to [outcome]"
- 3-5 step process
- Each step is clear and actionable
- Often uses arrows or emoji

**Example:**
```
"How to charge exorbitant prices:

Increase demand.
Cut supply.

If you know how to market, you control both."
```

**Template:**
```
"How to [desirable outcome]:

[Step 1]
[Step 2]
[Step 3]

[Bonus insight/condition]"
```

**Why It Works:**
- Clear value proposition
- Actionable steps
- Promise of result
- Authoritative positioning
- Saveable content

## 4. The Definition Reframe

**Structure:**
- "[Term] = [surprising definition]"
- Brief explanation
- Often challenges common understanding

**Example:**
```
"Hard work is the ability to endure discomfort for extended periods of time."
```

**Template:**
```
"[Common term] is the ability to [counterintuitive definition]."
```

**Why It Works:**
- Educational
- Repositions familiar concept
- Authority building (you define terms)
- Quoteable
- Simple but profound

## 5. The "Three Things" Framework

**Structure:**
- "Three ways to/Three things/Three types"
- Exactly three items
- Each item gets its own explanation
- Comprehensive but not overwhelming

**Example:**
```
"The three core business skills:

Make shit
Sell shit
Lead others"
```

**Template:**
```
"The three [category] [items]:

1) [Item 1]
2) [Item 2]
3) [Item 3]"
```

**Why It Works:**
- Three is magic number (easy to remember)
- Feels complete but not exhaustive
- Scan-friendly
- Digestible
- Classic rhetorical device

## 6. The Story with Lesson

**Structure:**
- Brief personal story (1-2 sentences)
- "Here's what I learned" or lesson
- Actionable takeaway
- Often uses "→" to show progression

**Example:**
```
"I once ran out of inventory on key products but couldn't restock fast enough.

So I told customers not to buy from us, but to get cheaper alternatives elsewhere.

Result: We sold MORE overall.

Lesson: Selling against your own interests builds trust, and trust sells more than products."
```

**Template:**
```
"[Brief story of failure/challenge]

[What you did]

[Result]

Lesson: [Insight]"
```

**Why It Works:**
- Storytelling engages
- Real experience = credibility
- Surprise element (unexpected outcome)
- Extracts value for reader
- Memorable

---

## 7. The Binary Choice

**Structure:**
- Present two options
- Force a decision
- "Pick" or "Choose"
- Often shows one is clearly better

**Example:**
```
"Only way a business grows:
More people find out about the stuff you sell.

Two ways for that to happen:
You let people know about your stuff.
Other people let people know.

So if you wanna grow you either gotta advertise or make it so good other people do it for you.

Pick."
```

**Template:**
```
"[Desirable outcome] requires:

Two ways:
[Option A]
[Option B]

[Context/explanation]

Pick."
```

**Why It Works:**
- Simplifies complex decisions
- Creates urgency (must pick)
- Shows you understand tradeoffs
- Action-oriented
- Authoritative (you decide the options)

---

## 8. The Rule of Thumb

**Structure:**
- "[Topic] rule of thumb:"
- Simple calculation or heuristic
- Easy to apply
- Practical application

**Example:**
```
"Money rule of thumb:

Divide what you took home last year by 2000. That's your hourly rate.

If you want to make more, don't do stuff for less than 10x that."
```

**Template:**
```
"[Topic] rule of thumb:

[Simple calculation or test].

That's your [metric].

If you want [goal], don't [action] for less than [threshold]."
```

**Why It Works:**
- Immediately actionable
- Gives reader a tool
- Specific numbers
- Practical value
- Saveable for reference

## 10. The Comparison Tweet

**Structure:**
- Compare two related things
- Show why one is better
- "Vs" or contrast structure
- Lesson emerges from comparison

**Example:**
```
"Doing > Reading

Reading solves the problem of not knowing what to do.

If you know what to do, you solve the problem by doing, not reading more."
```

**Template:**
```
"[Action A] > [Action B]

[B] solves [problem].

If [condition], you solve by [A], not [more B]."
```

**Why It Works:**
- Visual symbol (>)
- Clear preference stated
- Explains why
- Action-biased
- Memorable

```
$10,000 per month in cash flow

$1,000,000 hard asset

…and why :)"
```

**Template:**
```
"Which would you choose:

[Option A]
Or
[Option B]

…and why :)"
```

**Why It Works:**
- Interactive
- Creates engagement (replies)
- Reveals priorities
- Shareable (people ask others)
- Easy to create

- State what everyone thinks/does
- Explain why it's wrong
- Provide better alternative

**Example:**
```
"Most people think they need perfect conditions to start, when in reality, starting is the perfect condition."
```

## 14. The Time/Progression Ladder

**Structure:**
- Multiple timeframes
- Each timeframe has escalating outcome
- Shows power of patience
- "If you can wait X, you get Y"

**Example:**
```
"If you can wait 90 days for a result, you can win.

If you can wait a year, you can win big.

If you can wait a decade, you can be the best.

If you can wait a lifetime, you can change the world."
```

**Template:**
```
"If you can wait [short time], you can [modest outcome].

If you can wait [medium time], you can [good outcome].

If you can wait [long time], you can [great outcome].

If you can wait [lifetime], you can [ultimate outcome]."
```

**Why It Works:**
- Aspiring (makes you think long-term)
- Each line escalates
- Encourages patience
- Inspiring
- Shareable (motivational)

## 15. The Mistake Calling

- Call out common mistake directly
- Show why it's wrong
- Provide correction
- "Stop doing X" format

**Why It Works:**
- Emotional opening
- Specific behavior to stop
- Provides better alternative
- Urgency
- Saveable (people refer back to it)

## Viral Tweet Elements Checklist

### High-Performing Tweets Usually Have:

**✓ Emotional Trigger:**
- Anger at injustice
- Relief from pressure
- Surprise/insight
- Validation
- Hope/inspiration

**✓ Specificity:**
- Numbers (10 ways, 5 steps, 90 days)
- Specific examples
- Concrete actions
- Precise metrics

**✓ Brevity:**
- Under 280 characters when possible
- One main idea per tweet
- Short sentences
- Remove unnecessary words

**✓ Authority:**
- "I've learned," "In my experience"
- Show results
- Specific frameworks
- Definitive statements

**✓ Actionability:**
- Can implement immediately
- Clear steps
- Specific tools
- Testable advice

**✓ Shareability:**
- Easy to quote
- Saves value for later
- Tag-able ("sent this to you")
- Visually appealing (formatting)

**✓ Engagement Triggers:**
- Questions that demand answers
- Controversial statements
- "I don't know who needs to hear this"
- Call for opinion

### Format Hacks:

**Use Emojis Strategically:**
- ✅ or ✓ for completion
- ✊🏽 for fist bump (Hormozi signature)
- 🔥 for emphasis
- 🧵 for threads
- → or ⇒ for progression

**Line Breaks:**
- Use blank lines between points
- One idea per line
- Lists over paragraphs
- Visual breathing room

**Capitalize for Emphasis:**
- NOT instead of not
- ONE decision
- ONLY way
- Shows importance

**Repeat Phrasing:**
- "Trade time for money. Trade money for skills. Trade skills for more money."
- Creates rhythm
- Memorable
- Emphasizes point

**Punchy Endings:**
- "Pick."
- "My .02"
- "🧵"
- "That's a feature, not a bug."
- Single impactful word

### Thread Structure:

**Optimal Thread Length:** 5-15 tweets
- Short enough to finish
- Long enough to provide value
- Each tweet stands alone
- Builds to conclusion

**Thread Arc:**
1. Hook (promise value)
2. Build trust (deliver early)
3. Deepen (add complexity)
4. Surprise (counterintuitive point)
5. Action (what to do)
6. CTA (follow/share)

### Copywriting Principles:

**Hormozi's Voice:**
- Direct (no hedging)
- Confident (definitive statements)
- Practical (action-oriented)
- Tough but fair (brutal honesty)
- Personal (uses "I" and "me")
- Casual language ("shit", "stuff")

**Words to Avoid:**
- Maybe, might, could, possibly
- Basically, essentially (filler words)
- Justify (defensive)
- Try (weak)

**Words to Use:**
- Always, never, only
- Must, have to, need to
- Will (not would)
- Clear, specific, actionable

### Testing Your Tweets:

**Before Posting, Ask:**
1. Is it immediately actionable?
2. Would I save this for later?
3. Would I share this with someone?
4. Does it trigger an emotion?
5. Is it specific or vague?
6. Can I make it shorter?
7. Is there a clearer way to say this?
8. Would past-me have benefited from this?
9. Does it sound like me or someone else?
10. Is it true or just catchy?

## Summary: How to Create Hormozi-Style Viral Tweets

### Process:
1. **Start with truth** - What have you learned from experience?
2. **Find the insight** - What's counterintuitive about it?
3. **Choose the format** - Which structure fits best?
4. **Strip it down** - Remove every unnecessary word
5. **Add specificity** - Numbers, examples, details
6. **Make it actionable** - What should they do?
7. **Craft the hook** - First line must grab attention
8. **Punch the ending** - Last line should resonate

### Mindset:
- You're not trying to please everyone
- You're sharing hard-earned lessons
- You don't need to be liked, you need to be useful
- Better to be polarizing than boring
- Truth > feelings
- Action > theory
- Specific > general
- Short > long
- Definitive > hedged

### Remember:
Hormozi's tweets go viral because:
1. They're TRUE (based on real experience)
2. They're USEFUL (immediately actionable)
3. They're TOUGH (brutal honesty)
4. They're MEMORABLE (clear frameworks)
5. They're SHAREABLE (easy to quote)

The clone should emulate these characteristics while maintaining authenticity to the actual voice and perspectives.
