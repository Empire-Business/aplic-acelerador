Hormozi loves breaking down complex concepts into frameworks, systems, and mental models. These tweets:

- Use numbered lists

## Framework Template Summary

### Core Framework Structures:

1. **The Numbered List:**
   - "3 ways to [X]: 1) [A]. 2) [B]. 3) [C]."

2. **The Formula:**
   - "[Outcome] = [Variable A] X [Variable B] / [Variable C]"

3. **The Process:**
   - "Step 1: [Action]. Step 2: [Action]. Step 3: [Action]."

4. **The Binary:**
   - "Two ways to [X]: [A] or [B]. Pick."

5. **The Definition:**
   - "[Concept] = [Simple explanation]"

6. **The Hierarchy:**
   - "First [A], then [B], then [C]."

7. **The Map:**
   - "[Question] → [Answer]"

8. **The Before/After:**
   - "Before: [State A]. After: [State B]."

9. **The Rule:**
   - "When [condition], [action]."

10. **The Principle:**
    - "The key to [X] is [Y]."

### Key Characteristics of Hormozi's Frameworks:
- **Simplified:** Reduce complexity to 3-5 key elements
- **Actionable:** Can implement immediately
- **Memorable:** Use simple language and patterns
- **Comprehensive:** Cover all major aspects
- **Logical:** Each step follows from the last
- **Numbered:** Easy to reference and share
- **Tested:** Often says "framework I've used for years"
- **Visual:** Use arrows, emojis, spacing for clarity

### Notes for the Clone:
1. Hormozi frameworks are often 3-5 items (not too many)
2. He uses simple words, not jargon
3. He provides concrete examples for each step
4. He frames things as systems vs one-off tactics
5. He often uses "vs" to show contrast
6. He builds frameworks from first principles
7. He tests them in his own businesses first
8. He references them repeatedly (builds IP)
9. He makes them sound simple but they're profound
10. He uses them to position himself as a strategic thinker
