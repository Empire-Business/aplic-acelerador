# Alex Hormozi Hook-Style Tweets

## Overview
Hook tweets are designed to stop the scroll and grab attention immediately. They typically use:
- Counterintuitive statements
- Controversial takes
- Specific numbers/metrics
- Strong emotional triggers
- Pattern interrupts

---

## 1. Counterintuitive Hooks

**"Work life balance" assumes you're not living when you work.**
In my experience, it's been the opposite: when I work, I live.

**Analysis:**
- Challenges conventional wisdom
- Reframes a common complaint
- Personal experience adds credibility
- Short, punchy delivery

**Template:** "[Common belief] assumes [negative framing]. In reality, [opposite perspective]."

---

**Myth: Working too much leads to burnout.
Reality: Not being able to manage emotions leads to burnout.**

**Analysis:**
- Direct myth/reality structure
- Shifts blame from work to personal responsibility
- Controversial take on hot topic
- Creates curiosity about the "real" cause

**Template:** "Myth: [Common misconception]. Reality: [Counterintuitive truth]."

---

**I don't think traditional college is bad. It's just overpriced.**

**Analysis:**
- Qualified statement (not bad, just expensive)
- Nuanced position (more credible than extreme take)
- Addresses pain point (cost)
- Short and quotable

**Template:** "I don't think [popular thing] is [negative]. It's just [actual problem]."

---

**Dessert doesn't make you fat.
Just like buying shit doesn't make you poor.
It's about how much you spend vs consume. It's a ratio, not an absolute.**

**Analysis:**
- Uses analogy (dessert vs spending)
- Challenges binary thinking
- Provides logical framework
- Memorable phrasing

**Template:** "[X] doesn't cause [Y]. Just like [A] doesn't cause [B]. It's about [nuanced explanation]."

## 2. Controversial Takes

**Most people aren't that good.
They make one VSL and never edit it with obvious fixes.
They don't look at screen recordings to edit their pages.
They don't script out hooks.
They don't make enough ads.
They don't review sales calls.
Beating them isn't hard if you actually try.**

**Analysis:**
- Direct insult (creates strong reaction)
- Lists specific failures (shows expertise)
- Offers hope (you can beat them)
- Tribal signaling (us vs them)

**Template:** "Most [group] aren't that [quality]. They [list specific failures]. [Benefit if you do better]."

---

**Being the underdog makes you a dangerous competitor.
You have nothing to lose.
Everything to gain.
And no one sees you coming.
Don't resent it, lean into it.**

**Analysis:**
- Reframes weakness as strength
- Uses list format for impact
- Empowering message
- Memorable phrasing

**Template:** "[Negative position] makes you [positive framing]. [List advantages]. [Call to action]."

---

**Many businesses search for complex economic explanations for their failures when the obvious one is:
…they're just not that good.**

**Analysis:**
- Calls out overthinking
- Brutal honesty
- Builds authority (willing to tell hard truth)
- Creates defensiveness (engagement)

**Template:** "Many people look for [complex explanation] when [obvious simple truth]."

---

**No one with a brain worth picking, wants it picked.**

**Analysis:**
- Counterintuitive (advice usually says "find mentors")
- Short and punchy
- Challenges common wisdom
- Makes reader think

**Template:** "No one with [valuable trait] wants [that thing extracted]."

## 3. Number-Based Hooks

**"I own more suits when I was poor then when I was rich.
You can't spend appearances."**

**Analysis:**
- Personal story
- Visual contrast (poor vs rich)
- Memorable one-liner
- Challenges status symbols

**Template:** "I [action] when [negative state] than when [positive state]. [Punchline]."

---

**"The difference between a 7/10 product and a 9/10 product is about 5x the work."**

**Analysis:**
- Specific ratio (5x)
- Relatable scale (7 vs 9 out of 10)
- Sets expectations
- Justifies premium pricing

**Template:** "The difference between [decent] and [excellent] is [specific multiplier] the [effort/resource]."

---

**"10,000 cold calls will teach you more about sales than any book will."**

**Analysis:**
- Specific large number (10,000)
- Compares doing vs learning
- Challenges advice givers
- Emphasizes action over theory

**Template:** "[Large number] of [difficult action] will teach you more about [topic] than any [passive learning]."

---

**"54% of Americans read below a 6th grade level.
If you want to double your response rates, write to the majority."**

**Analysis:**
- Specific statistic (54%)
- Practical application (write simpler)
- Benefit promise (double response)
- Counterintuitive (simplify to improve)

**Template:** "[Percentage] of [group] [negative stat]. If you want [benefit], [opposite action]."

## 5. Pattern Interrupts

**"For the love of god please stop asking prospects "do you have any questions for me?"
Ask them to buy. If they have questions, they'll ask them. Otherwise, stop wasting time and move the ball forward."**

**Analysis:**
- Emotional opening ("for the love of god")
- Quotes specific phrase people use
- Direct command ("stop")
- Provides better alternative
- Tone of frustration/urgency

**Template:** "For the love of [deity/thing] please stop [common action]. [Better action]. If [condition], then [reaction]. Otherwise, [command]."

---

**"Just wanted to make a public prediction that @MrBeast will not only become a billionaire, but a centibillionaire."**

**Analysis:**
- Specific prediction
- Names famous person (social proof)
- Extreme claim (centibillionaire)
- Public commitment

**Template:** "Public prediction that [person] will not only [achieve X], but [even more extreme Y]."

---

**"I don't know who needs to hear this, but you can become a millionaire without having a 28-step morning routine."**

**Analysis:**
- Classic hook phrase ("I don't know who needs to hear this")
- Addresses pain point (morning routines)
- Relieves pressure
- Controversial in productivity space

**Template:** "I don't know who needs to hear this, but you can [desirable outcome] without [painful thing]."

---

**"Don't complain that you haven't taken a vacation in years.
You own the company. Get over it."**

**Analysis:**
- No empathy (tough love)
- Points out ownership (you chose this)
- Direct command ("get over it")
- Anti-entrepreneurship trend

**Template:** "Don't complain about [problem]. You [chose this/caused this]. [Tough love command]."

## 6. Question Hooks

**"Which would you choose:
$10,000 per month in cash flow
Or
$1,000,000 hard asset
…and why :)"**

**Analysis:**
- Forces reader to think
- Compares two desirable options
- Creates engagement (asks for comments)
- Reveals financial priorities

**Template:** "Which would you choose: [Option A] or [Option B]... and why?"

---

**"Are you "reinvesting in the business" or just making no profit?
Big difference."**

**Analysis:**
- Questions common narrative
- Offers uncomfortable alternative
- Short punchy confirmation
- Creates self-doubt

**Template:** "Are you [positive framing] or just [negative reality]? [Punchline]."

---

## Hook Templates Summary

### Basic Hook Formulas:
1. **Myth vs Reality:** "Myth: [X]. Reality: [Y]."
2. **Counterintuitive:** "Most people think [X], but actually [Y]."
3. **Number Shock:** "[X]% of people [negative thing]."
4. **Bold Statement:** "The [superlative] [thing] is [counterintuitive answer]."
5. **Question Choice:** "Which would you choose: [A] or [B]?"
6. **Direct Challenge:** "Stop [common action] and start [better action]."
7. **Personal Story:** "I [did X] when [negative state] more than when [positive state]."
8. **List Format:** "[Number] things [group] gets wrong about [topic]."
9. **Definitive Statement:** "There is no other way to [achieve X] except [method]."
10. **Pattern Interrupt:** "I don't know who needs to hear this, but [controversial statement]."

### Key Characteristics of Viral Hooks:
- **Brevity:** Under 280 characters, often much shorter
- **Clarity:** One main idea, no ambiguity
- **Emotion:** Triggers anger, surprise, curiosity, or relief
- **Specificity:** Uses numbers, names, concrete examples
- **Contrarian:** Goes against common wisdom
- **Authority:** Sounds like someone with experience
- **Action-Oriented:** Implies or states what to do
