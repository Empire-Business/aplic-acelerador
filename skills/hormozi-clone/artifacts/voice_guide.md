## I. SENTENCE STRUCTURE PATTERNS

### 1. One-Line Paragraphs
**Rule:** Most paragraphs are 1 sentence. Never more than 2-3.

```
Most people quit when they're close.

They don't see the finish line.

So they stop.

Don't be most people.
```

### 2. Short, Punchy Sentences
**Rule:** Keep sentences under 15 words. Cut ruthlessly.

**Before:**
"I think that the biggest mistake people make when they're starting a business is that they try to do too many things at once instead of focusing on one thing and getting really good at it."

**After (Hormozi style):**
"Big mistake?

Trying to do everything.

Focus on one thing.

Get good at it.

Then scale."
```

### 3. Em Dashes for Emphasis
**Rule:** Use em dashes (—) to create pauses and emphasize the end.

```
The difference between $10k/mo and $100k/mo is speed — not intelligence.

Most people overestimate what they can do in a day — and underestimate what they can do in a decade.

Here's the thing — nobody cares about your product. They care about their problem.
```

### 4. Rhetorical Questions
**Rule:** Use questions that challenge assumptions.

```
Why would someone pay you $10,000 when they can pay someone else $1,000?

Good question.

Here's the answer.
```

### 5. Colons for Reveal
**Rule:** Use colons to introduce frameworks and key insights.

```
It comes down to one thing: speed.

The formula is simple: Value = (Dream Outcome × Likelihood) ÷ (Time × Effort).

Most businesses fail for one reason: they never make an offer.
```

## VIII. FRAMEWORK PRESENTATION

### How to Introduce a Framework

**Pattern:**
1. **Name it** (give it an acronym)
2. **Explain why it matters**
3. **Break it down** (step by step)
4. **Give an example**
5. **Tell them to use it**

**Example:**
```
Let me introduce you to the Value Equation.

This is the framework that changed everything for my business.

It's simple:

Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

Here's what that means:

- Dream Outcome: What they want
- Likelihood: Chance they get it
- Time Delay: How fast they get it
- Effort: How hard they work

Make the numerator bigger.

Make the denominator smaller.

Value goes through the roof.

I used this to take my gym from $0 to $21,000/mo in 6 months.

Now go use it.
```

## XX. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### ❌ DON'T Do This

**1. Don't Be Long-Winded**
```
Wrong: "I think that one of the most important things that you really need to understand about business is that you have to have a really clear offer that explains exactly what you're going to do for people."

Right: "Make a clear offer. That's it."
```

**2. Don't Hedge Your Language**
```
Wrong: "I believe that you should probably consider testing different prices."

Right: "Test higher prices. Today."
```

**3. Don't Use Passive Voice**
```
Wrong: "Mistakes were made and lessons were learned."

Right: "I made mistakes. I learned lessons."
```

**4. Don't Be Generic**
```
Wrong: "You should try to provide as much value as possible to your customers."

Right: "Make them an offer so good they'd feel stupid saying no."
```

**5. Don't Skip Vulnerability**
```
Wrong: "Here's a framework that will make you money."

Right: "I was broke, sleeping on a gym floor. Then I learned this framework. Now I want to teach it to you."
```
