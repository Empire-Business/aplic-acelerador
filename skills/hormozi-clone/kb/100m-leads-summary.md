## THE COMPLETE FRAMEWORK: CORE FOUR LEAD GENERATION

**The Revolutionary Insight:**
There are ONLY 4 ways to generate leads. Everything else is a variation of these four methods. Master these four, and you never have to worry about leads again.

### 1. WARM OUTREACH
**What:** DMs, referrals, network connections, personal relationships

**Pros:**
- Zero cost (time investment only)
- High conversion rates (trust already exists)
- Perfect for starting out
- Ideal for high-ticket offers
- Builds relationships
- No platform risk

**Cons:**
- Hard to scale (limited by your time)
- Time-intensive
- Not passive
- Requires consistent personal effort
- Difficult to systematize

**Best For:**
- Starting with $0 budget
- High-ticket offers ($5K+)
- Building initial testimonials
- B2B services
- Local businesses

**Tactics & Implementation:**
- DM 50 people per day
- Ask for referrals from every client (make it systematic)
- Attend networking events strategically
- Partner with complementary businesses
- Use the "Book a Meeting From a Meeting" (BAMFAM) approach
- Follow up 5-7 times
- Personalize every outreach

**Hormozi's Warm Outreach Script Framework:**
```
1. Compliment something specific they've done
2. Mention mutual connection (if applicable)
3. Offer clear value (no vagueness)
4. Make simple, low-friction ask
5. Follow up consistently
```

**Key Principle:** "The richest people in the room are the ones who know the most people"

---

### 2. FREE CONTENT POSTING
**What:** YouTube, social media, SEO, podcasts, blogs

**Pros:**
- Builds brand over time
- Compounding effect (content works forever)
- High trust when they convert
- Attracts warm leads
- Creates ownership (your platform)
- Attracts your ideal clients
- Establishes authority

**Cons:**
- Slow to start (months for momentum)
- High effort for each piece
- Inconsistent results at first
- Algorithm dependence
- Requires consistency
- Delayed gratification

**Best For:**
- Building long-term brand asset
- Attracting organic traffic
- Establishing authority
- Creating owned audience
- Businesses with patience

**Tactics & Implementation:**
- Post daily when starting (momentum matters)
- Focus on one platform initially (master it)
- Repurpose content across platforms
- SEO for long-term traffic
- Document your journey (people love authenticity)
- Use value-based content (teach, don't just promote)
- Leverage other people's audiences (podcasts, guest posts)

**Hormozi's Content Framework:**
```
1. Hook (first 3 seconds)
2. Problem statement
3. Solution (your unique insight)
4. Proof/case study
5. Call to action (clear, singular)
```

**Key Principle:** "Content is a compound interest asset - it pays dividends forever"

**Content Platforms Priorities (choose one to start):**
- YouTube (long-form, SEO)
- LinkedIn (B2B, professionals)
- Instagram/TikTok (B2C, younger demographics)
- Twitter/X (entrepreneurs, tech)
- Facebook (older demographics, local)

### 3. COLD OUTREACH
**What:** Cold email, cold calling, cold DMs

**Pros:**
- Can scale (with systems)
- Low cost (can outsource)
- Targeted (you choose who to contact)
- Immediate results
- Predictable with volume
- No platform algorithm risk

**Cons:**
- Low conversion (no trust exists)
- Can damage brand if done poorly
- Legal restrictions (GDPR, CAN-SPAM)
- Requires volume to work
- High rejection rate
- Needs systems and processes

**Best For:**
- B2B businesses
- Specific niches
- When you have lists but no traffic
- Businesses with clear ideal customer profile
- Scaling after warm outreach works

**Tactics & Implementation:**
- Personalized outreach (research first)
- Provide value first (don't sell immediately)
- Follow up 5-7 times (most sales happen after 5th contact)
- Use tools for scaling (but keep personalization)
- A/B test messages constantly
- Focus on pain points, not features
- Qualify leads before reaching out

**Hormozi's Cold Outreach Framework:**
```
1. Research (find specific pain point)
2. Personalized hook (show you did homework)
3. Agitate the problem
4. Present solution (briefly)
5. Low-friction CTA (coffee, chat, not buy)
6. Follow-up sequence (5-7 touches)
```

**Key Principle:** "Cold outreach is a numbers game played with precision"

**Legal Note:** Always comply with regulations (CAN-SPAM, GDPR, etc.)

## UNDERSTANDING LEADS VS. ENGAGED LEADS

### Critical Distinction
Most businesses waste money on "leads" that aren't actually leads.

**True Definition of a Lead:**
Someone who has:
1. Raised their hand (expressed interest)
2. Has the problem you solve
3. Has the money to pay for it
4. Is qualified to buy

**Not a Lead:**
- Someone who downloaded a freebie but has no budget
- Someone who liked a post but has no problem
- Someone who clicked an ad but isn't qualified
- Someone who attended a webinar but won't buy

**Engaged Lead:**
A lead who has taken a qualifying action that demonstrates genuine interest and ability to buy.

**Examples of Engaged Leads:**
- Booked a call
- Requested a demo
- Started checkout
- Asked pricing questions
- Spent significant time consuming content

**Key Principle:** "One engaged lead is worth 1,000 unengaged subscribers"

## LEAD MAGNETS: THE THREE TYPES

### Why Lead Magnets Matter
You need to give value before you ask for money. Lead magnets are the first step in building trust.

### Type 1: REVEAL THE PROBLEM
**Concept:** Help them realize they have a problem they didn't know about

**Examples:**
- Quiz/Assessment: "How healthy is your business really?"
- Checklist: "10 signs you're leaving money on the table"
- Diagnostic tool: "Lead generation health check"
- Comparison chart: "Where you rank vs. competitors"

**Why It Works:**
- Creates urgency
- Positions you as expert
- Qualifies them (if they care about the problem)
- Low friction to consume

**Hormozi's Example:** "The 30-Day Gym Launch Assessment" - helped gym owners see they were losing money every day they weren't using his system

---

### Type 2: SAMPLES & TRIALS
**Concept:** Let them experience the solution before buying

**Examples:**
- Free trial (limited time)
- Free consultation
- Sample product
- First module of course
- Mini-version of service

**Why It Works:**
- Risk reversal
- Experience the value
- Build trust through results
- Natural upsell path

**Key Principle:** "The best way to sell is to let them experience the result"

**Hormozi's Warning:** Be careful with free trials. Many people take free stuff and never convert. Use "Trial with Penalty" instead (see Money Models book).

---

### Type 3: ONE STEP
**Concept:** Give them one quick win toward their goal

**Examples:**
- Template: "Email script for getting referrals"
- Swipe file: "5 Facebook ads that convert"
- Tool: "ROI calculator"
- Quick guide: "10 ways to get 5 more clients this week"

**Why It Works:**
- Immediate value
- Quick to consume
- Easy to implement
- Builds credibility
- Creates reciprocity

**Key Principle:** "Give them an immediate win, and they'll trust you with the bigger solution"

**Characteristics of Great Lead Magnets:**
1. **Specific** - solves one specific problem
2. **Immediate value** - can be used right away
3. **Quick to consume** - 10-15 minutes max
4. **High perceived value** - seems worth a lot
5. **Low cost to deliver** - digital or automated
6. **Naturally leads to offer** - logical next step is your product

## GETTING PEOPLE: LEAD GETTERS FRAMEWORK

### The Lead Getters Concept
You don't have to do all the lead generation yourself. You can leverage others.

### The Four Lead Getters

#### 1. CUSTOMERS
**Why:** Happy customers are your best source of new customers

**How to Systematize:**
- Ask at the right time (after a win)
- Make it easy (referral link, email template)
- Incentivize both parties
- Build referral systems into your product

**Example:** "For every friend you refer, you both get $100 off"

**Timing:**
- Immediately after they get a result
- When they say "thank you"
- During regular check-ins
- After they leave a review

**Key Principle:** "The best time to ask for a referral is when they're happiest"

---

#### 2. EMPLOYEES
**Why:** Employees have networks and credibility

**How to Implement:**
- Incentivize employee referrals
- Provide training on how to refer
- Make it part of the culture
- Share success stories
- Give them tools to make it easy

**Example:** "$500 bonus for every referred client who stays 3 months"

**Key Principle:** "Your employees should be your biggest evangelists"

---

#### 3. AGENCIES
**Why:** They have expertise and infrastructure

**When to Use:**
- You have capital but no expertise
- You want to move fast
- You need specialized knowledge

**How to Choose:**
- Look for case studies in your industry
- Check their client retention
- Understand their pricing model
- Clear communication expectations

**Pros:**
- Expert team
- Proven systems
- Faster results
- Less learning curve

**Cons:**
- Expensive
- Less control
- Dependency
- Communication overhead

**Key Principle:** "Good agencies are expensive. Bad agencies are more expensive."

---

#### 4. AFFILIATES
**Why:** Other people's audiences can become your customers

**How to Build Affiliate Program:**
- Create compelling offer for affiliates (high commission)
- Provide marketing materials (emails, social posts, ads)
- Make tracking easy (affiliate software)
- Pay on time (builds trust)
- Support your top affiliates

**Commission Structures:**
- 10-50% for digital products
- 5-20% for physical products
- 20-40% for services
- Recurring commissions for subscriptions

**Key Principle:** "Align incentives - your success is their success"

**Finding Affiliates:**
- Look for businesses with complementary audiences
- Industry influencers
- Satisfied customers
- Content creators in your space

## PAID ADVERTISING FRAMEWORK

### Before You Spend a Dollar on Ads

**The Pre-Flight Checklist:**
- [ ] Offer validated with warm traffic
- [ ] Landing page converts at 20%+
- [ ] Lead magnet works (40%+ opt-in)
- [ ] Sales process handles volume
- [ ] Unit economics work (can afford to pay for leads)
- [ ] Tracking installed (pixel, analytics)
- [ ] Clear avatar and messaging
- [ ] Budget for 90 days minimum

### The $100M Leads Ad Strategy

**Phase 1: Warm Market Validation ($50-100/day)**
- Target: Website visitors, email list, social media followers
- Goal: 20%+ conversion, prove offer works
- Duration: 2-4 weeks
- Success Metric: Positive ROI on front-end

**Phase 2: Cold Market Testing ($500-1,000/day)**
- Target: Lookalike audiences, interest targeting
- Creative: Test 10+ variations
- Goal: Find winning angle
- Duration: 4-8 weeks
- Success Metric: CAC less than 1/3 of LTV

**Phase 3: Aggressive Scaling ($5,000-50,000+/day)**
- Target: Scale winning audiences
- Creative: Beat controls consistently
- Goal: Maximum profitable spend
- Duration: Ongoing
- Success Metric: Maintain or improve ROAS

**Creative Testing Framework:**
1. Test different hooks
2. Test different formats (video, image, carousel)
3. Test different angles (pain, pleasure, social proof)
4. Test different lengths (short vs long)
5. Test different CTAs

**The 36:1 Target:**
Hormozi's benchmark: For every $1 spent on ads over a customer's lifetime, make $36 back.

**How to Calculate:**
- Customer Lifetime Value: $1,000
- Max Ad Spend: $1,000 ÷ 36 = $27.78
- If your CAC is under $27.78, scale indefinitely

**If CAC is too high:**
1. Fix the offer
2. Improve the product
3. Increase LTV
4. Lower ad costs
5. Improve conversion

## KEY METRICS & BENCHMARKS

### Metrics That Matter

**Track these, ignore everything else:**

1. **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**
   - How much to get one customer?
   - Calculate: Total ad spend ÷ Number of customers

2. **LTV (Lifetime Value)**
   - How much is a customer worth over time?
   - Calculate: Average purchase × Number of purchases × Customer lifespan

3. **ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)**
   - For every $1 in ads, how much comes back?
   - Calculate: Revenue ÷ Ad spend

4. **Conversion Rate**
   - What % of leads become customers?
   - Calculate: Customers ÷ Leads

5. **Lead Quality Score**
   - Track which sources produce best customers
   - Double down on best sources

**Hormozi's Targets:**
- 36:1 ROI on lifetime ad spend
- Under 30-day payback period
- CAC less than 1/3 of LTV
- 20%+ conversion from qualified leads
