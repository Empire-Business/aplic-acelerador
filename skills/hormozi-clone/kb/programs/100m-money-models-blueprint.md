**How It Works**

It's based on a single premise: *every business grows until it hits a constraint,
then it won't grow anymore.*

Once you relieve the constraint, the business grows again until it hits the next constraint.

**Think About It This Way...**

[Highway diagram]

Say you have a four-lane highway that narrows to one lane. What happens? Traffic jam.

If you add lanes before the bottleneck, what happens? Bigger traffic jam.

What happens if you add lanes after the bottleneck? Traffic still moves at one-lane speed.

In other words, you add potential to the system but not throughput.

This is what most business owners do.
They add capacity before or after the bottleneck.
They work. But nothing grows.

The only way to get rid of the traffic jam—aka—scale the business—is
to add lanes to the one-lane section.

Once you do that, traffic flows at "four-lane speed"—think maximum revenue growth—
until it hits the next bottleneck.

So, the obvious question becomes—*how do you make sure what you're working on
is the "one-lane problem" —aka— the constraint?*

**How to Discover Your Constraint (Your One-Lane Problem)**

1. Ask: "What's the one thing you do that makes the most money?"
2. Then ask: "What if I just did 50X more of that?"
3. When you say you can't, ask yourself: "Why not?"
→ The answer to that question is the constraint limiting the business.

**Easier Said Than Done**

Let's say "scaling ads" is the thing that would make you money
— BUT — the only thing that happens is you're no longer profitable when you scale them.

That's the clear symptom, but the root cause could be:
you don't earn enough per customer — or — it costs too much to get them.

To figure out which, you'd look at industry benchmarks.

Let's say you discover you're getting customers for the same price as everyone else…
then you'd need to make them worth more: raise prices, lower churn, add upsell/cross sell,
or some combination.

Alternatively, let's say you discover it costs *way* too much to get customers.

Then you'd need: fix targeting, make more ads, make better ads, split test
your offer/lead magnet, optimize click-to-journey for your customers…or some combination.

This is a lot. **So, where do you start?**

A wise man learns from others' mistakes"* — Some wise philosopher

This brings us to the only two ways you can solve the problem:
1) trial and error

To be clear, you'll figure it out either way.
