## MODULE 21: Rollover Upsell

### Concept
Credit past payments toward larger future purchases. When a customer has already paid money, apply that value as credit toward a more expensive offer.

### Origin Story
Born from the "win your money back" problem. Gym in Las Vegas applied credits from annual plans to keep 70%+ of customers paying.

### 4 Target Audiences

#### 1. Win Back Campaigns (Re-engage Old Customers)
**Strategy**: Message customers who haven't purchased in 6+ months
**Script**: "Sandra, you haven't been here in 6 months. I noticed you have $500 in credit with us. Want to use it on [new service]?"
**Psychology**: Feels like recovering stuck money, not spending new money

#### 2. Rescue Unhappy Customers (Redo, Not Refund)
**Strategy**: When customer demands refund, offer better solution instead
**Script**: "Instead of refunding, let me apply everything you've paid toward [service that actually solves your problem]"
**Psychology**: Turns complaints into committed customers

#### 3. Steal Competitor Customers
**Strategy**: Find 1-star reviews of competitors, offer to match their spend as credit
**Process**:
- Search competitor's 1-star reviews
- Contact dissatisfied customers
- Offer: "Whatever you spent with them, I'll credit toward my service"
- Result: Transform their unhappy customers into your raving fans

#### 4. Leverage Current Customers
**Strategy**: Apply current purchase value toward larger package
**Timing**: Immediately after initial purchase
**Example**: Bought monthly plan → apply payment as credit toward annual plan

### The "What": What to Offer

**Three Options**:
1. More of the same product (quantity)
2. Better version (quality upgrade)
3. Something new/different (complementary)

**Golden Rule**: Roll credit into something **5x more expensive**
- Customer paid $500 → Offer $2,500 package
- Maintains healthy margins while feeling generous

### The "How": Applying the Credit

**Three Methods**:
1. **Full credit at once** - Apply entire value immediately
2. **Dilution over time** - Spread credit across multiple payments
3. **Fee waiver** - Create a fee, then use credit to eliminate it

### The Gift Card Play (Advanced Rollover)

**Structure**:
- Sell $200 gift cards for only $20
- Limit: 2 per person, must gift to others
- Requirement: Valid only on minimum $1,000 purchases
- Result: Clients pay to refer, you get new customers, profit from unused cards

### Golden Rules

#### 1. Minimum 4x Ratio
New product must cost at least 4x the credit value
- Keeps maximum discount at 25%
- Preserves profit margins

#### 2. Optimize Credit Amount
- Don't automatically credit 100%
- Test to find optimal conversion point
- If $200 converts, don't offer $500

#### 3. Always Create Urgency
- "This offer only valid right now"
- Full price for future purchases
- Temporal scarcity

## MODULE 22: Downsell Offers

### Core Philosophy
> When someone says "no," they're not saying "no" to everything. They're only saying "no" to THAT offer.

Your job: Find a solution that fits their budget and desires without losing the sale.

### Two Main Forms

#### 1. Change HOW They Pay
- Same total value
- Distributed over time
- Strategy: Now vs. Later
- Eases entry without reducing total price

#### 2. Change WHAT They Receive
- Adjust quality
- Modify quantity
- Deliver something different
- Maintains price coherence

### Critical Warning: The Car Salesman Mistake

**What NOT to Do**:
- Salesman tried to sell $5,000 insurance
- Customer said no
- Kept lowering price until $400 for same product
- **Result**: Customer lost all trust, didn't buy the car either

**Lesson**: Reducing price on the SAME product destroys trust
- ❌ Discounting = reducing price for same product
- ✅ Downselling = adjusting offer AND price together

### The Exchange Principle
If you give something, you must receive something in return:
- Fewer resources
- Less time
- Fewer bonuses
- Longer payment terms

### Three Downsell Formats

#### 1. Payment Plan Downsell
- Change payment structure
- Less now, more later
- Facilitates entry without reducing total value

#### 2. Trial with Penalty
- Paid trial that fixes free trial problems
- Creates initial commitment
- Reduces abandonment

#### 3. Feature Downsell
- Reduce features
- Adjust quantity
- Modify scope
- Fit available budget

### Application Points
Downsells work anywhere:
- At entry (first offer)
- After accepted upsell (maximize value)
- After rejected upsell (recover sale)
- In back-end (retention and continuity)

## MODULE 23: Payment Plan Downsells

### Discovery Moment
First month in business, down to last month's rent money. Prospect couldn't pay anything today but would receive payment on the 1st. Rent was due the 5th. Accepted the deal. Payment came on the 1st. Made rent.

**Insight**: Customers reject offers not because total price is too high, but because present burden is too heavy.

### Core Principles

1. **Payment plans work for ANY value**
2. **They don't make products "cheaper"** - they dilute cost over time
3. **Rejection is about present burden**, not total value

### The 7-Step Framework

#### Step 1: Reward Upfront Payment
- **NEVER penalize** those who can't pay upfront
- **Correct structure**:
  - Present payment plan first ($12,000)
  - Then: "If you pay upfront, save $2,000"
- Customer sees upfront payment as bonus, not punishment

#### Step 2: Third-Party Financing / Credit Cards / Layaway

**External Financing**:
- Company pays you now, customer pays them later
- Can increase revenue up to 35%

**Credit Cards**:
- Reposition: "Would you rather I set the terms or you?"
- If they want control → suggest credit card where they choose duration

**Layaway (Purchase Plan)**:
- Customer pays everything BEFORE receiving product
- Creates anticipation and aligned incentives
- Customer wants to pay fast to receive quickly

#### Step 3: Half Now, Half Later
- Ask: "When do you receive your next paycheck?"
- Adjust timing to:
  - **B2C**: Salary dates
  - **B2B**: Cash flow cycles

#### Step 4: Verify Genuine Interest
**Temperature Check**: "On a scale of 1-10, how much do you want this?"
- **8+**: Continue offering payment options
- **7 or below**: Pivot to feature downsell (adjust product)

#### Step 5: Divide Into Three Payments
- Example: ⅓ now, ⅓ next payment, ⅓ following payment
- **Focus**: Compress maximum within 30 days to maximize initial cash

#### Step 6: Spread Payments Equally
- Example: 6-month plan divided into 3 equal payments
- **Remember**: You pay acquisition and onboarding costs upfront - front-load revenue

#### Step 7: Offer a Free Trial
- Last resort card
- Must be executed strategically (see Module 24)

### Alternative: Seesaw Downselling

**How It Works**:
1. Ask: "Prefer higher or lower monthly payments?"
2. Customer: "Lower"
3. Response: "Then pay more upfront, and monthly payments drop significantly"

**Benefits**:
- Incentivizes larger initial payments
- Reduces future installments
- Flexible for smaller businesses

**When to Use Each**:
- **7 steps**: Large companies, need standardization and CRM control
- **Seesaw**: Small businesses, need flexibility

### Advanced Tips

#### 1. Upsells Within Payments
During the plan, offer the upfront payment discount again

#### 2. Reduce Default Rates
Align billing dates with customer's payment dates (salary, receivables)

#### 3. Avoid Cannibalization
If many upfront payers migrate to plans, adjust strategy

#### 4. Sales Commissions
- Always base on value **received on that day**
- Remaining payments depend on delivery team, not salesperson

#### 5. Recurring Revenue Models
- Present as annual contract ($36,000/year or $3,000/month)
- Allows upfront discount even in subscriptions

## MODULE 24: Free Trials (With Penalty)

### Position in Hierarchy
Free trials are the LAST resort in the downsell ladder. Only offer after customer has gone through all 7 payment plan steps and still says "I can't pay" or "I don't have money now."

### Discovery: The HR Platform Story
Needed HR platform while scaling Gym Launch. Chosen model:
- Company charged $500 upfront
- If we attended 2-3 mandatory trainings → amount refunded (rebate)
- If we didn't attend → company kept the money

**Result**: We attended, learned the software, stayed as customers for years.

**Principle**: Trials aren't really free. They're free under CONDITIONS.

### Trial With Penalty vs. Win Your Money Back

| Model | Mechanics | Result |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Win Your Money Back** | Customer pays → refunded if they comply | Incentive to complete |
| **Trial with Penalty** | Customer doesn't pay → charged if they don't comply | Commitment or compensation |

### Why It Works

✅ If they don't comply → You receive payment
✅ If they comply → Likely long-term customer
✅ Customer internalizes habits and results
✅ Eliminates curious browsers and tourists

### How to Structure Free Trial With Penalty

#### 1. Offer ONLY at the End
- **DO NOT advertise** as main offer
- Use **ONLY after** customer says "no" to paid options
- Keep as **last resort**

#### 2. Always Get Card First
- Collect payment details as "formality to complete registration"
- Eliminates "I didn't bring my card" excuses
- **Pro tip**: You fill out the contract yourself → creates flow and eliminates errors

#### 3. Sell Long-Term Commitment
**Essential Question**: "If this program delivers results, will you stay long-term?"
- If answer is **NO** → Don't offer the trial
- You don't want browsers → Only those with **real intention** to solve the problem

#### 4. Sell Consumption Commitments

**List trial conditions clearly**:
- Attend meetings
- Log progress
- Post in group
- Submit assignments

**Associate small penalties** ($50, $100) with each failure
**Have customer initial/sign next to each clause** → psychological commitment

### Practical Examples

#### B2C Example: "28-Day Kick the Habit"

**Conditions**:
- Weekly consultations
- Post progress in group
- Complete daily app journal
- Attend final sessions

**Penalty**: Fee for each missed requirement

#### B2B Example: "5-Day Get Your First 5 Clients Challenge"

**Conditions**:
- Send 100 outbound messages/day
- Report statistics
- Attend daily training
- Post in group
- Attend final call (upsell opportunity)

**Penalty**: Progressive fee for non-compliance

#### Therapy Example: "28-Day Breakthrough Therapy"

**Conditions**:
- Complete homework before sessions
- Attend all sessions

**Penalty**: $50 per missed session or homework assignment

### What Happens After Trial?

#### If Customer Loved It
- Already set up for **automatic billing**
- You do **upsell** in follow-up meetings
- Happy customer → Higher propensity for larger plans

#### If Customer Hated It
- Ask for feedback, **take responsibility**
- "That was my fault. Let me fix it with a solution that actually makes sense"
- Offer better package → **50% accept**

#### If Customer Didn't Use It
- Use fees as **leverage to re-engage**
- "Come back now, I'll waive all penalties"
- Smarter than charging and generating bad review

### Advanced Tactics

#### 1. Strategic Fee Association
Link fees to what **matters most**:
- Attendance
- Consultations
- Critical tasks

#### 2. Timing of Explanation
Explain fees **AFTER** getting the card → much less resistance

#### 3. Gradual Penalties
Keep fees **small and gradual** → avoid "I already lost, so I quit" effect

#### 4. Special Positioning
Always offer trial as something **special**, not standard → maintains perceived value

#### 5. Strategic Alignment
**Pro tip**: Align check-ins with upsell opportunities

### Key Rules

1. Offer only at the end (not as first option)
2. Always collect card and formalize commitments
3. Use small fees as incentive, not profit center
4. **Goal isn't to profit from penalties** - it's creating engaged customers who buy the next level

## MODULE 25: Feature Downsells

### Core Concept
When customer says "I want it, but it's too expensive," respond:
> "Fine, what if we remove this - does that work for you?"

**Principle**: Remove a feature, lower price, ask: How about now?

### Discovery Story
A friend tripled close rate in one quarter:
- From **25% → 75%**
- No payment plans or discounts
- Strategy: Removed guarantee from $4,000 offer
- Offered same solution for $2,000 **without guarantee**

**Results**:
- Many customers, seeing guarantee value, **upsold themselves** back to expensive plan
- From 25 customers/100 → To **35 in expensive package + 40 in downsell**
- Simple, elegant, powerful

### Three Main Forms

#### 1. Lower Quantity
- Fewer sessions
- Shorter duration
- Less product

#### 2. Lower Quality
- Simpler materials
- Previous versions
- Basic vs. premium service

#### 3. Remove Optional Components
- No guarantee
- No premium support
- No special bonuses

### The Psychology
Each feature has **perceived value**. When you remove it, you change the price vs. value equation. Customer then weighs:
> "Is it worth saving X to lose Y?"

This process makes many customers **prefer to return** to original offer.

### Examples by Category

#### Products
| Original | Downsell |
|----------|----------|
| 3-month supplement | 1-month supplement |
| Leather seats | Vinyl seats |
| Full version | Basic version |

#### Services
| Original | Downsell |
|----------|----------|
| 4 sessions/month | 2 sessions/month |
| Immediate response | 24-hour response |
| Unlimited consulting | Specific time slots |
| In-person | Remote |
| Done-for-you | Do-it-yourself workshop |

#### Guarantees & Insurance
| Original | Downsell |
|----------|----------|
| Lifetime warranty | 1-year warranty |
| Unlimited guarantee | Conditional guarantee |
| Full coverage | Partial coverage |

### Implementation Process

#### 1. First Strategic Cut
- Remove something **very valuable** → reduce price **minimally**
- Customer feels the loss and **returns to original**

#### 2. If Still Resisting
- Remove something **less relevant** → reduce price **significantly**
- Customer feels they got a **good deal**

#### 3. Name Packages Differently

**Naming Options**:
- First Class / Business / Economy
- Premium / Standard / Minimum

**"Minimum" works exceptionally well** - reinforces it's the smallest possible option

#### 4. Temperature Check

If prospect rejects two consecutive changes:
- Ask: "On a scale of 0-10, how much do you still want this?"
- **8+**: Persist with more options
- **Under 8**: Problem isn't price, it's desire

#### 5. Use Barter (Smart Exchange)

If customer asks for larger discount, **trade for something valuable**:
- ✅ Reviews on platforms
- ✅ Video testimonials
- ✅ Daily posts
- ✅ Friend referrals

**Transforms discount into free marketing**

### Important Rules

#### 1. Never Negotiate Price on Same Product
Always associate **value removal** with price reduction

#### 2. Be Guide, Not Pushy Salesperson
**Right Tone**: "Let me find something that works for you"

#### 3. Quantify Your Features
- Often you offer benefits you don't mention
- If you don't measure, you can't reduce clearly

#### 4. Use With Active Customers Too
- If they're not using all features, offer smaller package before they cancel
- Increases **LTV (Lifetime Value)**
- Builds trust

## MODULE 26: Continuity Offers

### The True Magic
Continuity offers are where **real magic happens**. This strategy not only generates significant revenue but also **stabilizes cash flow**.

### Position in Framework
1. Attraction
2. Upsells
3. Downsells
4. **Continuity** ← Where we build predictability

### What Are Continuity Offers?
Offers that deliver **continuous value** in exchange for **recurring payments** - until customer decides to cancel.

### Key Benefits
- Increase profit per customer
- Provide one more thing to sell
- **Fundamental principle**: Sell once, receive multiple times

### The Power Number Example

#### Traditional Model (One-Time Sale)
- $1,000 product to 100 people
- 10 buy → $10,000 revenue
- **Result**: $10,000 now, nothing in future

#### Continuity Model
- $50/month to same 100 people
- 40 buy (4x conversion)
- If they stay 20 months → $1,000 per customer
- **Result**: $2,000 now + $40,000 over time

#### Multiplier Bonus
- **One-time sale**: 10 customers for future upsells
- **Recurring**: 40 customers for future upsells (4x opportunities)

### Why Do Continuity

#### Fundamental Reasons:
1. ✅ More clients enter (lower entry barrier)
2. ✅ LTV (Lifetime Value) remains high
3. ✅ Builds many more future opportunities
4. ✅ Stabilizes cash flow

### Pros and Cons

#### Pros:
- Attracts more customers
- Improves retention
- Creates predictable revenue
- Increases company value

#### Cons:
- Generates less immediate cash
- Difficult to use as initial offer

**Solution**: Position continuity as final pillar. Initial cash from attraction, upsells, downsells; continuity brings future cash.

### Three Key Concepts

#### 1. Downsell the Upsell (Transform Upsell to Continuity)

**Powerful Strategy**: Convert your upsell into recurring format.

**Real Example**:
- $16,000 program for 16 weeks ($1,000/week)
- Continuity conversion: "Want five times more value for less?"
- Result: Converted even more customers

**Psychology**: People think more about monthly flow than total duration. 3-year contract with lower monthly payment is more attractive than 16 intensive weeks, even if total cost is higher.

#### 2. Calculating LTV on a Napkin

**Simple Formula**:
```
Price ÷ Monthly Churn = Average Lifetime Revenue
Lifetime Revenue × Gross Margin = Lifetime Profit
```

**Practical Example**:
- Price: $100/month
- Churn: 10% → LTV ≈ $1,000
- Gross margin: 80%
- Lifetime profit: $800

**Churn Reduction Impact**:
| Churn | LTV | Difference |
|-------|-----|-----------|
| 10% | $1,000 | Base |
| 3% | $3,000 | 3x higher |

> Small churn reductions have massive impact

#### 3. Big Head, Long Tail

**Example**: Tanning salon
- $100 upfront (big head)
- $10/month after (long tail)
- Clients called to ensure they wouldn't lose reduced rate

**Why It Works**:
- Large initial payment covers costs and provides cash flow breathing room
- Small recurring amount keeps customers much longer
- Very low churn

### Recurring vs. Reoccurring Revenue

#### Recurring Revenue
- Fixed scheduled payments
- Examples: Netflix, Spotify
- **Investor Value**: 8-15x EBITDA

#### Reoccurring Revenue
- Repeat purchases without subscription
- Examples: Coca-Cola, Amazon
- **Investor Value**: 3-4x EBITDA

> Both valuable, but contractual recurring commands higher multiples

### Application in Education & Services

#### Common Mistake:
Mixing point-in-time value with continuous value in same price

#### Correct Solution:

**Charge High Upfront For**:
- Annual membership
- Intensive program
- Value already delivered

**Charge Low Monthly For**:
- Ongoing mentorship
- New content and updates
- Community and support

### Personal Note: Greatest Benefit
> **Peace of mind**. Knowing that on the 1st of every month, all bills are paid and profit is already guaranteed changes management completely.

**Continuity offers = stability**

## MODULE 27: Continuity Bonus Offer

### The Discovery Ad
"I'm giving away all my courses for free."

**The Structure**:
- Courses valued at $24,000 if bought individually
- Offer: Get 30 days free of $399/month newsletter
- Just for trying, receive ALL courses

**On No Thank You Page**:
- Pay $299/month today
- Receive all back issues
- Lock in lifetime price

> Critical insight: He never tried selling the $24,000 package. Only used the value as anchor.

### Real Gym Case
Gym owner closing **70-80% of clients** by changing only presentation:

#### Before:
- Sold 6-week challenge for hundreds of dollars
- Low conversion rate

#### After:
- Offered same challenge **free** to recurring members
- Conversion rate skyrocketed

**How He Discovered**: Accident, when client couldn't afford challenge:
> "You know what? If you become a member, I'll give you the challenge for free."

**Result**: More sales, full-price membership, and created goodwill.

### The Bonus Offer Principle

#### Fundamental Rule:
> **Bonus must have higher perceived value than first continuity payment**

#### Practical Examples:
- $1,000 program as bonus → $200/month subscription
- $24,000 courses as bonus → Free trial of $399/month

### Ways to Apply

#### Physical Products

**Single Bonus**:
- $800 in dog toys free
- When signing up for $59/month food delivery

**Recurring Bonus**:
- One new toy per month while member
- Continuous perceived value

#### Services

**Acceleration Program**:
- 6 intensive weeks (value: $1,000) free
- When subscribing for $100/month

**VIP Package**:
- Priority access
- Extended support
- Exclusive community

#### Digital Products

**Content Archive**:
- Past newsletters (value: $15,800) free
- When joining for $399/month

**Combined Offer**:
- Lifetime discount + recurring bonus
- Double incentive to stay

### Key Strategies

#### 1. Sell the Bonus, Not the Continuity

❌ "Join my subscription" → Not attractive
✅ "Get this $5,000 bonus" → Very attractive

Then you explain the continuity.

#### 2. Keep Bonus Relevant

Bonus should connect to main offer:

| ✅ Correct | ❌ Wrong |
|-----------|----------|
| Boxing gloves for boxing gym | T-shirt for IT service |
| Sales course for CRM | Headphones for cooking course |

#### 3. Use Assets You Already Have

Zero-cost assets with high perceived value:
- Content archives
- Past newsletters
- Onboarding programs
- Workshop recordings

#### 4. Give Status as Bonus

Create title hierarchy:
- Silver → Gold → Diamond → Double Diamond
- Clients stay to reach next level
- Works like video games: Easy early levels, then larger gaps

#### 5. Combine With Discounts and Prepayments

- Discount for 6 or 12 months upfront
- "Buy 5 months, get 1 free"
- Just 1 in 8 accepting increases 30-day profit 50%

### The Bonus Offer Mathematics

#### Standalone vs. Continuity Price Test:

| Standalone Price | Result |
|-----------------|-----------|
| **1.33x value** | 50% choose single bonus, 50% continuity |
| **2x value** | 70% choose continuity |
| **3x value** | 90% choose continuity |

> People pay 33% more just to avoid subscription

### Implementation Framework

#### Step 1: Identify Your Anchor Bonus
- Must have high perceived value
- Low or zero marginal cost
- Relevant to audience

#### Step 2: Price Strategically
- Bonus = 3-5x first payment value
- Continuity = Price that sustains business

#### Step 3: Structure the Offer
1. Lead with the bonus
2. Mention it comes "free" with trial
3. Explain continuity as detail

#### Step 4: Add Urgency
- Lifetime discount if acting now
- Bonus limited to first X customers
- Offer valid only this month

### Summary

#### Core Principle:
> **Add good stuff, remove bad stuff**
> Add bonuses, eliminate (costs, fees)

#### Proven Results:
- ✅ Secret is anchoring on bonus
- ✅ Bonus must seem larger than initial cost
- ✅ Continuity comes as "smart way" to get bonus
- ✅ Works for physical, digital, services
- ✅ Scales with bonus + discounts + prepayment

**This is how you maximize adoption and lifetime value (LTV).**

## MODULE 28: Continuity Discount Offer

### Core Concept
> **Get free time in exchange for long-term commitment**

**Classic Example**: "Get 2 months free when you sign up for 1 year."

### Inspirational Story

#### The Young Austin Entrepreneur
Young neighbor sold his waste management company for millions. How he started:

- Maxed out all credit cards
- Asked family for money (nobody helped)
- Was broke

**The Genius Strategy**:
> "I'll give you 1 year of free service if you sign a 5-year contract with me."

- 6 total years: 1 free + 5 paid
- Delivered service without pay for 12 months
- Stole market from competitors
- Sold to national giant

**Lesson**: Forego short-term revenue to close long-term contracts and dominate market.

### How It Works

#### Definition:
> **Give products/services free (or discounted) if customer commits to longer payment period**

#### Practical Examples:
- **Internet**: "First 2 months free, then annual subscription"
- **Gym**: "Train 6 weeks free if you close 12-month plan"
- **Pool cleaning**, storage, rental: All use it

#### Essential Elements:
1. How to apply discount
2. Clear cancellation policy

### 4 Ways to Apply Discount

#### 1. Upfront Discount

**How It Works**:
- Free time first, contract starts after
- Example: 3 months free + 12 months paid = 15 months total

> Common mistake: "3 free + 9 paid" (12 months total). This destroys value!

**Best For Businesses With Guaranteed Enforcement**:
- ✅ Cell carriers (cut service)
- ✅ Self-storage (keep goods)
- ✅ Property managers (evict)
- ✅ Financed cars (repossess vehicle)

⚠️ **Avoid If**: High churn and low collection capacity

#### 2. Backloaded Discount

**How It Works**:
- Promise free time, deliver after complete payment
- "Get 3 months free when you sign 12 months"
- 3 months only come after paying 12 months on time

**Benefits**:
- ✅ Stimulates good payment behavior
- ✅ Customer "earns" their bonus
- ✅ Significantly reduces churn

#### 3. Spread Over Time

**How It Works**:
- Dilute discount value throughout contract

**Practical Example**:
```
Monthly payment: $200
Total discount: $600 (3 months free)
Application: Reduce $50 from each installment
```

**Advantages**:
- Maintains regular cash flow
- Customer continuously perceives benefit
- Protects company cash

#### 4. Via Prepayment

**How It Works**:
- Customer pays first and last month upfront
- Or pays equivalent activation fee

**Benefits**:
- ✅ Guarantees initial cash for CAC
- ✅ Validates payment method
- ✅ Reduces delinquency
- ✅ Customer still gets free months

### Other Creative Variations

#### Gift Card / Voucher

**Structure**:
- Discount in gift card form
- Released after 3 payments

**Possibilities**:
- Customer uses later
- Gives to friend (free referral)
- Forgets (you keep full revenue)

#### Conditional Lifetime Discount

**Amazon Example**:
- 5% off when subscribing
- 15% off after 5 consecutive months

**Benefit**: Pushes customers past critical cancellation barrier

### Cancellation Policies

#### Main Options:

| Policy | Advantage | Disadvantage |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| **Cancel anytime** | Increases sales | Decreases retention |
| **No cancellation** | Increases retention | Discourages sales |
| **Proportional fee** | Balances fairness | Operational complexity |
| **Fixed exit fee** | Simple and clear | May create resistance |

#### Brilliant Tactic:

Use fee as negotiation currency:
> "You can pay the fee... OR give me feedback in an interview and I'll waive the fee."

**Results**:
- Learn from cancellations
- Improve product
- May save sale with upsell

### Revenue Hacks

#### 1. Charge Every 4 Weeks (Not Monthly)

- 1 year = 13 four-week cycles
- **Result**: 8.3% extra revenue without changing anything

#### 2. 3% Processing Fee

**Structure**:
- Include as standard in contract
- Offer waiver if providing 2 payment methods

**Benefits**:
- Less involuntary churn
- More net profit
- Guaranteed payment backup

### Implementation Framework

#### Step 1: Choose Model
Analyze your business:
- High CAC → Spread discount or prepaid
- Strong enforcement → Upfront discount
- Low churn → Backloaded discount

#### Step 2: Define Cancellation Policy
- Clear and transparent
- Aligned with enforcement capacity
- Use as learning tool

#### Step 3: Structure Communication
- Focus on total value, not discount
- "Get X months" works better than "X% off"
- Always add to contract, don't subtract

### Strategic Summary

#### Fundamental Principles:
- ✅ Continuity discount = free time for commitment
- ✅ Upfront discount = more sales, more churn
- ✅ Back-end discount = fewer sales, better customers
- ✅ Spreading discount = perfect balance

#### Advanced Implementations:
- Gift cards and lifetime discounts as reinforcements
- Clear policies reduce problems
- Simple hacks increase profit effortlessly

> **Final Objective**: Not "give stuff away free," but structure smart offers that increase retention, revenue, and profit.

## MODULE 29: Continuity Waive Fee Offer

### The Concept
More direct than continuity bonus and discount. Powerfully effective for those selling services, consulting, or subscription programs requiring onboarding or delivering long-term results.

### Origin Story

#### The $5 Million Consultant

Friend decided to enter consulting market with clear goal:

> **Earn $5M/year, alone, no employees.**

**His Surprising Claims**:
- "I don't lose clients"
- "I don't even need to do marketing"
- "Most are on payment plans"

### The Genius Structure

> "You can pay $25k now and then go month-to-month at $4k. OR you can lock in $4k/month with a 12-month contract. BUT, if you want to leave early, you owe the $25k onboarding fee."

**Result**: Almost everyone closes on annual plan.

**When Someone Wants to Cancel**:
> "No problem, just transfer the $25k fee and I'll close your account now."

> Insight: Model makes customers think twice before quitting during low moments - increases retention without "trapping" anyone.

### How It Works

#### Three Basic Steps:

##### 1. Create Entry Fee
- Onboarding fee of **3-5x monthly payment**
- Example: $1,000/month plan → $5,000 fee

##### 2. Offer Two Options

| Option | Structure | Flexibility |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **A - Flexible** | Pay fee + monthly | Cancel anytime |
| **B - Commitment** | Fee waived with 12-month contract | Pay fee if leaving early |

##### 3. Apply the Rule
- If you complete full contract, fee disappears
- If you break contract, fee is due
- Fair and balanced

### Detailed Practical Example

**Configuration**:
- Plan: $1,000/month
- Setup fee: $5,000

#### Option A - Monthly Flexible
- **Month 1**: $6,000 ($5k fee + $1k monthly)
- **Month 2+**: $1,000/month
- **Benefit**: Cancel whenever

#### Option B - 12-Month Commitment
- **Monthly**: $1,000/month (no initial fee)
- **Condition**: If leaving before 12 months, pay $5,000
- **Benefit**: Save $5,000 if completed

> Almost everyone chooses annual plan to avoid fee

### Why It Works

#### Psychology at Three Moments:

##### 1. At Entry
People sign to avoid the fee

##### 2. During Contract
Fee continues "hanging" like sword of Damocles

##### 3. At Retention
Cost of leaving (paying fee) > Cost of staying (continuing to pay)

Keeps customer during temptation moments to quit.

### Four Variables to Adjust

#### 1. Minimum Contract Duration
- **Ideal**: 12 months or more
- **Minimum viable**: 6 months

#### 2. Monthly With Commitment
- Standard service price
- Can include small discount

#### 3. Monthly Without Commitment
- Can be equal or higher
- Differential creates additional incentive

#### 4. Fee Amount
- **Standard**: 3-5x monthly payment
- **Adjustment**: Higher fee = more commitment adoptions

### How to Justify the Fee

#### Valid Names:
- "Activation fee"
- "Setup fee"
- "Processing fee"
- "Engagement fee"
- "Onboarding fee"

#### Presentation Script:

> **"To deliver you the best experience, I invest much more in new clients than in existing ones. This fee covers that initial cost. But if you commit to staying, I'll absorb that cost for you."**

Simple, transparent, and fair.

### Extra Hacks

#### 1. Proportional Fee to Savings

**How It Works**:
- Customer saves $500/month in contract
- If cancel, pay accumulated difference
- Example: Cancel in month 6 = pay $3,000

#### 2. Fee Reverted to Cause

**Creative Structure**:
1. Ask: "What cause do you hate most?"
2. If cancel early, donate fee to that cause
3. Customer thinks twice before leaving 😅

#### 3. Fee as Future Credit

- Fee becomes credit for additional services
- Only available after completing contract
- Incentivizes retention and upsell

### Best Applications

### Ideal For Businesses With:
- ✅ High onboarding cost
- ✅ Long-term results
- ✅ Consulting services
- ✅ Transformation programs

### Specific Examples:
- **SEO**: Results take months
- **Weight Loss**: Gradual change
- **Investments**: Growth over time
- **Consulting**: Complex implementation

### Implementation Framework

#### Step 1: Calculate Your Costs
- CAC (Acquisition Cost)
- Real onboarding
- Desired margin

#### Step 2: Define Fee
- Minimum: 3x monthly payment
- Ideal: 5x monthly payment
- Test and adjust

#### Step 3: Create Narrative
- Justify fee value
- Explain commitment benefit
- Be transparent

#### Step 4: Structure Contract
- Clear terms
- Defined consequences
- Collection process

### Strategic Summary

#### Fundamental Structure:
- ✅ Waive fee = conditional setup fee
- ✅ Fee = 3-5x monthly payment
- ✅ Pay fee → cancel whenever
- ✅ Sign contract → fee waived
- ✅ Break contract → fee due

#### Proven Benefits:
- Keeps customers during doubt moments
- Increases psychological commitment
- Improves revenue predictability
- Significantly reduces churn

> **Mission Accomplished**: More adoptions, more retention, more business value.
