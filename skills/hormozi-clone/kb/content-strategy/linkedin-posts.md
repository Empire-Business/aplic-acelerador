# Alex Hormozi LinkedIn Content Strategy

## Overview
Alex Hormozi's LinkedIn content follows a direct, value-driven approach that combines business insights with personal stories. His content strategy focuses on practical advice, counterintuitive perspectives, and engaging hooks that drive immediate action.

## Content Pillars

### 1. Business Strategy & Scaling
- **Focus**: Practical frameworks for growing businesses
- **Tone**: Direct, authoritative, data-backed
- **Frequency**: Daily posts with actionable insights
- **Example Topics**:
  - How to scale from $1M to $10M
  - Pricing strategies that work
  - Team building and leadership
  - Operational efficiency

### 2. Personal Development & Mindset
- **Focus**: Mental models for success
- **Tone**: Vulnerable, reflective, motivating
- **Key Themes**:
  - Overcoming failure
  - Discipline and routines
  - Risk-taking
  - Personal growth stories

### 3. Sales & Marketing Tactics
- **Focus**: Specific techniques and scripts
- **Tone**: Educational, practical, copy-pasteable
- **Format**: Lists, frameworks, step-by-step guides
- **Examples**:
  - "3 words to close more sales"
  - "When a customer says 'I need to think about it'"
  - Objection handling templates

### 4. Life Lessons & Philosophy
- **Focus**: Wealth, relationships, purpose
- **Tone**: Philosophical, authentic, sometimes controversial
- **Engagement**: High comment rates due to thought-provoking nature

## Post Structure Templates

### Template 1: The Counterintuitive Hook
```
[Counterintuitive statement that challenges conventional wisdom]

[Context/Setup]

[The traditional approach] ❌
[Why it doesn't work]

[My approach] ✅
[Why it works better]

[Actionable step/implementation]

[Question to drive engagement]
```

**Example**:
```
"Stop selling to scale your business.

Most people think you need to sell more to grow. They're wrong.

The real key: Increase your prices.

Here's why:
- More customers = more operational drag
- Higher prices = better customers, more profit
- Quality over quantity wins every time

Implement this: Review your pricing today.

Question: What's holding you back from raising prices?"
```

### Template 2: The Listicle
```
[Number] [Adjective] [Things/Strategies/Ways] to [Achieve Result]

[Intro sentence about the problem]

1. [First point with brief explanation]
   [Implementation detail]

2. [Second point with brief explanation]
   [Implementation detail]

[Continue to 3-7 items]

[Closing insight]

[CTA or question]
```

**Example**:
```
5 Things to Help People Understand What You're Saying

Communication is leverage. Here's how to improve instantly:

1. Remove adverbs
   Words like "really," "very," "extremely" add confusion.

2. Use active voice
   "A business is valued by" → "We value a business this way"

3. Shorter words and sentences
   Replace colons with periods. Start new sentences.

4. Use simple vocabulary
   "Multiple sources" → "Lots of stuff"

5. Speak with purpose
   Every word should earn its place.

Save this post. Use it next time you write something.
```

### Template 3: The Story Framework
```
[Compelling opening/hook]

[Context - where/when this happened]

[The problem/challenge]

[What most people do] ❌

[What I did] ✅
[Specific actions taken]

[The result - with numbers]

[The lesson/insight]

[How to apply it]

[Engagement question]
```

**Example**:
```
"I had $0 to my name and $50k in monthly expenses.

This was 2013. I had just lost everything.

Most people would have:
- Taken a job
- Given up on entrepreneurship
- Played it safe

I did something different:
1. Cut all personal expenses to $1,500/month
2. Slept in my gym to save rent
3. Worked 16-hour days for 6 months

Result: Built back to $50k/month in 8 months.

The lesson: You can be resourceful when you choose to be.

Action: Function like you have to make payroll tomorrow.

What would you do if your back was against the wall?"
```

### Template 4: The "Why" Post
```
[Controversial or contrarian statement]

[Why most people believe X]

[Why they're wrong]

[What I've learned from experience]

[The truth/principle]

[How to apply it]

[Question]
```

**Example**:
```
"Why I give away all my best knowledge for free.

Most entrepreneurs think:
- 'I need to protect my secrets'
- 'If I share it, competitors will copy'
- 'I should save it for my paid course'

They're wrong.

Here's what I've learned:
- The more I give, the more I get back
- Execution > information always wins
- 1% will execute, 99% will just consume

The truth: Generosity is a business strategy.

How to apply:
1. Share your frameworks freely
2. Help everyone you can
3. The right people will find you

Who's helped you for free that changed your life?"
```

## Content Voice & Tone

### Characteristics
1. **Direct & Blunt**: No fluff, straight to the point
2. **Data-Driven**: Uses numbers, percentages, specific results
3. **Vulnerable**: Shares failures and struggles openly
4. **Controversial**: Takes strong stances, challenges assumptions
5. **Action-Oriented**: Every post has a clear takeaway

### Language Patterns
- Short sentences
- Punchy paragraphs
- Numbers and statistics
- Emojis used sparingly for emphasis ✅❌💰
- Direct address ("you")
- Imperative verbs ("do this," "stop that")

## Engagement Strategies

### 1. Comment Drivers
- Ask specific questions at the end
- Take controversial positions
- Share unpopular opinions
- Use "either/or" questions
- Request experiences/stories

### 2. Share Triggers
- Unexpected insights
- Counterintuitive advice
- Specific frameworks people can save
- Personal stories with emotional hooks
- Copy-paste templates

### 3. Save Triggers
- Numbered lists
- Step-by-step guides
- Frameworks and models
- Before/after examples
- Scripts and templates

## Optimization Tactics

### Timing
- **Best times**: Morning (7-9 AM), lunch (12-1 PM), evening (6-8 PM)
- **Frequency**: 1-2 posts per day
- **Consistency**: Same times daily builds anticipation

### Visual Elements
- Text-only posts perform best for organic reach
- Use line breaks for readability
- Keep paragraphs under 3 lines
- Use bullet points for lists
- Strategic use of ALL CAPS for emphasis

### Length Strategy
- **Short posts**: 50-150 words (quick insights)
- **Medium posts**: 150-300 words (frameworks, stories)
- **Long posts**: 300-600 words (detailed guides, deep dives)

## Content Repurposing Strategy

### From YouTube → LinkedIn
1. Extract key insights from videos
2. Turn into 3-5 micro-posts
3. Create a framework post
4. Share the video link in comments

### From Twitter → LinkedIn
1. Expand on tweet topics
2. Add more context and examples
3. Use the listicle format
4. Link back to original tweet thread

### From Interviews → LinkedIn
1. Pull out key quotes
2. Add your commentary
3. Turn into lessons learned
4. Share behind-the-scenes context

## Performance Indicators

### Metrics to Track
- **Engagement rate**: (likes + comments + shares) / impressions
- **Comment quality**: Length, depth of responses
- **Share rate**: Viral coefficient
- **Save rate**: Long-term value indicator
- **Follower growth**: Organic from content

### Benchmark Targets
- **Engagement rate**: 5-10% (excellent)
- **Comment rate**: 2-5% (very good)
- **Share rate**: 1-3% (good)
- **Save rate**: 1-2% (excellent)
