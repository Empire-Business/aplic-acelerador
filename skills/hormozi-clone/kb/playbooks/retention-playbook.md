# $100M Retention Playbook - Alex Hormozi

## Framework Overview

**Retention is the most profitable lever in business.** Increasing retention from 10% monthly churn to 3% monthly churn increases LTV by 3.3×. This playbook provides systematic frameworks for keeping customers longer, dramatically increasing lifetime value, and turning one-time buyers into raving fans.

**Core Insight:** It costs 5-25× more to acquire a new customer than to keep an existing one. Yet most businesses obsess over acquisition while neglecting retention. This is the profit killer hiding in plain sight.

## Key Concepts and Principles

### The Mathematics of Retention

**Churn Rate Impact:**
```
10% Monthly Churn:
- Average customer lifespan: 10 months
- $100/month × 10 months = $1,000 LTV

3% Monthly Churn:
- Average customer lifespan: 33 months
- $100/month × 33 months = $3,300 LTV

Result: 3.3× increase in lifetime value from same customer
```

**The Retention Multiplier Effect:**
- Lower churn = higher LTV
- Higher LTV = higher CAC you can afford
- Higher CAC = more aggressive growth
- More growth = economies of scale
- Scale = competitive advantage

### The Five Horsemen of Retention

Hormozi's five retention strategies that alone can reduce churn by 50%+:

**1. Track Attendance Religiously**
- Monitor who shows up vs. who pays
- Contact absentees immediately
- Attendance predicts churn perfectly
- Zero attendance = churn imminent

**2. Reach Out 2× Per Week Minimum**
- Personal touch points
- Value delivery between sessions
- Relationship building
- Presence prevents leaving

**3. Send Handwritten Cards**
- Thank you notes
- Birthday cards
- Achievement recognition
- Physical mail stands out

**4. Host Member Events**
- Community building
- Make friends with other members
- Social glue keeps people paying
- People don't leave friends

**5. Exit Interviews When They Cancel**
- Learn why they leave
- Fix systemic issues
- Sometimes win them back
- Always gain insight

## The Nine-Step Churn Reduction System

### 1. Activation Points

**Definition:** Moment when customer experiences first value
**Why it matters:** Activated users churn 5× less than non-activated

**Implementation:**
- Identify your activation event
- For fitness: First workout completed
- For SaaS: First key feature used
- For course: First module completed
- For service: First deliverable received

**Tactics:**
- Guide to activation within 7 days
- Remove all friction
- Celebrate when achieved
- Make it impossible to miss

### 2. Onboarding Optimization

**The First 7 Days Rule:**
- Most churn happens in first week
- If they survive 30 days, they usually stay long-term
- Focus disproportionate energy here

**Onboarding Checklist:**
- [ ] Welcome call/video from founder
- [ ] Clear roadmap of what to expect
- [ ] Quick wins in first 48 hours
- [ ] Introduction to community
- [ ] Success buddy/partner assigned
- [ ] First milestone tracked

### 3. Incentive Alignment

**Problem:** Misaligned incentives cause churn
**Solution:** Align your success with their success

**Examples:**
- Only bill contingent on results
- Performance-based pricing
- Long-term contracts with renewal incentives
- Bonuses for customer achievements

### 4. Community Linking

**The Principle:** People don't leave friends

**Implementation:**
- Private Facebook group/Discord/Slack
- In-person events
- Accountability partners
- Small group cohorts
- Member spotlights

**Psychology:**
- Social accountability
- Fear of missing out (FOMO)
- Identity tied to group
- Relationships > product

### 5. Fire Bad Customers

**Counter-intuitive Truth:** Some customers cost you money

**Characteristics of Fire-able Customers:**
- Complainers who never implement
- Always want refunds/discounts
- Toxic to community
- Drain team energy
- Don't do the work

**Cost Analysis:**
- One bad customer destroys team morale
- Negative reviews hurt acquisition
- Support costs exceed revenue
- Opportunity cost of serving them instead of good customers

**How to Fire:**
- "This isn't a good fit"
- Refund proactively
- End relationship gracefully
- Protect your team and community

### 6. Annual Pricing

**The Data:** Annual customers churn 2-3× less than monthly

**Why:**
- More commitment required
- Higher friction to cancel
- Skin in the game
- Identity formed

**Implementation:**
- Default to annual billing
- Offer 1-2 months free for annual
- Make monthly the expensive option
- Payment plans for annual if needed

### 7. Cancellation Calls

**The Most Underrated Retention Tactic:**

**Process:**
1. Customer requests cancellation
2. Schedule immediate call
3. Understand why (not always price)
4. Address root cause
5. Win back OR let go gracefully

**Results:**
- 30-50% save rate
- Learn why people leave
- Fix systemic issues
- Sometimes restructure offer

**Script:**
```
"I noticed you requested to cancel. Before we process that,
can I ask what happened? We're constantly improving and
I want to understand what went wrong so we can fix it."

[Listen carefully]

"Based on what you said, would [solution] address this?
Or is this truly not the right time?"
```

### 8. NPS Surveys

**Net Promoter Score Question:**
"How likely are you to recommend us to a friend? (0-10)"

**Scoring:**
- 9-10: Promoters (your advocates)
- 7-8: Passive (satisfied but not excited)
- 0-6: Detractors (at risk of leaving)

**Follow-up Questions:**
- For promoters: "What would you tell someone?"
- For passives: "What would make it a 10?"
- For detractors: "What went wrong? How can we fix it?"

**Frequency:**
- After 30 days
- Quarterly thereafter
- After major interactions
- Before renewal decisions

### 9. Customer Journey Mapping

**Map Every Touchpoint:**
- First awareness
- Sales process
- Purchase
- Onboarding
- First value moment
- Ongoing experience
- Renewal moments
- Potential exit points

**Identify:**
- Where do people drop off?
- What causes frustration?
- What creates delight?
- Where are communication gaps?

**Optimize:**
- Remove friction points
- Add surprise delights
- Improve clarity
- Enhance communication

## The Churn Calculation

### How to Calculate Your Churn Rate

**Monthly Churn Rate:**
```
Churn Rate = (Customers Lost ÷ Total Customers) × 100

Example:
- Started with 1,000 customers
- Lost 100 customers
- Churn Rate = (100 ÷ 1,000) = 10%
```

**Annual Churn Rate:**
```
Don't just multiply monthly × 12
Use: 1 - (1 - Monthly Churn)^12

Example 10% monthly:
1 - (0.90)^12 = 1 - 0.28 = 72% annual churn
```

**Customer Lifetime:**
```
Average Lifetime = 1 ÷ Churn Rate

10% monthly churn:
1 ÷ 0.10 = 10 months average lifetime

3% monthly churn:
1 ÷ 0.03 = 33 months average lifetime
```

**Lifetime Value:**
```
LTV = (Monthly Revenue × Lifetime) - Acquisition Cost

Example:
$100/month × 33 months - $500 CAC = $2,800 LTV
```

## Warning Signs of Churn

### Behavioral Red Flags

**Immediate Action Required:**
- Stops attending/using
- Unresponsive to communication
- Negative feedback in surveys
- Payment failures
- Complaints to support

**Monitor Closely:**
- Reduced engagement
- Login frequency declining
- Community participation drops
- Doesn't respond to outreach

### Operational Red Flags

**System Issues:**
- Bugs or technical problems
- Deliverables late/missing
- Quality declining
- Support response times slow
- Communication gaps

**Fix Immediately:**
- Root cause analysis
- Communicate transparently
- Make it right for customers
- Prevent future occurrences
- Compensate for inconvenience

## Recovery Strategies

### Save-At-Risk Customers

**Step 1: Identify**
- Use your warning signs
- Look at engagement metrics
- Check payment issues
- Review feedback

**Step 2: Reach Out**
- Personal contact (call > email)
- Express genuine concern
- Ask how to help
- Don't sell, serve

**Step 3: Address Issues**
- Fix their specific problems
- Remove friction
- Add resources
- Show you care

**Step 4: Follow Up**
- Ensure resolution
- Monitor engagement
- Prevent future issues
- Deepen relationship

### Win-Back Cancelled Customers

**Timing:**
- 30-60 days after cancellation
- Not immediate (gives them space)
- Not too late (still remember you)

**Message:**
```
"Hey [Name], noticed you left a few months ago.
We've made some improvements based on feedback and
thought of you. Would you be open to seeing what's new?"

[Mention 2-3 specific improvements]
[Offer incentive to return]
```

**Incentives:**
- First month free or discounted
- New bonuses not available before
- Special "welcome back" offer
- Ask what would make them return

## Measuring Success

### Key Retention Metrics

**Leading Indicators (Predictive):**
- Attendance/participation rate
- Engagement score
- Community activity
- Support ticket patterns
- Feature usage

**Lagging Indicators (Results):**
- Monthly churn rate
- Annual churn rate
- Average customer lifetime
- Lifetime value
- Referral rate

**Target Benchmarks:**
- Monthly churn: <5% (excellent), <10% (good)
- Annual retention: >80% (excellent), >70% (good)
- Average lifetime: 12+ months
- LTV: 3× acquisition cost minimum

### The Retention Scorecard

**Calculate Your Score:**
1. Activation rate: % who hit first value moment (Target: 80%+)
2. 30-day retention: % still active after 1 month (Target: 70%+)
3. 90-day retention: % still active after 3 months (Target: 60%+)
4. NPS score: Promoters - Detractors (Target: 50+)
5. Engagement score: % active in last 30 days (Target: 80%+)

**Grading:**
- 450-500: Exceptional
- 400-449: Excellent
- 350-399: Good
- 300-349: Fair
- Below 300: Needs work
