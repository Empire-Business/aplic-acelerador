# $100M Branding Playbook - Alex Hormozi

## Framework Overview

**Most branding advice is complete BS.** "Get your name out there" and "build awareness" are vague, expensive, and ineffective. This playbook teaches a measurable, systematic approach to branding that actually drives business results.

**Core Insight:** Brand is the deliberate pairing of your business with things your customers already like. It's measured by three metrics: Reach (how many know you), Influence (how many act on your behalf), and Direction (where you're taking them).

---

## Key Concepts and Principles

### What Brand Actually Is

**Definition:**
```
Brand = The deliberate pairing of your business with
        things your customers already like
```

**Examples:**
- Nike pairs with athletes, winning, performance
- Apple pairs with creativity, design, innovation
- Your business pairs with [what?]

**Not:** Your logo, colors, or slogan (those are just expressions)

**The Three-Part Measurement:**

1. **Reach** - How many people know you exist?
   - Measured by: followers, email list, traffic, recognition

2. **Influence** - How many people act on your behalf?
   - Measured by: referrals, mentions, word-of-mouth

3. **Direction** - What are you known for?
   - Measured by: clarity of positioning, category dominance

### The Brand Value Equation

```
Brand Value = Reach × Influence × Direction
```

**Increase any one → Brand grows**
**Increase all three → Brand explodes**

**Example:**
```
Small Business:
- Reach: 1,000 people know you
- Influence: 10% refer others (100 people)
- Direction: Clear (3 specific things)
Brand Value: 1,000 × 100 × 3 = 300,000

Strong Brand:
- Reach: 100,000 people know you
- Influence: 30% refer others (30,000 people)
- Direction: Clear (3 specific things)
Brand Value: 100,000 × 30,000 × 3 = 9 billion
```

## Step-by-Step Brand Building Process

### Phase 1: Define Your Ideal Customer (Week 1)

**Step 1: Be Specific About Who You Serve**

**Wrong:**
- "Small business owners"
- "People who want to get fit"
- "Anyone who needs marketing"

**Right:**
- "Gym owners with 1-3 locations doing $500K-$2M revenue"
- "Busy professionals 35-55 who want to lose 15lbs in 90 days"
- "B2B SaaS founders with $1M-$10M ARR raising Series B"

**Why specificity matters:**
- Easier to reach them
- Clearer messaging
- Higher conversion
- Stronger influence (they talk to each other)

**Step 2: Understand What They Already Like**

Your customers already:
- Read certain publications
- Follow certain influencers
- Attend certain events
- Buy certain products
- Have certain values

**Exercise:**
1. List 20 things your ideal customer already likes
2. Find patterns (what do they have in common?)
3. Those patterns are your brand pairing opportunities

### Phase 2: Pair Your Business with What They Like (Weeks 2-4)

**The Pairing Framework:**

```
[Your Business] + [What They Already Like] = Brand Association
```

**Examples:**

**Gym Launch:**
- Pair with: Business growth, freedom, wealth building
- NOT just: Fitness, health, working out

**Acquisition.com:**
- Pair with: ROI, scaling, wealth acceleration
- NOT just: Investing, business consulting

**Your Brand:**
- [Your product] + [What they value] = [Your brand positioning]

**The Three Layers of Pairing:**

**Layer 1: Content Pairing**
- What topics do you create content about?
- What values do you express?
- What perspectives do you share?

**Layer 2: Visual/Experiential Pairing**
- What does your brand look/feel like?
- What's the aesthetic?
- What's the vibe?

**Layer 3: Partnership Pairing**
- Who do you collaborate with?
- What brands do you associate with?
- What events do you sponsor/attend?

### Phase 3: Maximize Reach (Weeks 5-8)

**The Reach Multipliers:**

**1. Content Volume**
- More content = more reach
- Quantity over quality initially
- Improve quality over time

**Target:**
- 1 post/day minimum on primary platform
- 3-5 posts/day if you can manage

**2. Platform Expansion**
- Start with 1 platform (dominate it)
- Add second platform (repurpose)
- Add third, etc.

**Order matters:**
- Pick where your customers already are
- Not where you want to be or think is cool

**3. Paid Amplification**
- Organic reach has limits
- Paid accelerates everything
- Start small, scale what works

**Budget Rule:**
- Start with $10-50/day
- Double down on winners
- Kill losers immediately

**4. Partnership Reach**
- Borrow other people's audiences
- Podcasts, collaborations, guest posts
- Faster than building from scratch

**Goal:**
- 1 podcast appearance/week minimum
- Build relationships over time

### Phase 4: Amplify Influence (Ongoing)

**The Influence Equation:**
```
Influence = (Satisfaction × Remarkability) ÷ Effort to Share
```

**Increase satisfaction:**
- Overdeliver on results
- Better customer experience
- Responsive support

**Increase remarkability:**
- Do things worth talking about
- Create surprises and delights
- Take stands (have opinions)

**Decrease sharing effort:**
- Make it easy to refer
- Provide shareable content
- Create referral incentives

**The Influence Multipliers:**

**1. Create Share-Worthy Content**
```
Shareable = (Valuable + Controversial + Entertaining)
```

- Valuable: Teaches something useful
- Controversial: Takes a stand
- Entertaining: Actually enjoyable

**2. Make Referrals Easy**
- Referral links/codes
- Template messages
- Incentive programs
- Public recognition

**3. Build Community**
- Private groups
- Member events
- Peer-to-peer connections
- Identity formation

**When people make friends with other customers:**
- They stay longer (retention ↑)
- They refer more (influence ↑)
- They defend your brand (advocacy)

### Phase 5: Clarify Direction (Ongoing)

**Direction = What You're Known For**

**The Rule of Three:**
```
Your brand should be known for exactly 3 things.

No more. No less.
```

**Examples:**

**Nike:**
1. Athletic performance
2. Winning
3. Just do it attitude

**Apple:**
1. Beautiful design
2. Innovation
3. Creative professionals

**Hormozi:**
1. Practical business advice
2. No-BS approach
3. Proven results

**Your Three Things:**
1. ___________________
2. ___________________
3. ___________________

**Test:**
- Can you explain in 3 words?
- Would customers say the same 3?
- Is everything you do aligned with those 3?

**If not → Simplify**

## The Brand Bouquet Metaphor

Hormozi uses the "bouquet" metaphor for brand management:

**Your Brand is a Bouquet of Flowers:**

Each flower = One association you create

**Good bouquet:**
- Roses (red, romance)
- Lilies (white, purity)
- Baby's breath (delicate accents)
- **Result: Cohesive, beautiful, memorable**

**Bad bouquet:**
- Cactus (spiky, dangerous)
- Dandelion (weed, invasive)
- Rotting vegetables (gross)
- **Result: Confusing, repelling, forgettable**

**Principle:**
- Every piece of content adds a flower to your bouquet
- Every partnership adds a flower
- Every product adds a flower
- **Question: Are you adding the right flowers?**

**Audit Your Current Bouquet:**
- List 10 associations people have with your brand
- Are they intentional or accidental?
- Are they consistent or contradictory?
- Are they attractive or repelling?

## Hormozi's Branding Frameworks

### The "Be Unlikeable" Framework

**Most brands try to be liked by everyone. Result: Liked by no one.**

**Better approach: Be polarizing**

**Why:**
- People who love you → Raving fans (high influence)
- People who hate you → Still talking about you (reach)
- People who are indifferent → Problem (they ignore you)

**Hormozi's approach:**
- Swears occasionally
- Controversial opinions
- Calls out BS directly
- Doesn't sugarcoat

**Result:**
- Some people hate him (fine)
- Many people love him (great)
- NO ONE is indifferent (winning)

**Application:**
- What's your controversial opinion?
- What do you believe that others don't?
- What will you take a stand on?

### The Consistency Framework

**Brand = Pattern Recognition**

Humans identify patterns. Be consistent and you become a brand.

**Consistency Dimensions:**

**1. Visual Consistency**
- Same colors/fonts
- Same style of graphics
- Same aesthetic
- Same quality level

**2. Voice Consistency**
- Same tone/style
- Same phrases/language
- Same perspective
- Same personality

**3. Value Consistency**
- Same type of insights
- Same quality of advice
- Same philosophical approach
- Same standards

**4. Frequency Consistency**
- Same posting schedule
- Same content types
- Same reliability
- Same presence

**The Compound Effect:**
```
1 great post = Good
100 great posts = Growing brand
1,000 great posts = Unignorable brand
10,000 great posts = Industry leader
```

**Most quit at 10-50 posts.** That's why they don't build a brand.

### The "Enemy" Framework

**Every brand needs an enemy.**

**The enemy isn't a person** (that's petty and unprofessional)

**The enemy is:**
- A wrong way of doing things
- A common mistake people make
- An outdated approach
- A systemic problem

**Examples:**

**Hormozi's enemies:**
- "Gurus" who teach but haven't done it
- Businesses that charge for value they don't deliver
- People who overcomplicate simple things
- Mediocrity and laziness

**Why enemies work:**
- Creates contrast (you vs. them)
- Rallying point for your tribe
- Shows what you stand against
- Makes positioning clear

**Your enemy:**
```
We fight [enemy]
by doing [your solution]
for [your people]
```

## Measuring Brand Success

### The Three Metrics

**1. Reach Metrics:**
- Total followers/subscribers
- Email list size
- Monthly unique visitors
- Brand searches (how many search for you by name)
- Mentions (how many talk about you)

**2. Influence Metrics:**
- Referral rate (% of new customers from referrals)
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)
- Social sharing rate
- User-generated content
- Community engagement

**3. Direction Metrics:**
- Brand association clarity (survey people)
- Positioning strength (what are you known for?)
- Category ownership (do you own a word/concept?)
- Differentiation (how distinct from competitors?)

### The Brand Strength Scorecard

**Rate each 1-10:**

**Reach:**
- [ ] follower growth rate
- [ ] content reach
- [ ] brand awareness
- [ ] media coverage
- [ ] partnership success

**Influence:**
- [ ] referral rate
- [ ] NPS score
- [ ] social mentions
- [ ] community activity
- [ ] advocacy behavior

**Direction:**
- [ ] positioning clarity
- [ ] category ownership
- [ ] differentiation
- [ ] consistency
- [ ] recognition

**Score interpretation:**
- 40-50: World-class brand
- 35-39: Strong brand
- 30-34: Good brand
- 25-29: Developing brand
- <25: Weak brand

## Common Brand Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Trying to Be Liked by Everyone

**Problem:**
- Bland, generic content
- No clear positioning
- Forgettable

**Solution:**
- Polarize intentionally
- Take strong stands
- Accept that some will hate you

### Mistake 2: Inconsistency

**Problem:**
- Post sporadically
- Change voice/style constantly
- No recognizable pattern

**Solution:**
- Post daily minimum
- Stick to one voice
- Build pattern recognition

### Mistake 3: No Clear Direction

**Problem:**
- Known for nothing specific
- Confusing positioning
- Me-too brand

**Solution:**
- Choose 3 things to be known for
- Repeat them constantly
- Delete everything else

### Mistake 4: Building in Wrong Places

**Problem:**
- Focused on platform YOU like
- Not where CUSTOMERS are

**Solution:**
- Go where customers already are
- Fish where fish are
- Don't try to create new markets

### Mistake 5: Branding Without Business

**Problem:**
- Pretty logo but no sales
- Lots of followers but no customers
- Vanity metrics

**Solution:**
- Brand serves business
- Focus on revenue first
- Build brand from profits

## Hormozi's Brand Philosophies

**On What Brand Is:**
> "Brand is the deliberate pairing of your business with things your customers already like. It's not your logo. It's not your colors. It's what you stand for."

**On Reach:**
> "Most people quit too early. 50 posts isn't building a brand. 1,000 posts is getting started. 10,000 posts is when you become unignorable."

**On Influence:**
> "The best marketing is a happy customer who tells their friends. Focus on creating remarkable experiences worth talking about."

**On Direction:**
> "Be known for three things. No more, no less. If you can't explain your brand in three words, you don't have a brand."

**On Polarization:**
> "Some people will hate you. Good. At least they're paying attention. The opposite of love isn't hate, it's indifference. Never be indifferent."

**On Consistency:**
> "Brand is pattern recognition. Show up the same way, in the same places, with the same value, every single day. Eventually, people can't ignore you."

1. **Know exactly who you serve** (specificity wins)
2. **Pair with what they already like** (don't reinvent)
3. **Show up consistently forever** (pattern recognition)
4. **Be worth talking about** (remarkable experiences)
5. **Accept that some will hate you** (polarization)

**The formula:**
```
Strong Brand = (Reach × Influence × Direction) × Time
```

**Most businesses fail at branding because:**
- They try to be liked by everyone → Liked by no one
- They quit too early → No pattern recognition
- They're boring → Nothing worth talking about
- They're inconsistent → No brand, just content
