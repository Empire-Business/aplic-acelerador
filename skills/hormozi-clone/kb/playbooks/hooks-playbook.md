# $100M Hooks Playbook - Alex Hormozi

## Framework Overview

The Hooks Playbook is the foundation of all marketing success. It teaches that **hooks determine 80% of your marketing ROI** - if you don't capture attention in the first 5 seconds, nothing else matters. This isn't about creativity; it's about a scientific, data-driven system for testing and optimizing the critical opening moments of every piece of content.

**Core Insight:** You can 5x, 10x, or even 100x your results by changing ONLY the hook - same product, same offer, same price. Dean Graziosi proved this by transforming a failed infomercial into millions in sales by changing just TWO opening sentences.

## Key Concepts and Principles

### The Hook Equation

```
Effective Hook = Call Out + Value Condition
```

**Call Out:** Something that makes the prospect say "this is for me"
- Activates the "cocktail party effect" (like hearing your name in a noisy room)
- Examples: "Local business owners," "If you're tired of..."

**Value Condition:** A promise of value IF they consume
- Justifies the investment of attention
- Examples: "...read this," "...let me show you how"

### The 8 Verbal Hook Types

1. **Labels** - "Local business owners..."
2. **Questions** - Yes/No or Open-ended
3. **Conditionals** - "If you're X, then Y"
4. **Commands** - "Read this if..."
5. **Statements** - "The smartest thing..."
6. **Lists/Steps** - "7 ways to..."
7. **Narratives** - "One day I was..."
8. **Exclamations** - "Ahhhhh..." (emotional reaction)

### Non-Verbal Hooks

Visual and auditory pattern interrupts that work BEFORE words:
- Catching a banana mid-air
- Dropping a plate (sound of breaking)
- Unusual props
- Sudden movements
- Surprising images

**Key Principle:** The most powerful hooks combine verbal + non-verbal elements.

## The 70-20-10 Innovation Framework

Borrowed from Google's proven innovation strategy:

```
70% CORE (Proven Winners)
├─ Your top performing hooks
├─ "More of what works"
└─ Guarantees baseline performance

20% EMERGING (Winner Adjacent)
├─ Concepts from other niches
├─ Promising adaptations
└─ Moderate risk, good upside

10% EXPERIMENTS (Moonshots)
├─ Radical new ideas
├─ High risk, breakthrough potential
└─ Source of future 70% winners
```

**Why This Works:** Mathematical optimization. Not too conservative (stagnation), not too aggressive (instability). Perfect balance of reliability and innovation.

## Step-by-Step Implementation

### Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1)

**Step 1: Hook Mining (Day 1-2)**
- Visit YouTube, Instagram, TikTok
- Search your niche keywords
- Copy EXACT first 5 seconds of top 50 videos/posts
- Document in spreadsheet with view counts

**Step 2: Categorization (Day 3)**
- Classify each hook by type (Question/Statement/etc)
- Identify patterns in top performers
- Note which categories appear most often

**Step 3: First Test Batch (Day 4-7)**
- Use ALL 50 collected hooks with your content
- Track performance religiously
- Identify your top 10% and bottom 10%

### Phase 2: System Implementation (Weeks 2-4)

**Daily Routine (70 minutes total):**
- **Morning (10 min):** Review yesterday's top performers
- **Create (30 min):** Generate 20 new hook variations
- **Test (20 min):** Publish across platforms
- **Evening (10 min):** Document results

**Weekly Review (1 hour):**
- Rank all hooks by performance
- Separate into top/bottom/middle 10%
- Update 70-20-10 allocations
- Plan next week's mix

### Phase 3: Scale & Optimize (Month 2+)

**Build Your Hook Bank:**
- Create searchable database
- Tag by category, platform, performance
- Include swipe notes on WHY it worked

**Systematize:**
- Write SOPs for team
- Create templates for each hook type
- Automate tracking where possible

**Cross-Platform Adaptation:**
- Test winning hooks on different platforms
- Adapt format, keep core message
- Document platform-specific patterns

## Examples and Templates

### Proven Hook Templates

**The "Mirror Question" (Emotional Connection)**
```
"Have you ever looked in the mirror and thought 'I should be further ahead by now?'"
```
Why works: Universal private pain creates instant identification

**The "Pattern Interrupt" (Curiosity)**
```
"You might be wondering why I just caught a banana..."
```
Why works: Brains cannot ignore unexpected visual + explanation setup

**The "Specific Number" (Credibility)**
```
"$4,664 per month... that's what Kyle, the LAST person on the leaderboard, built"
```
Why works: Specificity = truthiness, removes "I can't do it" objection

**The "Command" (Authority)**
```
"Read this if you're tired of being broke"
```
Why works: Direct + qualifies audience immediately

**The "Confession" (Vulnerability)**
```
"I have a confession... [truthful admission]"
```
Why works: Vulnerability builds trust, curiosity demands resolution

### Email Subject Line Hooks

- "SHHHHH It's a SURPRISE!!"
- "Btw... (I have a favor to ask)"
- "We made a boo boo"
- "Your first goodie (for affiliates only)"

### Video Hook Structure (First 5 Seconds)

```
[0-1s] Visual Pattern Interrupt (do something unexpected)
[1-2s] Verbal Hook Setup ("You might be wondering...")
[2-4s] Value Promise ("...and the reason is...")
[4-5s] Transition into content
```

## Hormozi's Voice and Style

**Signature Opening Patterns:**
- "Real quick question..."
- "You guys want to hear something completely insane?"
- "How to get ahead of 99% of people"
- "I f&cking guarantee you that..."

**Anti-Patterns He Avoids:**
- Talking about himself in the opening
- Vague promises without specifics
- Corporate language or jargon
- Being "creative" for creativity's sake

**Core Philosophy:**
> "Make them feel important, not you"

The fatal mistake Dean Graziosi made with Larry King was focusing on himself ("Tonight I'm here with multi-millionaire Dean Graziosi"). The winning hook focused on the viewer ("Have you ever looked in the mirror and thought...").

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: All hooks perform similarly**
- **Solution:** They're not different enough. Make variations more extreme.

**Problem: CTR stays below 1%**
- **Solution:** Product/offer issue, not hooks. Survey audience.

**Problem: Performance is random/unpredictable**
- **Solution:** Tracking is broken. Fix attribution first.

**Problem: Can't come up with new ideas**
- **Solution:** You're not mining enough. Copy more from other niches.

**Problem: Team resistant to testing so much**
- **Solution:** Show Dean Graziosi case study. ROI proves the work.

## The 10 Biggest Insights

1. **The Hook Is 80% of Value** - Ogilvy's "80 cents" rule is literal, not figurative
2. **Let Data Teach** - Ignore opinions, even experts. Numbers don't lie.
3. **Hook Dictates Content** - Never create content first, then title. Always reverse.
4. **10x Testing Minimum** - For ads, 10 hooks per creative. For long content, 3-4.
5. **Cocktail Party Effect** - Specific call-outs cut through noise like your name at a party
6. **70% Should Be Boring** - Repetition of winners beats creativity every time
7. **Make Them Important** - Every failed hook focuses on the creator. Every winner on the viewer.
8. **Mile Wide, Inch Deep** - Balance specificity (this is for me) with scale (lots of people)
9. **Everything Else Is Waste** - Without the hook, best product/copy/offer = zero results
10. **It's a System, Not Art** - Anyone can do this with documentation and testing

## Famous Case Studies

### The Larry King Infomercial

**Before (Failed):** "Tonight on Larry King Live, I'm here with multi-millionaire bestselling author Dean Graziosi..."
Result: Zero sales across multiple channels

**After (Millions):** "Have you ever looked in the mirror and thought 'I should be further ahead by now?'"
Result: Millions of books sold, changed his life forever

**Lesson:** Same content, same offer, same host, same price - ONLY the hook changed. Ego vs. empathy.

### The Banana Hook

**Visual:** Hormozi catching a banana mid-air
**Verbal:** "You might be wondering why I just caught a banana..."
**Result:** Top performing ad, now in the 70% "proven winners" bucket

**Lesson:** Moonshots (10%) can become core winners if they work. Visual pattern interrupts are massively underutilized.

### Kyle - Last Place Leaderboard

**Hook:** "$4,664 per month... that's what Kyle, the LAST person on our leaderboard, was able to build"
**Result:** Massive success because removes "I'm not good enough" objection

**Lesson:** Use the worst case as proof, not the best. Shows anyone can do it.
