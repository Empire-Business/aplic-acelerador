# $100M Pricing Playbook - Alex Hormozi

## Framework Overview

The Pricing Playbook reveals that **pricing is the most powerful profit lever** in any business. Most entrepreneurs dramatically undercharge because they copy competitors who are also broke. This playbook teaches systematic approaches to pricing that can instantly multiply profits without losing customers.

**Core Insight:** The easiest way to make more money is to charge more for the same thing. A $100 price increase on 1,000 customers = $100,000 pure profit. This is why pricing deserves obsessive focus.

---

## Key Concepts and Principles

### The Three Pricing Models

**1. Cost Plus Pricing** (What it costs + markup)
- Simple but leaves money on table
- Doesn't consider value to customer
- Common but suboptimal

**2. Competitor-Based Pricing** (What others charge)
- Problem: Everyone else might be broke too
- Leads to race to the bottom
- Destroys industry margins

**3. Value-Based Pricing** (What it's worth to customer)
- Optimal approach
- Charges for transformation/result
- Captures maximum profit

**Hormozi's Rule:** "There's no strategic advantage to being the second lowest price. But there IS for being the highest." - Dan Kennedy

### The Value Equation

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time × Effort)
```

**To increase price, you must:**
1. Increase Dream Outcome (what they want)
2. Increase Perceived Likelihood (certainty it works)
3. Decrease Time (speed to result)
4. Decrease Effort (work they must do)

**Goal:** Make denominator (Time × Effort) approach zero

### The Pricing Psychology Framework

**Vicious Price Cycle (Lowering Prices):**
- Lower prices → Lower perceived value → Worse results → More demanding customers → Less service → Lower profits → Death spiral

**Virtuous Price Cycle (Raising Prices):**
- Higher prices → Higher perceived value → Better results → Better customers → More service → Higher profits → Reinvestment → Growth

## Step-by-Step Pricing Process

### Phase 1: Price Testing (Week 1)

**Step 1: Establish Baseline**
- Document current conversion rate at current price
- Track churn rate at current price
- Measure LTV at current price
- Know your numbers before changing anything

**Step 2: Test with New Customers First**
Never test price increases on existing customers initially. Always test on new, cold traffic first.

**Step 3: Test Multiple Price Points**
Run simultaneous tests:
- Current price (control)
- 2× current price
- 5× current price
- 10× current price

**Expected Results:**
- Conversion rate will drop
- But profit per customer increases
- Usually 2-5× price = optimal profit
- Sometimes 10× works better

**Example from Playbook:**
| Price | Conversion | Sales | LTV | Total Return | Change |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $10 | 5% | 5 | $100 | $500 | - |
| $20 | 4% | 4 | $200 | $800 | +60% |
| $100 | 2% | 2 | $300 | $600 | +20% |

**Winner:** $20 price point, despite 20% fewer sales, generates 60% more total revenue.

### Phase 2: Implementation (Week 2-4)

**Step 1: Segment Customers**
- NEW customers: Move to new price immediately
- EXISTING customers: Gradual rollout with communication

**Step 2: The RAISE Letter Framework**
Use this 5-part structure to communicate price increases:

**R - Remind them of value delivered**
Document everything you've done for them:
- "Over the past 12 months, we've added:"
- "Feature A helped you achieve Y"
- "You've used X successfully"

**A - Address price directly**
Don't bury it. Be clear:
- "For us to continue investing in [PRODUCT], we need to increase prices"

**I - Invest in their future**
Show how higher price benefits THEM:
- "With these resources, we'll:"
- "Hire more support staff"
- "Improve speed/performance"
- "Add features you've requested"

**S - Soften with loyalty reward**
Give existing customers special treatment:
- "As a loyal customer, you keep current price for 3-6 months"
- "Then we'll gradually transition you"

**E - Explain away concerns**
Add safety valve:
- "PS: If this materially impacts your ability to pay, let me know and we'll work something out"

### Phase 3: Optimization (Ongoing)

**Weekly:**
- Monitor churn rates
- Track conversion at new price
- Collect feedback on price perception

**Monthly:**
- Test small price increases (5-10%)
- Measure impact on metrics
- Adjust based on data

**Quarterly:**
- Consider larger price adjustments
- Reassess market positioning
- Plan next increases

## The 28 Pricing Plays

### Quick Wins (Implement Today)

**1. 28-Day Billing Cycles**
- Instead of monthly (30 days), bill every 28 days
- Get 13 payments per year instead of 12
- 8.3% revenue increase, customers barely notice

**2. Processing Fees**
- Charge 3% for credit card processing
- Or offer 3% discount for ACH/wire transfer
- Most won't switch, pure margin increase

**3. Sales Tax Handling**
- List price + tax separately
- Makes price feel lower (anchoring)
- "It's $99 + tax" vs "It's $107.97"

**4. Annual Price Increases**
- Build in automatic annual increases
- 5-10% per year compound massively
- Warren Buffet's Sees Candies: 10-17% annual price increases for 50 years

**5. Annual Billing Discounts**
- Month-to-month: $100/month
- Annual: $997/year (2 months free)
- Secures cash flow upfront

**6. Round Up Strategy**
- $97 instead of $95
- $997 instead of $950
- $9,997 instead of $9,500
- Psychological difference, real margin increase

**7. Renewal Fees**
- Charge for renewal each period
- Even small fees add up
- Tests true commitment

**8. Automatic Continuity**
- Default to auto-renew
- Require active cancellation
- Reduces churn dramatically

**9. Ultra High-Ticket Anchor**
- Show $10K option before $1K option
- Makes $1K feel reasonable
- Always have highest price visible

**10. Warranty Upsells**
- Extended warranty/service contracts
- High margin add-on
- Peace of mind for customers

**11. Tiered Value Ladders**

**18. Usage-Based Pricing**

## Hormozi's Pricing Rules

### The Commandments

**1. Never Grandfather Existing Customers**
- Value depends on price
- Locking prices limits future options
- Exceptions create complexity
- Better to communicate and raise together

**2. Never Sell "One-Time Price" for Lifetime Access**
- Forever is longer than you think
- You'll run out of the money they paid
- Lifetime deals become liabilities
- Many who offered lifetime now regret it

**3. Raise Prices At Least Once Per Year**
- Inflation happens
- Your value increases
- Costs rise
- Regular increases normalize it

**4. Test Price Raises on New Customers First**
- Get data before committing
- See market reaction
- Build confidence
- Then roll to existing base

**5. If Raising >50%, Meet Personally**
- Big jumps require human touch
- Video calls preferred
- Explain value directly
- Handle objections live

**6. Pair Price Raises with Launches**
- Best time: same quarter as new product
- Frame as "early adopter pricing ending"
- Momentum carries the increase
- Natural time for change

**7. Do The Math First**
- Know what % you can lose and still profit
- Model scenarios
- Make data-driven decision
- Remove emotion

## The Price Raise Letter Template

### Complete Template

```
Dear [NAME],

Over the past [TIME PERIOD], we've added tremendous value to [PRODUCT/SERVICE]:
• We've made you [RESULT A]
• Feature B helped you [BENEFIT]
• Feature C is now available that you use daily

For us to continue investing in making [PRODUCT] even better for you,
we need to adjust our pricing.

With these additional resources, we're committed to:
• [INVESTMENT 1 - How it helps you]
• [INVESTMENT 2 - How it helps you]
• [INVESTMENT 3 - How it helps you]

Because you've been such a loyal customer for [TIME], we have a special offer:
While new customers pay [NEW PRICE] immediately, we're extending your current
pricing for [3-6 MONTHS]. After that, we'll transition you gradually.

[Optional: If business impact would be severe]
PS - If this price change would materially impact your business/livelihood,
please let me know directly and we'll work something out.

Thank you for being part of our journey,
[YOUR NAME]
```

```
Hi [NAME],

Quick update: We're investing heavily in [IMPROVEMENTS] and
adjusting pricing to support these enhancements.

As a loyal customer, your price stays the same for [TIMEFRAME],
then we'll gradually transition you.

Questions? Just reply to this email.

Thanks,
[YOUR NAME]
```

## Real Examples from Hormozi

### Gym Price Increase (Trey's Story)

**Situation:**
- Gym owner losing $2K/month
- Prices too low to sustain
- Customers taking advantage

**Action:**
- Tripled monthly rates ($49 → $147)

**Result:**
- Some customers left (expected)
- Revenue: -$2K → +$4K profit

**Result:**

## Pricing Psychology Tactics

### The Anchoring Effect

**How it works:**
- First number seen sets expectation
- High anchor makes everything seem reasonable
- Always show highest price first

**Example:**
- Show $10,000 package first
- Then $997 looks cheap
- Even though $997 is still expensive

### The Rule of 100

**Under $100:** Percentage discounts seem bigger
- "20% off $50 = $10" (sounds good)
- "$10 off $50" (sounds small)

**Over $100:** Absolute discounts seem bigger
- "20% off $500 = $100" (hard to calculate)
- "$100 off $500" (sounds huge)

### Charm Pricing

- $97 vs $100
- $997 vs $1,000
- $9,997 vs $10,000
- Left-digit effect creates perception of much lower price

### Prestige Pricing

- Round numbers signal luxury
- $1,000 vs $997 for premium
- $10,000 vs $9,997 for VIP
- Avoids "cheap" feeling

### Price Partitioning

- Instead of "$500 all-in"
- Show "$400 + $100 premium service"
- Makes components clear
- Feels more justified

## Testing Protocol

### How to Test Price Properly

**1. Control Variables**
- Keep everything constant except price
- Same ad, same funnel, same offer
- Only change the price

**2. Statistical Significance**
- Minimum 1,000 visitors per price point
- More is better
- Don't conclude too early

**3. Measure Multiple Metrics**
- Conversion rate
- Revenue per visitor
- Refund rate
- Churn rate
- LTV

**4. Test Simultaneously**
- Run all prices at same time
- A/B test properly
- Avoid time-based bias

**5. Document Everything**
- Exact prices tested
- Results for each
- Context and notes
- Learnings for future

### Reading the Results

**If conversion stays same:** Raise price more
**If conversion drops slightly but revenue up:** Keep testing higher
**If revenue drops:** You went too high, test midpoint
**If refund/churn spike:** Value doesn't justify price

## Troubleshooting Common Situations

**"Customers are complaining about price"**
- Are they complaining or just commenting?
- Are they still buying?
- If still buying, complaints don't matter
- If not buying, value communication is issue

**"Competitors are cheaper"**
- Good, let them race to bottom
- You focus on value, not price
- Better customers don't want cheapest
- Premium positioning pays long-term

**"We're losing sales to price objections"**
- Is it truly price or value?
- Test better value communication
- Add more bonuses
- Improve offer, don't just drop price

**"Market won't bear higher prices"**
- Test higher anyway, let data decide
- Maybe your market is wrong
- Or maybe you're wrong
- Don't assume without testing

## The Math Behind Pricing

### Breakeven Analysis

**How many sales can you lose and still make more money?**

```
Current: 100 sales × $100 = $10,000
New Price: 50 sales × $250 = $12,500
Result: Lose 50% of customers, make 25% more money
```

### The 10× The 10% Rule

**If 10% of customers pay 10× the price, you double revenue.**

```
Normal: 100 × $100 = $10,000
With 10% at 10×:
90 × $100 = $9,000
10 × $1,000 = $10,000
Total: $19,000 (almost 2×)
```

### LTV Impact

```
Price $50, Churn 10%:
LTV = $50 ÷ 0.10 = $500

Price $100, Churn 12%:
LTV = $100 ÷ 0.12 = $833

66% higher LTV even with worse churn
```
