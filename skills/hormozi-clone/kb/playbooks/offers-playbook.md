# $100M Offers Playbook - Alex Hormozi

## Framework Overview

**Your offer is the single most important factor in your business success.** A great offer sells with mediocre copy. A mediocre offer won't sell with world-class copy. This playbook teaches the systematic process for creating "Grand Slam" offers that practically sell themselves.

**Core Philosophy:** "Make offers so good people feel stupid saying no." This isn't about hype - it's about value stacking, risk reversal, and psychological positioning that makes your offer the obvious choice.

---

## Key Concepts and Principles

### The Value Equation

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time × Effort)
```

**To increase value (and what you can charge):**

**Increase Numerator:**
1. **Dream Outcome** - What they ultimately want
2. **Perceived Likelihood** - How certain they are it'll work for them

**Decrease Denominator:**
3. **Time** - How fast they get results
4. **Effort** - How much work they must do

**Goal:** Make denominator approach zero (instant results with zero effort)

### The Grand Slam Offer Framework

**A Grand Slam Offer has:**
1. Clearly defined outcome (specific and measurable)
2. Guaranteed timeline (or it's free)
3. Unique mechanism (proprietary system)
4. Accountability (you won't let them fail)
5. Community support (others doing it together)
6. Risk reversal (insane guarantee)

### Value Stacking Principle

**Build value like this:**
```
Core Component ....................... $2,000
Bonus #1: Speed booster ............... $997
Bonus #2: Remove objections ........... $500
Bonus #3: Insurance policy ............. $2,000
Bonus #4: Expert access ............... $1,500
Total Value: ............................ $6,997
Your Price: ............................. $997
You Save: .............................. $6,000
```

**Key:** Individual components must have real, justifiable value (not made up)

## Step-by-Step Offer Creation

### Phase 1: Research & Diagnosis (Week 1)

**Step 1: Market Research**
- Interview 20+ prospects
- Find the #1 pain point
- Identify current solutions (and why they fail)
- Collect exact language customers use
- Document what they've already tried

**Step 2: Competitive Analysis**
- What do competitors offer?
- What are their weaknesses?
- What's missing from their solutions?
- Where do customers feel ripped off?

**Step 3: Your Unique Mechanism**
- Create proprietary name for your system
- Example: "The 6-Week Summer Shred" vs "Weight Loss Program"
- Make it sound unique even if it's common sense
- Trademark if possible

### Phase 2: Build the Core (Week 2)

**Step 1: Define the Outcome**
- Specific: "Lose 15lbs" not "get fit"
- Measurable: Numbers and metrics
- Time-bound: "In 6 weeks" not "eventually"
- Guaranteed: "Or your money back"

**Step 2: Determine the Timeline**
- What's the fastest realistic timeframe?
- Cut it in half - now that's your goal
- Build buffer: Overdeliver on speed
- Example: Promise 30 days, deliver in 21

**Step 3: Remove the Effort**
- What can you do FOR them?
- Done-for-you templates
- Pre-made materials
- Automated systems
- Concierge support

### Phase 3: Stack the Value (Week 3)

**Core Offer Design**
```
What: Clearly defined deliverable
Result: Specific outcome
Timeline: Speed guarantee
Format: How it's delivered
Support: What help they get
```

**Bonus Creation (The 5 Types)**

**1. Speed Bonus**
- Accelerates results
- Example: "Jump-start guide to get results in first 7 days"
- Value: $500-1,000

**2. Convenience Bonus**
- Makes implementation easier
- Example: "Pre-made templates so you don't start from scratch"
- Value: $500-1,000

**3. Exclusivity Bonus**
- Not available separately
- Example: "Private community access"
- Value: $500-2,000

**4. Remove Objection Bonus**
- Addresses specific concerns
- Example: "Software normally $97/mo, included free"
- Value: $1,000-2,000

**5. Insurance Policy Bonus**
- Risk reversal
- Example: "If not satisfied after 90 days, we work for free until you are"
- Value: $2,000+

### Phase 4: Pricing & Guarantee (Week 4)

**Pricing Strategy**
```
1. Calculate value stack total ($7,000 example)
2. Set price at 10-20% of value ($997)
3. Or charge 10-100× your delivery cost
4. Always test higher prices
5. Anchor with more expensive option
```

**The Guarantee Hierarchy**
```
Level 1: 30-day money back (Weak)
Level 2: 60-day money back (Standard)
Level 3: Results guarantee or money back (Strong)
Level 4: Results guarantee + we pay you (Insane)
Level 5: "Better than free" guarantee (Nuclear)

Example: "If you don't lose 15lbs in 6 weeks,
we'll refund your money AND pay you $500 for your time"
```

**Structure:**

- "10 new clients in 30 days or we pay you $100"

## The 10x Value Rule

**Your offer should be worth 10× what you charge.**

**Examples:**
- Charge $1,000 → Deliver $10,000 value
- Charge $5,000 → Deliver $50,000 value
- Charge $50 → Deliver $500 value

**How to calculate:**
1. What's the outcome worth to them?
2. What does it cost them NOT to solve it?
3. What do alternatives charge?
4. What's the transformation worth?

**If you can't justify 10× value:** Your offer is too weak or your price is too high

## Risk Reversal Strategies

### The 5 Levels of Guarantees

**Level 1: Satisfaction Guarantee**
"30-day money back if not satisfied"
- Standard, expected
- Doesn't move needle much

**Level 2: Results Timeline**
"Get results in X days or refund"
- Better, shows confidence
- Still protects you somewhat

**Level 3: Specific Results Guarantee**
"Achieve [specific outcome] or refund"
- Strong, compelling
- Requires proof of effort

**Level 4: Results + Penalty**
"Get [result] or we refund + pay you extra"
- Very powerful
- Shows insane confidence

**Level 5: Better Than Free**
"Try it free, keep the bonuses even if you cancel"
- Removes ALL risk
- Creates reciprocity

### The "Double Your Money" Guarantee

**Ultimate risk reversal:**
```
"Not only will we refund your money if you don't get results,
we'll DOUBLE your money back if you do everything we say
and still don't achieve [specific outcome]"
```

**Use sparingly:** Only when you're 100% certain your system works

## Premium Offer Elements

### What Makes an Offer Premium?

**Scarcity Elements:**
- Limited spots (we can only serve X people)
- Limited time (offer ends on Friday)
- Limited access (doors close, may not reopen)

**Exclusivity Elements:**
- "Not for everyone"
- Application required
- Must qualify
- "By invitation only"

**Status Elements:**
- VIP designation
- Private community
- Direct access
- Priority support

**Urgency Elements:**
- Price increases after launch
- Bonuses expire
- Cart closes
- Real countdown

## Common Offer Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Vague Outcomes

**Bad:** "Get in better shape"
**Good:** "Lose 15lbs of fat in 6 weeks"

**Bad:** "Make more money"
**Good:** "Add $10K/month to your business in 90 days"

**Bad:** "Grow your audience"
**Good:** "Get 10K followers in 6 months"

### Mistake 2: No Timeline

**Bad:** "Learn how to invest"
**Good:** "Complete your first real estate deal in 90 days"

**Bad:** "Build a business"
**Good:** "Replace your 9-5 income in 6 months"

### Mistake 3: Too Much Effort Required

**Bad:** "Here's a course, go implement it yourself"
**Good:** "We'll implement it for you in 4 weeks"

**Bad:** "Templates you can customize"
**Good:** "Done-for-you templates already filled out"

### Mistake 4: No Support

**Bad:** "Here's the material, good luck"
**Good:** "Daily coaching calls + private community + direct access"

### Mistake 5: Weak Guarantee

**Bad:** "No refunds" (huge red flag)
**Good:** "Results in 60 days or full refund + you keep bonuses"

## Testing Your Offer

### How to Test Before Full Launch

**Step 1: The 10-Person Test**
- Present offer to 10 qualified prospects
- Goal: 3+ sales (30% conversion)
- If <30%: Offer needs work
- If >50%: Price might be too low

**Step 2: Collect Feedback**
- Ask everyone who bought WHY they bought
- Ask everyone who didn't buy WHY NOT
- Look for patterns
- Adjust accordingly

**Step 3: Iterate Quickly**
- Fix obvious issues
- Add missing elements
- Clarify confusing parts
- Test again

**Step 4: Scale What Works**
- Don't change winning formula
- Optimize traffic and funnel
- Improve delivery
- Raise prices over time

## Advanced Offer Strategies

### The Name Your Price Strategy

**For testing pricing:**
- Let customer choose what to pay
- Most pay more than you'd charge
- Collects data on perceived value
- Creates goodwill

**Best for:** Info products, digital goods, testing new markets

### The Pay What You Want (Minimum)

**Structure:**
- Set floor price ($X minimum)
- Customer can pay more if they want
- Most pay floor, some pay much more
- Creates transparency and trust

**Best for:** SaaS, memberships, ongoing services

### The Tiered Value Ladder

**Structure:**
- Basic: $97 (good)
- Standard: $297 (better)
- Premium: $997 (best)
- VIP: $2,997 (best + concierge)

**Key:** Each tier must have clear differentiation

### The Subscription + One-Time Combo

**Structure:**
- Monthly: $97/month (ongoing access)
- Annual: $997/year (2 months free)
- Lifetime: $2,997 once (pay once, own forever)

**Psychology:** Gives options, captures different segments

## The Mathematics of Offers

### The Value Perception Formula

```
Perceived Value = (Real Value × Presentation × Trust) ÷ Price
```

**To increase perceived value:**
1. Increase real value (more stuff)
2. Improve presentation (better copy/design)
3. Build trust (proof, guarantees)
4. Decrease price (or increase value more)

### The Conversion Math

**Goal: 10% conversion from warm traffic**

If you're at 3% conversion:
- Double perceived value
- Double guarantee strength
- Cut price in half
- Or improve all three slightly

**The 10× Rule:**
- 10× the value of alternatives
- Or 10× the outcome
- Or 10× the speed
- Or 10× the ease
