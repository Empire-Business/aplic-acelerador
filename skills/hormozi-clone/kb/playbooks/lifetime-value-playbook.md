# $100M Lifetime Value Playbook - Alex Hormozi

## Framework Overview

**Lifetime Value (LTV) is the metric that matters most.** Most businesses optimize for conversion rate or average order value, but LTV is the true measure of customer worth. This playbook reveals the "Crazy 8" framework - eight ways to systematically increase LTV and transform your economics.

**Core Principle:** Small increases in LTV compound massively. Improving LTV by 2-3× doesn't just increase revenue - it changes everything: what you can afford to spend on acquisition, how fast you can grow, even whether your business model works at all.

---

## Key Concepts and Principles

### The LTV Equation

**Two ways to calculate:**

**Method 1: Simple**
```
LTV = Average Monthly Revenue × Customer Lifespan (in months)

Example:
$100/month × 12 months = $1,200 LTV
```

**Method 2: Churn-Based (More Accurate)**
```
LTV = Monthly Revenue ÷ Monthly Churn Rate

Example:
$100/month ÷ 10% churn = $1,000 LTV
```

**Why LTV Matters:**
- Determines how much you can spend on acquisition (CAC)
- Higher LTV = higher CAC affordable = faster growth
- Increases profitability without cutting costs
- Compounds over time

### The LTV Multiplier Effect

**The Mathematics:**
```
Scenario A: $50 LTV, $25 CAC (2× return)
$25 profit per customer
Need 400 customers to make $10K

Scenario B: $150 LTV, $50 CAC (3× return)
$100 profit per customer
Need only 100 customers to make $10K
```

**Same ad spend, 4× the profit.** That's the power of LTV.

## The Crazy 8: Eight Ways to Increase LTV

### 1. Increase Prices

**The Math:**
```
Current: $97/month, 10% churn
LTV = $97 ÷ 0.10 = $970

After Price Increase: $147/month, 12% churn (slightly worse)
LTV = $147 ÷ 0.12 = $1,225
```

**Result:** 26% higher LTV even with higher churn

**Why works:**
- More revenue per customer
- Customers who pay more, stay longer (committed)
- Better customers (more serious)
- Higher perceived value

**Implementation:**
- Test price increases with new customers
- Use RAISE framework for existing customers (see Pricing Playbook)
- Add value alongside price increases
- Never lower prices

### 2. Decrease Costs/Delivery Expenses

**The Math:**
```
Current: $100 revenue, $40 costs = $60 gross profit
After: $100 revenue, $20 costs = $80 gross profit
33% increase in profit per customer
```

**Why works:**
- More margin to reinvest
- Higher effective LTV (profit LTV, not revenue LTV)
- Better service (invest savings in delivery)
- Competitive advantage

**Implementation:**
- Automate repetitive tasks
- Negotiate better supplier deals
- Use technology instead of people where appropriate
- Optimize fulfillment
- Reduce support costs with better onboarding

### 3. Increase Number of Purchases (Frequency)

**The Math:**
```
Current: 2 purchases/year × $50 = $100/year LTV
After: 4 purchases/year × $50 = $200/year LTV
```

**Why works:**
- More touchpoints = more revenue
- Deeper relationship
- Habits formed
- Harder to leave

**Implementation:**
- Subscription/continuity models
- Automated replenishment
- Reminder systems
- Seasonal campaigns
- Cross-sell sequences

### 4. Cross-Sell Related Products

**The Math:**
```
Current: $100 LTV from core product
After: Add $50 cross-sell to 40% of customers
New LTV = $100 + ($50 × 0.40) = $120
20% increase
```

**Why works:**
- LTV increases without new acquisition cost
- Higher satisfaction (complete solution)
- Competitive moat (one-stop shop)
- Creates bundles

**Implementation:**
- Map customer journey
- Identify complementary needs
- Create natural cross-sell points
- Package deals
- Automated recommendations

### 5. Sell More Quantity (Volume Discounts)

**The Math:**
```
Current: 1 unit × $100 = $100
After: 2 units × $80 = $160 (with discount)
60% more revenue, same customer
```

**Why works:**
- Customers feel they're getting deal
- You lock in future revenue
- Reduced transaction costs
- Inventory optimization

**Implementation:**
- Volume tier pricing
- "Buy 2, get 1 free"
- Annual plans (12 months for price of 10)
- Bulk ordering incentives

### 6. Sell Better Quality (Premium Versions)

**The Math:**
```
Current: $100 product, 20% buy premium
Premium: $150 upgrade, 20% × $50 = $10 extra/customer
New LTV = $110 (10% increase)
```

**Why works:**
- Segment your customers (willingness to pay)
- Higher margin on premium
- Better customers self-select
- Perceived quality difference

**Implementation:**
- Good/Better/Best tiering
- Gold/Silver/bronze packages
- VIP upgrades
- Add-on features

### 7. Downsell Fewer (Reduce Churn)

**The Math:**
```
Current: $100/month, 20% monthly churn
After: Add $50/month option for at-risk customers
Churn drops to 10%
Old LTV: $100 ÷ 0.20 = $500
New LTV: Weighted average = $650
30% increase
```

**Why works:**
- Saves customers who'd cancel entirely
- Maintains relationship
- Future upsell opportunity
- Better than losing them

**Implementation:**
- Identify customers at risk of leaving
- Offer lower-tier alternative
- Pause instead of cancel
- "Basic" version retention offer

### 8. Downsell Lower Quality (Budget Versions)

**The Math:**
```
Current: 100 customers × $100 = $10,000
Add: $50 option captures 20% who wouldn't buy
New: 80 × $100 + 20 × $50 = $9,000
BUT: Add 20 new customers at $50 = $1,000 more
Total: $11,000 (10% increase)
```

**Why works:**
- Captures price-sensitive segment
- Entry point for budget customers
- Upsell path later
- Market expansion

**Implementation:**
- Self-service version
- Digital vs. in-person
- Group vs. 1-on-1
- DIY vs. done-for-you

## The Retention-LTV Connection

### The Churn Multiplier

**Reducing churn has massive impact:**

```
10% monthly churn:
- Customer lifespan: 10 months
- LTV at $100/month: $1,000

5% monthly churn:
- Customer lifespan: 20 months
- LTV at $100/month: $2,000

3% monthly churn:
- Customer lifespan: 33 months
- LTV at $100/month: $3,300
```

**From 10% to 3% churn = 3.3× LTV increase**

**This is why retention is the most profitable lever.** See the Retention Playbook for complete systems.

## Real-World Examples

### Example 1: Gym Business

**Before:**
- $150/month membership
- 10% monthly churn
- LTV = $150 ÷ 0.10 = $1,500

**After applying Crazy 8:**
1. **Price increase:** $150 → $200
2. **Decrease costs:** Automate scheduling (save $20/month)
3. **Cross-sell:** Add $50 nutrition program (30% take rate)
4. **Premium:** $300 VIP coaching (10% upgrade)
5. **Downsell:** $100 basic option for at-risk (reduces churn)

**New Economics:**
- Base: $200/month
- Cross-sell: $15/month average ($50 × 30%)
- Premium: $20/month average ($200 × 10%)
- Total revenue: $235/month
- Churn: 7% (down from 10%)
- **New LTV: $235 ÷ 0.07 = $3,357**

**Result:** 124% increase in LTV ($1,500 → $3,357)

### Example 2: SaaS Business

**Before:**
- $97/month subscription
- 8% monthly churn
- LTV = $97 ÷ 0.08 = $1,212

**After applying Crazy 8:**
1. **Price increase:** $97 → $147
2. **Annual billing:** $1,297/year (2 months free)
3. **Enterprise tier:** $497/month (5% upgrade)
4. **Decrease costs:** Move to cloud hosting (save $8/user)
5. **Add-ons:** $50 premium support (15% take rate)

**New Economics:**
- Base: $147/month
- Add-ons: $7.50/month average
- Weighted premium uplift: $20/month
- Total revenue: $174.50/month
- Churn: 5% (annual customers stay longer)
- **New LTV: $3,489**

**Result:** 188% increase in LTV ($1,212 → $3,489)

## Advanced LTV Strategies

**Problem:** Low LTV customers cost the same to acquire as high LTV customers

**Solution:** Filter before acquisition

**Implementation:**
- Minimum qualification criteria
- Application process
- Pricing that filters out bargain hunters
- Targeting that focuses on high-LTV segments

**Result:** Higher average LTV from day 1

**Progressive value delivery:**

```
Lead Magnet ($0)
```

## Troubleshooting Low LTV

### Problem: LTV Under $500

**Diagnosis:** Likely one-time purchase model

**Solutions:**
- Add subscription/recurring revenue
- Develop backend offers
- Create continuity
- Build membership/community

### Problem: High Churn (>15%)

**Diagnosis:** Retention issue

**Solutions:**
- Fix onboarding (see Retention Playbook)
- Improve product/service
- Add community
- Better support
- Fire bad customers

### Problem: Low Purchase Frequency

**Diagnosis:** No reason to buy again

**Solutions:**
- Develop product line
- Create consumption cycle
- Add replenishment program
- Develop seasonal campaigns
- Bundle related products

### Problem: Low Average Order Value

**Diagnosis:** Underpriced or underpackaged

**Solutions:**
- Increase prices (test first)
- Create bundles
- Add upsells
- Premium tiers
- Volume discounts
