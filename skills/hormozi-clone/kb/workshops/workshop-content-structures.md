# Workshop Content Structures & Templates - Alex Hormozi Style

## Overview

This document provides the exact content structures, slide templates, and organizational frameworks Alex Hormozi uses to create workshop presentations that convert. Based on analysis of the $100M Money Models launch and related workshop materials.

---

## The Macro Structure: 12 Phases

### Phase 1: Cold Open & Hook (Slides 1-150)

**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Pace:** 10 slides/minute
**Objective:** Immediate attention + commitment to stay

**Slide Breakdown:**

**Slides 1-10: The Countdown**
- Solid black screen
- Large countdown timer
- Music building
- Text: "Starting in [TIME]"

**Slides 11-30: The Reveal**
- "Welcome to [EVENT NAME]"
- Host appears
- Big promise in text
- "What you'll learn" bullets

**Slides 31-60: The Authority Flash**
- Rapid-fire results
- "$250M portfolio"
- "463,167 registrations"
- "Sold for $46.2M"
- One stat per slide
- Fast pace (3-5 sec each)

**Slides 61-90: The Promise**
- "In the next [TIME], I'll show you..."
- Specific outcome bullet points
- "Without [objection 1]"
- "Without [objection 2]"
- "Even if [limitation]"

**Slides 91-120: The Live Element**
- Guinness judge on screen
- "We're breaking world records"
- Proof of external validation
- Screenshots of validation

**Slides 121-150: What's At Stake**
- "Here's why you need to stay..."
- Tease the surprise
- "Better than NFT"
- Preview what's coming

### Phase 2: Disclaimer & Frame (Slides 151-200)

**Duration:** 5 minutes
**Pace:** 10 slides/minute
**Objective:** Legal compliance + authentic positioning

**Slide Structure:**
- Disclaimer text on screen
- "Not a promise" language
- Risk acknowledgment
- "Results vary" statement
- Positioning slides
- "I'm no saint" reveal
- "I want to make money like you"

**Key Message Slides:**
```
"Before we begin, a quick note..."

[DISCLAIMER TEXT]

"I make free content every day because..."

"Results not typical. Your results will vary.

But I'll show you exactly what works."
```

### Phase 3: Origin Story (Slides 201-400)

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**Pace:** 8-10 slides/minute
**Objective:** Connection + credibility + relatability

**The Hero's Journey Structure:**

**Slides 201-240: The Bottom**
- "Where I started..."
- Photos of bad situation
- Sleeping on gym floor
- $100K in debt
- Specific numbers
- Emotional context

**Slides 241-280: The Struggle**
- Failed attempts
- Lost everything twice
- Partners quitting
- Rejection stories
- Near-death business moments
- Timeline visualization

**Slides 281-320: The Discovery**
- The key insight
- First small win
- 22 gym members
- First location
- Breakthrough moment
- "What if..." question

**Slides 321-360: The Growth**
- 6 locations in 3 years
- Gym Launch formation
- First $1M month
- Scale timeline
- Company screenshots
- Revenue graphs

**Slides 361-400: Current Reality**
- Portfolio companies
- $250M in revenue
- Acquisition.com stats
- Team photos
- Facility shots
- "How I got here"

### Phase 4: Problem Agitation (Slides 401-550)

**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Pace:** 8-10 slides/minute
**Objective:** Pain + urgency + problem awareness

**Slide Pattern:**

**Slides 401-450: The Statistics**
- "82% of businesses fail..."
- Source citation (US Bank)
- One stat per slide
- Large numbers
- Red/green color coding
- Visual graphs

**Slides 451-500: The Reality**
- "54% lose money or break even"
- "Median income: $48K/year"
- Cost breakdown
- What owners give up
- "80-hour weeks"
- Missed family moments
- Stress visualization

**Slides 501-550: The False Solutions**
- What everyone tries
- Why it doesn't work
- Method 1: [Label]
- Method 2: [Label]
- Method 3: [Label]
- "All have this in common..."
- The missing element

### Phase 5: The Framework (Slides 551-900)

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes
**Pace:** 6-8 slides/minute
**Objective:** Deep education + proprietary system

**Structure for Each Component:**

**Introduction Slides (551-600):**
- "The One Thing..."
- Framework name reveal
- Definition
- Visual diagram
- "Here's why it matters"

**Formula Slides (601-650):**
- The equation
- Break down each part
- What it means
- How to calculate
- Example calculation
- Target numbers

**Component 1: Attraction (651-720):**
- What it is
- Why it matters
- 5 mechanisms overview
- Each mechanism gets:
  - Name slide
  - Definition slide
  - Example slide
  - Visual slide
  - Common mistakes
  - How to implement

**Component 2: Upsell (721-780):**
- Same pattern as above
- 4 mechanisms
- Examples
- Visuals

**Component 3: Downsell (781-840):**
- Same pattern
- 3 mechanisms
- Payment plans
- Trials
- Features

**Component 4: Continuity (841-900):**
- Same pattern
- 3 mechanisms
- Recurring revenue
- Churn reduction
- LTV focus

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes

**Case 3: Prestige Labs (1021-1080)**

**Case 4: ALLEN (1081-1140)**
- Different model
- B2B example
- Service business

**Case 5: School (1141-1150)**
- SaaS example

- Rapid growth

**Duration:** 20 minutes

**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Pace:** 6-8 slides/minute

**Slide Stack:**

**Duration:** 30-40 minutes

8. Total stack value

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes

**Duration:** 2-3 hours
**Format:** Live consultation
**Objective:** Demonstrate expertise

## Production Templates

### The 10-Slide Transition Pattern

**Use between major sections:**

```
Slide 1: Section recap (3 bullets)
Slide 2: "Now here's the problem..."
Slide 3: Problem statement
Slide 4: "But there's a solution..."
Slide 5: Solution preview
Slide 6: "Let me show you..."
Slide 7: Section title
Slide 8: What we'll cover
Slide 9: First concept header
Slide 10: First concept
```

### The "Proof Sandwich" Pattern

**Surround every claim with proof:**

```
Slide 1: Make claim
Slide 2: "Here's proof..."
Slide 3-7: Proof slides (screenshots, graphs, testimonials)
Slide 8: "The point is..."
Slide 9: Reinforce claim
Slide 10: Next concept
```

### The "Interactive" Pattern

**Every 15-20 minutes, force engagement:**

```
Slide 1: Question for audience
Slide 2: "Type [X] in the chat"
Slide 3: Show responses
Slide 4: Comment on responses
Slide 5: "Here's why this matters..."
Slide 6: Teach concept
Slide 7: Continue
```

## Timing Guidelines

### Phase Duration Targets

- **Hook:** 10-15 minutes (fast pace)
- **Story:** 15-20 minutes (medium pace)
- **Problem:** 10-15 minutes (slow, build tension)
- **Framework:** 45-60 minutes (teaching pace)
- **Cases:** 30-45 minutes (storytelling pace)
- **Offer:** 20-30 minutes (build to reveal)
- **Close:** 10-15 minutes (urgent pace)

### Slide Timing Rules

- **Text slides:** 5-10 seconds each
- **Data slides:** 10-15 seconds each
- **Story slides:** 15-20 seconds each
- **Concept slides:** 30-60 seconds each
- **Transition slides:** 3-5 seconds each

### Pacing Variation

**Every 20 minutes, change pace:**
- Fast section → Slow section
- Teaching → Story
- Data → Emotion
- You → Audience interaction

## Visual Design Principles

### Color Psychology

- **Black/White:** Authority, serious
- **Red:** Urgency, problems, important
- **Green:** Growth, money, success
- **Blue:** Trust, data, professional
- **Yellow/Gold:** Value, premium
- **Orange:** Action, energy

### Font Hierarchy

- **Headline:** 60-72pt
- **Subheadline:** 36-48pt
- **Body:** 24-32pt
- **Fine print:** 16-20pt

### Layout Rules

1. **One concept per slide**
2. **Max 7 words per slide**
3. **Center important elements**
4. **Use white space**
5. **High contrast required**
6. **No watermarks on photos**
7. **Readable at 1080p**
