## WHAT IS A MONEY MODEL?

### Definition
**A money model is a sequence of offers.** It's not just what you sell, but:
- **WHAT** you offer
- **WHEN** you offer it
- **HOW** you present it
- **HOW** you let them pay

### The Rental Car Example (That Changed Everything)

**December 2019 - A $19 rental became $100**

**The Sequence:**
1. Car rental: $19/day
2. Upgrade: "Want a roomier pickup?"
3. Late return: "Return anytime without late fees?"
4. Premium insurance: "Cover any damage?"
5. Downsell: "Okay, minimum insurance then"
6. Prepaid gas: "Return empty, we fill it for $3.75/gallon"

**Result:** $19/day became $100/day (5x increase)

**The Insight:** They knew every problem I'd have BEFORE I had it, and offered to solve each one.

### Why Money Models Matter

**Traditional Business (The Trap):**
- Spend $100 to get customer
- Make $500 profit over 2 YEARS
- Run out of cash in 30 days
- Go out of business

**Money Model Business:**
- Spend $100 to get customer
- Make $500 in first 30 DAYS
- Reinvest in 5 more customers
- Scale infinitely

### The Four Types of Offers
1. **Attraction Offers** - Get strangers to become customers
2. **Upsell Offers** - Get customers to spend more
3. **Downsell Offers** - Turn "no" into "yes"
4. **Continuity Offers** - Get recurring revenue

**All four work together to create a $100M Money Model.**

## STAGE 1: ATTRACTION OFFERS (GET MONEY)

### The Five Types of Attraction Offers

#### 1. WIN YOUR MONEY BACK

**Concept:** Customer pays upfront, gets money back if they hit a goal

**Example - Gym Challenge:**
- Pay: $600 for 6-week program
- Goal: Attend 20 sessions + hit targets
- Reward: Get $600 back as cash OR credit

**Why It's Genius:**
- ~10% actually achieve goal and get refund
- 90% pay full price
- Gerou $1 BILLION in sales for Hormozi

**The Psychology:**
- People overestimate their discipline
- Goals feel achievable when signing up
- Credit is more valuable than cash (locks them in)

**How to Implement:**
1. Set achievable but challenging goal
2. Make requirements clear and trackable
3. Offer refund as cash OR store credit
4. Make goal require effort (screens for committed)

---

#### 2. SWEEPSTAKES (GIVEAWAYS)

**Concept:** Give away big prize, offer everyone else a discount

**Example - Storage Facility:**
- Prize: Free storage for 1 year
- Everyone else: "First month free, then 50% off for 6 months"

**The Sequence:**
1. Advertise huge prize
2. Collect contact info
3. Pick winner
4. Offer everyone else a "consolation" deal

**Why It Works:**
- Free prize attracts attention
- Discount to losers feels like a win
- You get leads and sales

---

#### 3. HOOK OFFER (DECOY)

**Concept:** Advertise free/cheap offer, upsell premium version

**Example - Gym Launch:**
- Advertise: Free do-it-yourself gym system
- Reality: Upsell $16,000 done-for-you licensing

**The Psychology:**
- Free DIY attracts massive leads
- Most realize they want help
- Premium offer feels reasonable vs. DIY

**How to Implement:**
1. Create valuable free offer
2. Make it comprehensive but time-consuming
3. Offer premium "done-for-you" version
4. Price premium at 10-100x of free version

---

#### 4. BUY X, GET Y FREE

**Concept:** Buy something, get something else free

**Example - Dog Food:**
- Buy 6 months of food
- Get 2 months free
- Total: 8 months for price of 6

**Variations:**
- Buy main product, get accessories free
- Buy now, get future service free
- Buy subscription, get one-time item free

**Why It Works:**
- Perceived value of "free"
- Locks in longer commitment
- Increases average order value

---

#### 5. PAY LESS NOW, PAY MORE LATER

**Concept:** Discount for upfront payment OR pay more over time

**Example - Newsletter:**
- Pay $297 now, keep $297/month rate forever
- OR pay $399/month starting after 30 days

**The Psychology:**
- People love discounts
- Locks in lower rate
- Creates commitment

**Variations:**
- Lifetime discount for prepay
- Charter pricing for early adopters
- Founding member rates

---

## STAGE 2: UPSELL OFFERS (GET MORE MONEY)

### The Four Types of Upsell Offers

#### 1. CLASSIC UPSELL

**Concept:** Solve the next problem immediately

**The Rental Car Pattern:**
- Got the car? Now you need insurance
- Got insurance? Now you need gas
- Got gas? Now you need late return

**Formula: "You can't have X without Y"**

**Examples:**
- Bike → Helmet ("Can't have bike without helmet")
- Course → Nutrition course ("Can't out-exercise bad diet")
- Software → Setup service ("Can't use software without setup")

**Timing:** IMMEDIATELY after first purchase

**Key Principle:** Every solution creates a new problem. Solve it immediately.

---

#### 2. MENU UPSELL

**Concept:** Tell them what they don't need, then what they do, then AB choice

**The Four-Step Process:**

**Step 1: UNS**
"You don't need X" (builds trust)

**Step 2: PRESCRIBE**
"But you definitely need Y" (expert positioning)

**Step 3: AB CHOICE**
"Do you want chocolate or vanilla?" (not IF, but WHICH)

**Step 4: CARD ON FILE**
"Wanna just use the card on file?" (ease of payment)

**Example - Supplement Sales:**
"Based on your goals, you don't need the weight gainer (cross out).
But you DO need the protein powder and pre-workout.
Do you want chocolate or vanilla?
Great, let's just use the card we have on file."

**Why It's Brilliant:**
- Builds trust (unselling)
- Positions expertise (prescribing)
- Removes "no" as option (AB)
- Makes payment frictionless (card on file)

---

#### 3. ANCHOR UPSELL

**Concept:** Offer expensive thing first, then reasonable alternative

**The Suit Story:**
- Alex wants suit, budgeted $500
- Salesperson shows $16,000 suit first
- Alex gasps
- Salesperson shows $2,200 suit
- Alex buys it (feels like a deal!)

**The Formula:**
1. Present premium (5-10x price)
2. Get the gasp
3. Come to rescue
4. Present main offer
5. Ask for payment

**Why It Works:**
- $2,200 feels cheap vs. $16,000
- Some people still buy premium
- Higher anchor = higher perceived value

**Key Insight:**
"The only thing worse than making $1,000 offer to person with $100 budget
is making $100 offer to person with $1,000 budget"

Always have premium option. Some will buy it.

---

#### 4. ROLLOVER UPSELL

**Concept:** Credit previous purchase toward next offer

**The Aha Moment:**
Hormozi's friend Justin made money by rolling over "win your money back" credits into year-long memberships.

**Example:**
- Customer won $600 back from challenge
- Instead of cash, apply to $1,200 yearly membership
- Customer pays $600 more
- Gets $1,200 value

**Variations:**
- Re-engage old customers with credit
- Rescue upset customers (credit past purchase to new offer)
- Steal competitor's upset customers (credit what they paid elsewhere)
- Spread first purchase over longer term

**The Gift Card Play:**
- Sell $200 gift cards for $20
- Limit 2 per customer
- Require them to gift to others
- When recipients come, apply $200 to $1,000+ purchase

**Result:** People pay YOU to refer customers

## STAGE 3: DOWNSELL OFFERS (TURN NO TO YES)

### The Three Types of Downsell Offers

#### 1. PAYMENT PLAN DOWSELL

**Concept:** Same product, spread payments over time

**The Seven-Step Process:**

1. **Reward prepayment** (not punish payments)
   - Say: "It's $15, or $10 if prepaid"
   - Not: "It's $10, or $15 with payments"

2. **Offer third-party financing**
   - "Want to use our financing partner?"

3. **Offer half now, half later**
   - "When do you get paid?"

4. **Check desire** (1-10 scale)
   - If 8+, continue with payment plans
   - If 7 or below, change offer

5. **Split into 3 payments**
   - "One third now, one third next paycheck, one third after"

6. **Evenly spread payments**
   - "16 equal weekly payments"

7. **Free trial** (last resort)

**Key Math:**
- Monthly billing: 10.7% churn/month
- Quarterly billing: 5% churn/month
- Annual billing: 2% churn/month

**Conclusion:** Always push for longer payment terms

---

#### 2. TRIAL WITH PENALTY

**Concept:** Free trial IF they meet your terms, otherwise they pay

**Not "Free Trial" - It's "Trial With Penalty"**

**Example - HR Software:**
- Trial: Free software
- Condition: Must complete onboarding training
- Penalty: If skip training, pay $500 fee
- Reality: 3 training calls are upsell opportunities

**Why It Works:**
- Only committed people take it
- Requirements create ideal customers
- Trial becomes filtering mechanism

**How to Implement:**
1. Identify what makes ideal customer
2. Make those the trial terms
3. Get credit card upfront
4. Explain fees AFTER getting card
5. Make check-ins required (upsell opportunities)

**The 5-Step Process:**
1. Offer trial last (after they say no to main offer)
2. Always get a card upfront
3. Sell staying long-term
4. Explain fees AFTER getting card
5. Make check-ins required

---

#### 3. FEATURE DOWSELL

**Concept:** Lower price by removing features (not discounting)

**The Key Rule:**
**NEVER drop your price just to get someone to buy.**
**Always remove something of value to justify lower price.**

**Example - Removing Guarantee:**
"Full price includes money-back guarantee.
If you don't want the guarantee, I can knock $400 off.
Which would you prefer - with guarantee or without?"

**The Psychology:**
- Removing guarantee shows its value
- People suddenly want the guarantee
- Many choose the higher-priced option

**Types of Feature Downsells:**

**A. Quantity Downsells**
- "Instead of 3-month supply, start with 1?"
- "Instead of 4 sessions/month, do 2?"

**B. Quality Downsells**
- "Instead of leather, do vinyl?"
- "Instead of 5-min response, overnight?"

**C. Feature Removal**
- "Drop priority support, save $200?"
- "Remove guarantee, save $400?"

**D. Done-For-You to DIY**
- "Instead of us doing it, here's the DIY version?"

**The Formula:**
Take something away → Lower price → Ask "How about now?"

## STAGE 4: CONTINUITY OFFERS (GET MAXIMUM MONEY)

### Why Continuity Matters

**The Math:**
- 10 customers × $1,000 one-time = $10,000 total
- 40 customers × $50/month × 20 months = $40,000 total

**Continuity = More customers + More money per customer**

**But There's a Problem:**
Continuity attraction offers leave you cash-strapped today.

**The Solution:**
Use continuity as the LAST offer, after you've made profit from:
1. Attraction offer
2. Upsell offers
3. Downsell offers

---

### The Three Types of Continuity Offers

#### 1. CONTINUITY BONUS

**Concept:** Give awesome bonus if they join continuity

**Example - Gym:**
- Bonus: Free 6-week challenge (normally $600)
- Offer: Become member, get it free
- Result: Tripled membership sales

**How to Structure:**
- Make bonus worth more than first payment
- Make bonus related to core offer
- Make bonus something you already have/deliver

**Types of Bonuses:**
- Past products (all previous newsletters, $15,880 value)
- Onboarding/setup (something you'd do anyway)
- Physical product (digital membership gets physical gift)
- Titles/Status (gold member, VIP status)

**Key Insight:**
"Focus on the bonus, not the membership.
Advertise what you give away, not what you sell."

---

#### 2. CONTINUITY DISCOUNT

**Concept:** Lower price for continuity commitment

**Pricing Math:**
- To get 50% to choose continuity: Make one-time 1.33x more
- To get 70% to choose continuity: Make one-time 2x more
- To get 90% to choose continuity: Make one-time 2.66x more

**Example:**
- One-time purchase: $600
- Continuity: $200/month
- At 1.33x ratio, 50% choose continuity
- At 2x ratio, 70% choose continuity

**How to Offer:**
- Free time upfront (30 days free, then $X/month)
- Lifetime rate lock (join now, keep this rate forever)
- Bulk prepay discounts (buy 5 months, get 1 free)

---

#### 3. WAIVED FEE

**Concept:** Startup fee for month-to-month, waived if commit to term

**Example - Consulting:**
- Month-to-month: $1,000/month + $5,000 fee
- Annual commitment: $1,000/month, $5,000 fee waived
- Cancel early? Pay the $5,000 fee

**Why It's Brilliant:**
- Large fee motivates commitment
- Waived fee motivates signing up
- If they quit early, you still get paid

**How to Implement:**
1. Make fee 3-5x monthly rate
2. Offer to waive if commit to 12+ months
3. Require payment if cancel early
4. Drop fee after commitment fulfilled

**Key Principle:**
"Customers will stay longer if leaving costs more than staying"

## PRICING STRATEGY & MATH

### The 30-Day Payback Rule

**Golden Rule:**
```
Make enough profit in 30 days to cover CAC + delivery costs
```

**Why:**
- Can use credit cards as working capital
- Self-funding growth
- No external capital needed
- Forces pricing discipline

**How to Calculate:**
- Ad spend: $100
- Product price: $497
- COGs: $100
- Delivery cost: $50
- Net profit: $247
- Payback: $100 ÷ $247/month = 0.4 months (12 days)
- ✅ PASS - Under 30 days

---

### Continuity vs One-Time Pricing

**The Data from 1,000s of Gyms:**

**If 50% choose continuity:**
- Price one-time 1.33x higher
- Example: $399 one-time vs. $266/month

**If 70% choose continuity:**
- Price one-time 2x higher
- Example: $600 one-time vs. $399/month

**If 90% choose continuity:**
- Price one-time 2.66x higher
- Example: $799 one-time vs. $199/month

**Key Insight:** The larger the price difference, the more people choose continuity.

---

### Payment Plan Churn Data

**Billing Frequency Impact on Churn:**
- Monthly (12x/year): 10.7% monthly cancellation
- Quarterly (4x/year): 5% monthly cancellation
- Annual (1x/year): 2% monthly cancellation

**Conclusion:** Always offer annual or longer terms when possible.

---

### LTV (Lifetime Value) Formula

```
LTV = (Average Purchase Value) × (Purchase Frequency) × (Customer Lifespan)
```

**Example:**
- Average purchase: $97
- Purchases per year: 3
- Customer lifespan: 18 months

LTV = $97 × 3 × 1.5 = **$436**

**Why LTV Matters:**
- With $100 LTV: Can only spend $30 to acquire customer
- With $5,000 LTV: Can spend $500 to acquire customer
- Higher LTV = ability to scale

**How to Increase LTV:**
1. More frequent purchases (continuity)
2. Higher purchase values (upsells)
3. Longer customer lifespan (retention)
4. More referrals (viral growth)

## IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP

### Important Principles

**1. Perfect One Offer At A Time**
- Don't implement full money model at once
- It will break your business
- Master one stage, then move to next
- Measure in quarters, not weeks

**2. Raise Prices In Stages**
- Start cheap to get feedback
- Raise until "no's" outweigh "yes's"
- Test, measure, adjust

**3. Simple Scales, Fancy Fails**
- Not 100 different products
- 100 ways to offer same product
- More ways to sell = more money

**4. Affiliate Products Fill Gaps**
- Don't need to create everything
- Sell other people's stuff for commission
- Expand offers without operational headache

## COMMON MISTAKES

### ❌ DON'T:
- Implement full money model at once (will break business)
- Discount without removing value (destroys perceived value)
- Sell only one thing (leaves 3-10x money on table)
- Skip upsells (massive profit killer)
- Ignore continuity (highest LTV builder)
- Price without considering 30-day payback
- Offer "free trials" without penalty (freebie seekers)
- Drop price to close sale (always remove value instead)

### ✅ DO:
- Perfect one offer at a time
- Start cheap, raise prices gradually
- Make more offers, not more products
- Upsell immediately (while card is out)
- Turn "no" into "yes" with downsells
- Add continuity for predictable revenue
- Increase LTV before scaling traffic
- Track everything, optimize constantly
