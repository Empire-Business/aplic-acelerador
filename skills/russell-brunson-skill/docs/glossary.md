# Glossary - Russell Brunson Terminology

Complete glossary of terms, phrases, and concepts used by Russell Brunson and throughout this skill.

---

## A

### Application Funnel
A funnel designed to qualify leads before they can speak with you or buy a high-ticket offer. Typically includes an application form after the initial opt-in.

### Attractive Character
The persona/identity you create in your marketing. Russell's framework for building a personal brand that attracts followers and customers.

### Actionetics (now Follow-Up Funnels)
ClickFunnels' built-in email and automation platform. Russell's tool for managing follow-up sequences.

---

## B

### Brunson Box
A featured content box typically placed below videos on sales pages, containing bullet points that highlight key benefits and create curiosity.

### Bridge Page (See Also: Thank You Page)
A page that connects one step in a funnel to the next, often used to deliver a lead magnet before presenting an offer.

---

## C

### ClickFunnels
The software company Russell co-founded in 2014. A platform for building sales funnels without technical skills.

### Continuity
Recurring revenue programs (subscriptions, memberships) that provide stable cash flow.

### Core Offer
The main product in a value ladder, typically priced between $97-$497.

---

## D

### Dream 100
Russell's strategy for partnering with the top 100 influencers in your niche. Involves identifying, categorizing, cultivating, and collaborating with key influencers.

### Downsell
An offer presented after a customer declines an upsell, typically at a lower price point or for a related product.

### DotCom Secrets
Russell's first book, focused on the technical framework of building sales funnels and offers.

---

## E

### Epiphany Bridge
Russell's storytelling framework for selling new concepts through personal story. Includes backstory, desire, conflicts, epiphany, and transformation.

### Expert Secrets
Russell's second book, focused on positioning, building a following, and creating a movement.

---

## F

### Funnel
A series of steps/pages designed to guide a prospect toward a specific action (usually a purchase).

### Funnel Hacking
The practice of analyzing successful competitors' funnels to model what works.

### Funnel Hacker
Someone who studies, models, and implements successful funnels. The identity of Russell's community.

### Funnel Hacking Live
Russell's annual live event for funnel hackers.

### Funnel Stack
The complete sequence of pages and offers in a funnel.

---

## G

### Gold or Lead (Chumbo ou Ouro)
Russell's framework for creating hard deadlines. Turn "lead" (flexible) deadlines into "gold" (rigid) ones with public commitment and rewards.

---

## H

### High-Ticket
Premium offers typically priced $1,997 and above.

### Hook
The opening of a piece of content designed to grab attention and create curiosity.

---

## I

### Invisible Funnel
A funnel where the sales message is embedded within content, often using video sales letters without traditional pages.

---

## L

### Ladder of Value (See Also: Value Ladder)
The progression of offers from free to high-ticket that customers ascend.

### Lead
Someone who has expressed interest in your offer by providing contact information.

### Lead Magnet
A free offer given in exchange for someone's email address.

### Landing Page
A single page designed to capture leads (squeeze page) or make a sale (sales page).

---

## M

### Micro-Continuity
Fixed-term membership programs (e.g., 12 months) rather than ongoing subscriptions.

---

## O

### One Big Domino
The one thing that, if your prospect believes it, makes all other objections irrelevant.

### One Funnel Away Challenge
Russell's 30-day challenge that teaches participants to build and launch their first funnel.

### Opt-in
The action of someone providing their email address in exchange for something.

### Order Bump
An impulse buy added at checkout, typically $7-$97, that customers can add with a single click.

### Offer Wall
A thank you page that presents multiple offers for different products.

---

## P

### Perfect Webinar
Russell's framework for presenting via webinar, structured around three "secrets" that systematically overcome objections.

### Pressure Page
A page designed to create urgency before a cart closes or deadline expires.

---

## R

### Reverse Squeeze Page
A landing page that delivers value first (usually a video) before asking for the opt-in.

---

## S

### Sales Letter
Long-form written sales content, typically 10-20 pages.

### Sales Page
A page designed to sell a product or service.

### Squeeze Page
A landing page with a single purpose: capture email addresses.

### Stack, Don't Swap
Russell's philosophy of adding value rather than replacing elements when optimizing offers.

### Split Testing
Running simultaneous variations of a page or element to see which performs better.

---

## T

### Thank You Page
The page shown after someone opts in or purchases. Used to deliver value and make the next offer.

### Traffic Secrets
Russell's third book, focused on generating traffic and building an audience.

### Tripwire
A low-priced offer ($7-$47) designed to convert leads into customers and recover ad spend.

### Two Comma Club
ClickFunnels' award for users who generate $1 million+ through a single funnel.

---

## U

### Upsell
An additional offer presented immediately after a customer makes a purchase.

---

## V

### Value Ladder
The progression of increasingly valuable (and expensive) offers that customers move through.

### VSL (Video Sales Letter)
A sales presentation in video format, typically 15-60 minutes.

---

## W

### Webinar
A live or automated online presentation that educates and sells.

### Webinar Funnel
A funnel designed to register people for a webinar, deliver the presentation, and sell an offer.

---

## Russell-isms (Signature Phrases)

| Phrase | Meaning/Usage |
|--------|---------------|
| "Boom!" | Emphasis marker, used for impact |
| "You're just one funnel away" | Core philosophy of hope |
| "The math is the math" | Data-driven decision making |
| "What you measure grows" | Focus on metrics |
| "Marketing is the sex of business" | Marketing is the fun part |
| "Sell your way to success" | Action-oriented philosophy |
| "Stack, don't swap" | Add value, don't replace |
| "Funnel hacking" | Studying and modeling success |
| "The money's in the list" | Email list importance |
| "Opinion doesn't matter" | Data over opinions |
